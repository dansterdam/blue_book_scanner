BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING:
T-33 Pilot flying 40 miles East of Grand Junction, Colorado sighted a bright object straight ahead at 30 degrees above horizon. Observed 15 miles. Heading of aircraft at the time of sighting was 75°. Light intensity was similar to that of an arc light. Size of the object was estimated to be about 9 miles long on the surface of the canopy.

EVALUATION: CASE CATEGORY: Probably Meteor (Fireball)
CONCLUSION: The witness did not see the object at night. The description of the object is that of a classic fireball meteor. There is no available evidence which indicates this was anything other than a meteor.
