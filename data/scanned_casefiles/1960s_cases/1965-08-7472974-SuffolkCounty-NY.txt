Civilian & Military

A/C (ADVERTISING LIGHTS)

4. NUMBER OF OBJECTS
One

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
1. Large circular object with over 100 lights, very low, hovered
for 5-10 minutes, then flew away.
2. Both PA object moving N to S along 6 miles from end of runway
proceeding through military climb corridor, maneuvering in
area. Observed through BX and all that was observed was
string of bright lights in row. Maneuvered in Easterly and
Westerly heading for about 35 minutes.
3. Received phone calls from civilians observing object.
4. A/C diverted for identification observed another a/c with
light that gave off strobing effect.

Over One hour Total

6. TYPE OF OBSERVATION
Over One hour Total

STATEMENT FROM SSGT DOUGLAS LINEWOOD, A1769048, AIR TRAFFIC CONTROLLER, SUFFOLK BEACON PERFORMING NO UNUSUAL INCIDENTS WHILE OBSERVED ON THE NIGHT OF 11 AUGUST 1965.

AT APPROXIMATELY 01524 WHILE WORKING A G-97 CALL SIGN AG 506, I WAS REQUESTED BY SUFFOLK CENTER TO PLOT A TARGET THAT WAS AT THAT TIME 15 MILES BEARING 060° FROM JORK. THE TARGET PROCEEDED ON A HEADING OF ABOUT 260° UNTIL I MILES BEARING 090° FROM THE RADAR. THEN STARTED A SERIES OF LEFT HAND 360° ORBITS. AFTER 3 OF THESE ORBITS THE TARGET TOOK UP A RATHER IRREGULAR NORTH WESTERLY HEADING WITH SEVERAL LEFT AND RIGHT HAND TURNS STARING IN A TRACK OF 330°. I WAS HEADED TO ASK THE G-97 TO INTERCEPT THE TARGET. THIS WAS DONE AT A POSITION 3½ MILES, BEARING 090° FROM JORK. THE G-97 PILOT REPORTED THE TARGET NO RESPONSE AS A/C WENT NAVIGATIONAL SIGHTS THAT GAVE OFF A STROBING EFFECT. THE TARGET CONTINUED TO 8 MILES BEARING 350° AT WHICH TIME A RIGHT TURN TO ABOUT 160° AND IT HAS ADVISED THE PATTERN COULD BE DISCONTINUED.
