11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
Object South of Venus moving SE observed for three hours. Smaller than Venus. Flashing different colors, red. Venus assumed to be in the South.

Fomalhaut is the only star of 1st mag in the South at the reported time (if time is Z time).

OBSERVER: Venus at 9h47.23 on 1 Oct. Not visible therefore altitude was not observed. Venus in Virgo. At 0500 local 2 Oct Venus was at 9h 51.5'. The sun was at 12 32.24. Flashing about one hour later, and a glow less bright than Venus could not be bright and flashing at a reference point from Venus than its was a lesser magnitude. Mars and Jupiter are not visible in the South at the reported time. Case regarded as an unreliable report.
