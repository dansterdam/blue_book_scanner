11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
Observer watched a cigar shaped object with its long axis parallel to the ground flying toward the NE. The altitude was about 4-5 of a high flying aircraft. The object followed a course from SE to NE. The object had the color of red-white flames. The object had an incandescent exhaust about five times the length of the object itself.

The description is consistent with that of an aircraft observation.

1. The following is the information concerning a report of an unidentified flying object on the night of 8 July 1966.

2. Description of the object:
   a. Shape - It looked like a missile, cigar shaped.
   b. Size - It looked like a straight pin as far as length and was about as big around as the head of a pin.
   c. Color - It was the color of red-white flame.
   d. Number - There was one.
   e. Formation - None.

   f. Details - This object was flying with its long axis parallel to the ground.

   g. Tail - The object had an incandescent exhaust about five times the length of the object itself.
   h. Sound - None.

3. Description of the course of the object:
   a. The observer happened to look at the sky and saw this object.
   b. The altitude was described as about that of a B-52, about 30,000 ft.
   c. The altitude when the object disappeared was the same as when first seen.
   d. The object followed a course from SW to NE. The observer described the course in terms of, "from White Sands to Chicago."
   e. The object disappeared in the NE due to distance.
