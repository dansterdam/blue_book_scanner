10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Obj with appearance of bright star in w. Obj appeared to move horizontally and vertically about 5°. Dropped to about 20° elev in 15 min and 25 min later disappeared on horizon.

11. COMMENTS
Obj sighted was probably bright star Sirius, magnitude -1.6. Direction and elevation reported for obj and that of star were same at time of sighting. Time reported for disappearance coincident with settling time for star as viewed fm location of witness. Sirius is brightest star (apparent) in our heavens and was misidentified by this witness. Identification was probably helped along by distortions due to atmospheric refraction and active imagination.
