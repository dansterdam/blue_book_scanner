PROJECT 10073 RECORD

1. DATE - TIME GROUP: 31 October 65 01/001CZ
2. LOCATION: Amityville, New York

3. SOURCE: Civilian
4. NUMBER OF OBJECTS: One

5. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION: 15 minutes
6. TYPE OF OBSERVATION: Ground-Visual

7. COURSE: NE, stationary in S

8. PHOTOS: No

9. PHYSICAL EVIDENCE: No

10. CONCLUSION: Astro(VENUS) Description characteristic of star/planet. Venus in SW setting at time of report. Considered as the most probable cause.

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS: Object was circular with square rectangular windows, traveling with a circular motion. Approximately size of a basketball when viewed from 15 miles away with a white pulsating glow, from the inside. An orange or reddish light on one part of it. Not traveling in a set pattern. Observer later stated object was traveling NE. Object was motionless for a while, then disappeared immediately. Object was first observed with naked eye, late through binoculars. If object SW then NE flight would be toward observer at 1910L
