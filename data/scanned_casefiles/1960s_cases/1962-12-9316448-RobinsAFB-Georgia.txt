References: Incident reported by Mr. [redacted] to Captain John T. Taylor, USAF, Flying Safety Officer, WRAMA.

SUMMARY:
On 13 December 1962, at approximately 0915 hours, Capt. Taylor reported to Security and Law Enforcement Division that Mr. [redacted] had advised by telephone that at approximately 0712 hours, that [redacted] observed an object traveling west southwest, descending at 45 to 60 degrees angle going from north to south. Object was alleged to be close by. The color was a bright green light with lighted tail approximately one foot long. Object was travelling very fast, disappeared behind trees.

Incls: Area, Diagram of UFO sighting.

f. Identifying Information on Observer:
(1) Civilian, Mr. [redacted], Georgia. Radar Repairman, RAFB, Georgia. Considered reliable.

g. Weather and Winds - Aloft Conditions at Time and Place of Sighting:
(1) Observer revealed weather conditions - Clear, temperature approximately 100 F. No wind.

(2) Report obtained from Detachment 13, 15th Weather Squadron, RAFB, Georgia, revealed - Weather Clear, Visibility Good, No Wind. Sunrise 0727.

h. Any other activity or condition which might account for the sighting -
(1) Investigation revealed that the area known as Jefferson Hills, Warner Robins, Georgia, situated at the approximate point where object was sighted is equipped with mercury vapor street lights.

(2) Mr. Grady L. Cox, Service Coordinator, Service Department, Flint Electric Membership Corporation, Warner Robins, Georgia, advised that reference street lights are automatically and individually operated, turning on at dusk and cutting off at dawn by a switch contained in the head of each lights. Mr. Cox advised that said lights usually flash and/or flicker when the switch operates.

i. No known air traffic or balloon releases in the area at time of sighting.

j. Comments of Preparing Officer:
(1) Mr. William D. Barker, Investigator, Security and Law Enforcement Division, WRAMA, Robins AFB, Georgia, conducted the investigation into the alleged sighting of UFO described above. Leads and clues developed in the case reflected that the mercury vapor type street lights referred to in this report probably was the object sighted.
