Field Officer

New Orleans 16, La.

My dear

This is to acknowledge with thanks your letter of 10 August 1962, reporting the unidentified celestial object you sighted on 29 July 1962.

We regret that we have been unable to determine the identity of the object, but have forwarded your report to the National Aeronautics and Space Agency, which is in the best position to furnish the information you seek.

We appreciate the effort and interest you have shown in our marine reporting program and look forward to the continued receipt of such navigational information as may come to your attention during future voyages.

Sincerely yours,

M.S. "FERNGATE"
OSLO

The North Atlantic Ocean 19 th. August 1962.

To the United States Navy Hydrographic Office,
Washington 25,D.C.

The first Officer Sverre Nygård of the Norwegian M.s."Ferngate"
Captain G.Nilssen, will hereby report the following:

At 2116 G.M.T. July 29,1962, in lat. 31 06 N.,long.32 18 22 E.
something that looked like a Satellite or a bright star was observed in the
vicinity of Pegulus,in a bearing 235.

The Satellite-like object traveled in a Eastward direction
toward Vega and down to the horizon.

The object was visible about 7 minutes.Weather clear, wind N.W.
force 3, barometer 1006 mb, air temperature +26 Celsius.

I am also interested to know what this could have been.
With thanks for further information regarding this phenomenon.
I sign.

Yours faithfully.

[Redacted]
New Orleans 16.
