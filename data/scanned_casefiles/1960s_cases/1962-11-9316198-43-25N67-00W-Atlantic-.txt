T.M.S. IRVING GLEN, Nov. 5th 1962.

AFSC (FTD)
United States Gov. Mtng Off.
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

At 0250 G.M.T. November the 2nd, 1962, in lat. 43°25' N., long. 67°00' W., on passage from St-John, N.B. to Venezuela, a very bright object of color greenish, about half the size of the moon, was observed. It appeared bearing 070° true, altitude 45°, and traveled straight downward to an altitude of about 3°. When it disappeared it was about the size of an orange. It was visible about 5 seconds.

Weather blue, no wind, calm sea, barometer 1031 mlbs, and air temperature 52°F.

Master of the ship:

Second Officer
T.M.S. Irving Glen,
Kent Lines Ltd.,
St-John, N.B.,Canada.
