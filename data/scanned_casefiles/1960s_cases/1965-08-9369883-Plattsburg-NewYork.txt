PROJECT 10073 RECORD

1. DATE - TIME GROUP: 20 August 65  21/0216Z

2. LOCATION: Plattsburgh, New York

3. SOURCE: Military

4. NUMBER OF OBJECTS: One

5. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION: 3 Minutes

6. TYPE OF OBSERVATION: Gnd-Visual, Gnd-RADAR

7. COURSE: Maneuvered

8. PHOTOS: No

9. PHYSICAL EVIDENCE: No

10. CONCLUSION: A/C VISUAL POINT 1.16 (U) E.O.

Visual characteristics of an A/C, Radar returns indicate an A/C with B97 or "Manning" equipment. Flight pattern consistent with this evaluation.

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS:
GCA Radar experienced jamming for a period consistent with the visual sighting. Jamming confined to the position of the object under observation. Jamming ceased with the departure of the object. Object maneuvered, described as flat 15-20 ft. in diameter. Color was bright metallic with bluish reflection. Circled back and forth at high speed turns. Times included a circle with 1/2 mile radius. Time was 15-8 and the distance from the observer estimated at 2-3 miles. After circling for 2 minutes, the object flew away to the east at high speed. Flight near base of clouds at 4,500 ft. Flight constant with A/C on jamming mission.

1. Reference your message 389 DC011899 AUT 65. Object observed on the night of 20 August 1965 by Major Harry Button has all the characteristics of an aircraft sighting. Request investigation into possible aircraft in the area at time of observation.

The reported disappearance of an object on GCA radar was to the West while the visual observation indicated departure to the East. It is requested that a more detailed report of the radar portion be obtained to include duration and possible causes of jamming.
