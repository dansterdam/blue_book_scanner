Dear Mr. [redacted]

The report of an unidentified flying object you gave to the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory was referred by agency to the Air Force Aerospace Technical Intelligence Center for analysis and/or evaluation.

It is the conclusion of the Aero-Space Technical Intelligence Center that you probably observed a meteor. The absence of a trail is no reason to rule out a meteor, for some leaves trails and others do not. It is also true that some of the trails which are left are faint and can only be seen with an optical aid. Further, not meteor trails are not very persistent and may not be long enough for detection.

It is improbable that you saw the flame from a jet aircraft; first because this flame is predominantly blue, and second, it would not be very bright from the 90° vantage point enjoyed by you in viewing this object.

You also included a newspaper article concerning the objects reported to be circling the earth with Echo, but in the opposite direction. ATIC is of the opinion that these objects were probably high flying aircraft.
