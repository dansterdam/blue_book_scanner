11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS:
Object like a white light larger than star. In South. Disappeared with dawn.

ORIGINS: Contact with witness following day. Husband thought object was star. Location x South. Star Sirius to South at 90 deg elevation. With in last possible portal of reported direction is wrong. 26 Oct 64
At approximately 1220 [redacted] of University of Dayton called.

Last night, 25 Oct 64 at 10:50 pm saw an object. He was going East on Patterson Road, stopped for traffic light at Shroyer Road, looking East object fell in blazing flame. Thought maybe it was space debris. Saw for a few seconds. 30 deg angle played out in Blue-Red. Towards end more dropping than straight flight. Little to South of Mount Saint John appears to come from North. (Mount Saint John is approximately three to four miles from corner of Shroyer and Patterson)

At 1500 hours we called SPADATS. No decay reports for that time as yet.

M/R 70150 Called Flight Ops. Will check with tower operators on duty and call back for possible observation that night. Return call indicated that operators did not observe meteor or anything unusual at the time of the sighting.
