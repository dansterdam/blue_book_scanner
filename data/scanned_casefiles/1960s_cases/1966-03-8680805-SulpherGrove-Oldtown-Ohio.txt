11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
Looked like a small star only it appeared to jump up and down. Reddish tinge color to bluish white. Witness was rather vague regarding its motion said that he couldn't estimate its speed. It moved from 5 to 90 degrees in 15 minutes. However another witness thought it was a star and said that the 10 ft drop could have been an illusion.

Sighting of 23 March 1956

Four Groups - 1 Group North of Yellow Springs , 1 Group North of Runway, 39.51 Lat, 84.41 Long, 1 Group NW of Sulpher Grove 1 Group SSE and of Base, 3 miles north of Oldtown. Second group saw object, couldn't estimate speed, looked like small star, like pea, or 1/inch, first at 5 deg SA, elev 81 deg, moved, jumped up and then dropped down, 5 deg az to 90 deg elev, North - East, 90 az, 4.5 elev, had sexton compasses. At 0500 lost sight and contacted other group. It looked reddish color, couldn't identify as a/c, just point of light, couldn't estimate speed, 5 deg - 90 deg, 15 minutes. Reddish ting, to bluish white. Did see Venus 7.526 elev, 119 deg az, Paul Managhan, 119 Lawnview Avenue, Dayton, Ohio 45409 said the 10 foot drop could have been an illusion.
