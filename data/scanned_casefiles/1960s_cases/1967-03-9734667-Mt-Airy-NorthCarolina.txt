PROJECT 10073 RECORD

1. DATE - TIME GROUP: 3 Mar 67  4/0130Z

2. LOCATION: Mt. Airy, North Carolina

3. SOURCE: Civilian

4. NUMBER OF OBJECTS: Several

5. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION: Several minutes

6. TYPE OF OBSERVATION: Ground Visual Bx

7. COURSE: Southward

8. PHOTOS: Yes  XX No

9. PHYSICAL EVIDENCE: Yes  XX No

10. CONCLUSION: Aircraft (possible)

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS:
Observers watched two orange colored objects which were later joined by a third. The two lights were flying side by side as if in formation. There was a flashing red and white light noted. A third object joined similar lights. First two and all three traveled in formation toward the south. One observer said there were more than 15 lights. Four or five lights on each object. All were in a horizontal plane traveling toward the south. The observers also watched several similar objects travel. From the east and head toward the south. There are three low level aircraft training routes in the immediate area. In so possible that the witnesses saw normal aircraft on a training flight.

Gentlemen:

I would like to report sighting some unusual flying objects south of town, Mt. Airy, N. C. on the night of March 3, 1967. These objects were first noticed by my son, Bob age 8, and my daughter, Jane age 17 while walking a falling pattern from the front steps of my home, at this address, which is near the center of this town of 5000 population. I enclose her description of events as attachment #1.

I was working at the telephone office, where I have been employed as a central office repairman for 21 years, when I received a telephone call from my daughter telling me of two strange objects. I immediately gave home, which took about five minutes, but nothing was in sight. My wife and daughter told me that I was too late to see anything. I received the call about 9:30 P.M. E.S.T.

Pat gave me a very good description of sighting two orange objects which were first noticed by a third. It sounded very unusual to me and I was deeply intrigued by it.

We stayed in the yard a while and finally went in the house and she and I sat on the enclosed porch. The events that followed were enclosed in attachment #2, which was written by myself, my wife, and their son.

The attached descriptions were written next morning, independently, observed, while it was fresh in our minds.

Two pictures were taken with a 35mm camera with 50mm lens. These were taken at F3.5 at 1/10 sec. on Tri X film, ASA 400. Since I am sure they are underexposed, they have not been developed as yet. I am planning to take test exposure on another roll of stars and a development time that will yield a good picture. Since it has been cloudy since Friday night, the test roll has not yet been made, and thus the delay to see if we get any pictures. At this time I don't think so, because they looked mighty well in the view-finder.

March 3, 1967 8:20 p.m. EST                Attachment 1   page 1

The first thing that I saw was a shooting star that traveled directly over the house. In a few seconds as I was searching the sky for other stars I saw a distinct bright light approaching from the east. My little brother Bob saw the object too and he kept saying "there is an airplane," "there's an airplane." But soon it became evident that the light was not caused by an airplane, or at least it did not appear as any airplane that I had ever seen before. (The first light may have been followed by a second, because suddenly from no where another light appeared. These two lights began to level off and fly side by side, as if in formation. The distance between the two lights varied from time to time. They were now approaching our house from the east. The continued for a while straight on this course, coming closer and closer to the house. (the use of close here is somewhat at the wrong term. At no time was the object actually very close to us.) The nearer that the objects came, the larger they appeared. The color that had previously been a weak red now appeared as two separate colorsone red light and one white. Bob and I continued watching, but as the objects continued coming toward us, we became somewhat afraid and we ran into the house. We called our mother and when we all three came back out (I picked up binoculars on my trip inside) the lights were in almost the same location, but they were further away from us. We began to move in a straight line toward the south. In follow their progress. The first time that I viewed them through the binoculars they appeared as red and white lights. I went into the house after giving the binoculars to mother and called my father who was at work. When I came out again, the lights appeared to be much closer to us. They were midway between the east and south. I looked through the bin oculars. The lights were more distinct. They did not appear to be flashing now. There was some mass discernible behind the lights. I could not distinguish the shape clearly. During all of these happenings the lights were continuing to move southward. At times, as we watched their progress the lights did not appear to move at all, and then they would begin again. Suddenly from my right, a third moving object appeared. This light passed, then lining up with the other two it too "fell in line" and led to other objects.
