                                          Indicated Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object

Time: Wednesday, 6/27/62 - Approx. 9:30 AM
Place: Ohio Route #3, approx. 1 mile south of crossover, 
       Ohio Turnpike, southwest of Cleveland.
Course of Object: approx. NNE - NE
Altitude of Object: estimated 2,500 - 3,000 feet.
Distance of Object: estimated 3,500 - 4,000 feet, orig. sighting.
Weather: Light clouds, generally sunny, clear, 11 mph wind.

Details of Sighting:
I was driving north on Route #3, 50 - 55 MPH - windows open - 
looked out my left window, and sighted object at approx. 10 o'clock, 
moving across my path and away from me in an approximate NNE-NE 
direction. Kept object in sight for two or three seconds until 
it passed behind some trees. - I interrupted sighting to observe 
road, and, upon looking back, lost object in distance, estimated 
at one o'clock. Object followed a straight line, and, as near as 
can be estimated, a level course. No sound was heard, prior to, 
during sighting - no sign whatever of exhaust smoke, or contrail.
Lost impressive factor was individual or occasional space, as 
witnessed by great diminution in size of object in flight during 
short time of observance.

Observed Details of Object:
The assumed estimate of altitude and distance of object must be 
subject to some qualification. With first observation, object's 
color was visible - and remained visible - for first two or three 
seconds of observation. Object had a dull, grayish-black, 
size; it likewise assumed normal gray-black appearance of any 
airman at extended distance. During this first few seconds of 
seconds of sighting, the reflective metallic surface of object 
seemed to be surrounded by a narrow, spectrum-like aura - probably 
a natural result of bright light on a reflective surface at that 
distance.

Shape of Object: Ellipsoid - no visible empennage or wings, 
whatever was Long, approx. four to five hundred feet in length - 
could have been disc-shaped, but unable to confirm.

Qualifications of Observer:
Field engineer for an abrasive manufacturer - qualified in 
detailed observation and reporting - close familiarity with all 
terrains, military and civilian - several hundred hours commercial 
flight. Must emphasize - have never observed any aircraft within 
one quarter the speed of this object.
