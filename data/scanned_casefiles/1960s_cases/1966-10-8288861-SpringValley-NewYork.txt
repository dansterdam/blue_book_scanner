7. COURSE: Stationary
8. PHOTOS: No
9. PHYSICAL EVIDENCE: No

10. CONCLUSION: Astro(Sirius) ✓ 
@ 0100Local Sirius was on an azimuth of 126 degrees and at an elevation of approximately 15 degrees.

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS:
Observers watched a round multi-colored light in the sky. The light was compared with a star except for the flashing colors and apparent movement. Object appeared to be moving slowly back and forth. The object did not disappear while the observers were watching.

The observers have identified the same body as in their original sighting on successive nights, it was Sirius. Investigating officer positively identified Sirius in relation to its position to the constellation "Orion" better known as the navigator's triangle.
