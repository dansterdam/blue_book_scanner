1. (U) The following information is the result of an investigation conducted by Captain Howard D. Lowrey, AOINC/MS, Combat Intelligence, Intelligence Division, 43rd Bombardment Wing (U), on 26 and 27 August 1965. Investigation was conducted in accordance with AFR 200-2 concerning the report of an Unidentified Flying Object (UFO).

2. (U) Description of the object: Nine (9) light Blue plastic bags approximately 36 inches in length, each bearing the inscription: "WARNING! To avoid chances of suffocation keep away from babies and children. Do not use in cribs, beds, carriages or play pens. This bag is not a toy." The bags were tied together with a string that extends approximately 35 inches below the bags. Attached to the string is a warning number T-223 and T-232. Enclosure the battery and bulb were two batteries, one marked "Eveready" with green and purple. The plastic bags appear to be type utilized by commercial cleaning establishments to enclose clothes for customers.

3. (U) Investigation Information: Road, Northwest, Arkansas, age 50, self-employed Asphalt Broker, reported observing the UFO approximately 0525L hours, on 24 August 1965. He had just arrived at an area approximately 150 yards back of his house. He estimated the wind to be very light. From a higher ground he first saw the object, which was a light, white object. He took the UFO to Mr. Glenn Bates, Chief of FAA, Drake Airfield, Subsequently Mr. Bates was interviewed by a reporter from the Northwest open coverage in the Northwest Times, Pine Bluff, Arkansas.

Weather conditions at Fayetteville, Arkansas during the period from 2200, 23 August to 0800L, 24 August was:

b. Ceiling - Clear
