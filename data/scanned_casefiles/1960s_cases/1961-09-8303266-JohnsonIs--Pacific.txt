AF TN: 1436 (19 Sep 61)   BASKETBALL IN DIAMETER AND FIVE DIAMETERS IN LENGTH. OBLONG IN SHAPE AF WHITE ADBLUSH IN COLOR. ON APPROXCOURSE 180 DEGREES AND AT A SPEED FASTER THAN THAT OF A JET INTERCEPTOR. SEEMED ON A BALLISTIC TRAJECTORY. TO-AND-ONE-HALF SECONDS AFTER INITIAL SIGHTING SPARKS SHOT OFF FROMNOSE. SIMILAR TO A SPARKLE. THERE WAS NO CHANGE IN COURSE/SPEED OR TRAJECTORY. OBJECT COULD HAVE BEEN A SHOOTING STAR BUT LOW ALTITUDE AND PROXIMITY CAUSED OBSERVER TO QUESTION THIS. LIGHT INTENSITY WAS SIMILAR TO NORTH STAR AND REMAINED CONSTANT. OBJECT VANISHED OVER HORIZON.

4. RADAR OPERATOR: OBJECT POINTED OUT BY PLANE CAPTAIN. APPEARED AS A BALL OF FIRE FLASHING THRU THE SKY. OBSERVED OBJECT FOR APPROX 3 SECONDS. REDDISH - ORANGE IN COLLAR. COURSE 180 DEGREES. FASTER THAN A JET AIRCRAFT. ANGLE OF ELEVATION 25-30 DEGREES. APPEAREDCLOSE TO ISLAND. APPROX TWO TIMES SIZE OF NORMAL STAR. CORSE/SPEED REMAINED CONSTANT BUT RAPIDLY LOST ALTITUDE. LEFT RED - ORANGE STREAK IN SKY. SIMILAR TO SPARKS FROM AN EMERY WHEEL.

5. ECK OPERATOR - SAW OBJECT FOR 2-3 SECONDS. SIZE SIMILAR TO ROTATING AERODROME BEACON. NOT CERTAIN OF COLOR, BUT WAS LIGHT. SAW BRIGHT RED SPARKS TRAILING FROM SIDES. JUST PRIOR TO LOSING SIGHT OF OBJECT SAW SPONTANEOUS BURST OF LIGHT, BRIGHT RED IN COLOR. OBJECT DID NOT ALTER COURSE/SPEED. SPEED APPEARED NO FASTER THAN A JET FIGHTER. SEEMED AT LOW ALTITUDE AND CLOSE TO ISLAND.
