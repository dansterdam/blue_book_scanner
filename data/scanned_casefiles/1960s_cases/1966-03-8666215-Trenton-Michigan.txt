Dear Sirs;

My name is [redacted] and I am thirteen years old. I am alaround space enthusiast. I've been reading up on these UFO's or extraterrestrial vehicles as you call them. I understand that the greatest percentage of these sightings can be explained, but I think I have one that might fall into the unidentified category.

I'm a Detroit Free Press carrier in Trenton, Michigan and I get up quite early in the morning to deliver the newspapers. On the morning of March 14,1966 at about 5:30 in the morning I was just starting my route when I happened to look towards the W.J.R. radio tower. I usually look for the constellation Gemini. Well, as I was looking at the tower I saw a whitish blue object about the size of a basketball ( evidently they were much bigger because I was looking at it from a distance) and flying at terrific speed. At first I thought it was a meter or a falling star or some other explainable thing-- but then my thoughts changed. It began dodging around the radio station (tower) (drawing included). It came from the west out of the blue as they say and progressed steadily. It slowed down to an and then made almost a 90 degree turn going straight up and climbed to about 12000 feet, and then went into a dive heading straight down and going fast. It pulled out and flew west again and then all of a sudden it turned and went east and into another dive, turned around and went west again. Just before it was coming to the tower it made another almost 90 degree turn and went up until it turned to the east and flew away.

I watched for about ten minutes from the time it first came into view until it flew out of sight.

The morning that I saw it was a clear and cold morning meaning that the sky was cloudless without a moon. A few stars were visible but it was cold. The day before was cold also, as a matter of fact the whole week before was cold also.

There weren't any visible burning lights like the other UFO's have had on them, but I noticed something unusual every time it speeded up. The whitish blue began to fade. I didn't think much of it until I read in the paper about sending any information of these sightings into the nearest Air Force Base or Project Blue Book. I never used to believe in them but I sure do now. I'd like to know

Reference the attached letter from requesting the evaluation on his sighting of 14 March 1966. A detailed study on the sighting has been completed. His case is being placed in the "Other" category, specifically, Conflicting Data.

In one place he mentions that the object disappeared after approximately ten minutes. In another place he mentions that the object did not disappear while he was watching it. The description, color-wise, is similar to that of a satellite observation. An aircraft in the distance could have been observed and at such a distance, turns would have appeared much steeper than usual. The turns could account for the color fading at times.
