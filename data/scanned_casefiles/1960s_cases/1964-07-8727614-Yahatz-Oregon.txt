PART II. CHARLES E. PETERSON (RETIRED TSGT, INTELLIGENCE
OPERATIONS SPECIALIST, QOTRL REPORTED SIGHTING UFOB AT 0640Z
WHILE NORTH OF YAHATZ, OREGON, ON 21 JULY 1964. HE WAS LYING
ON HIS BACK WHEN HE NOTICED MOVEMENT OF A BRIGHT OBJECT SLIGHTLY
BELOW THE MOON AND 45 DEGREES ABOVE THE HORIZON. THE OBJECT
RESEMBLED AND WAS APPROXIMATELY THE SAME SIZE AS A STAR, HOWEVER
IT WAS A BRIGHTER BLUE-WHITE. THERE WAS ONLY ONE OBJECT. THE
OBJECT ROSE ACROSS THE FACE OF THE MOON, TURNED HALF LEFT AND
PROCEEDED UP THE COAST IN A NORTHERLY DIRECTION. NO DIVERSIONARY
MOVEMENTS WERE NOTED, BUT THE OBJECT PAUSED FOR A SPLIT SECOND
AND THEN PROCEEDED AGAIN IN A STRAIGHT LINE, IT PASSED THROUGH
THE FIRST AND SECOND STAR OF THE HANDLE OF THE BIG DIPPER.
MR [REDACTED] GAVE THE IMPRESSION OF AN INTELLIGENT OBSERVER.
HE HAD PREVIOUS INTELLIGENCE EXPERIENCE AS AN OPERATIONS SPECIALIST
IN THE AIR FORCE. HE WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE FORMAL REPORTING OF
UFOB INCIDENTS WHILE STATIONED AT GREAT FALLS, MONTANA, IN 1953.
