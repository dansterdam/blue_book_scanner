GUANTANAMO BAY, CUBA
6 APRIL 1960

1. On 6 April 1960, while conducting operations in the Guantanamo Bay area, the USS Van Voorhis (DE 1028) recovered a series of photographs, reportedly taken by the crews of various US naval vessels operating in the area at the time.
2. The photographs were analyzed for the Commanding Officer, USS Van Voorhis, and the following facts were determined:
a. The photographs were taken between 0200 and 0400 hours on 4 April 1960.
b. The photographs depict unidentified aerial objects seen in the sky over Guantanamo Bay, Cuba.
c. The objects appeared to be metallic in nature, circular in shape, and approximately 30-40 feet in diameter.
d. The objects were observed maneuvering at high speeds, making sharp turns, and appearing to hover at times.
e. No sound was heard from the objects.
f. The sightings occurred over a period of approximately 2 hours.
3. The photographs and associated documentation have been forwarded to Project Blue Book for further analysis and evaluation.
