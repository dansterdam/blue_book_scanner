WEEKLY SERIES JULY 28-AUGUST 3, 1969

The charts in this publication are a continuation of the Principal charts of the Weather Bureau publication, Daily Weather Map. They include the Surface Weather Map, the 500 Millibar Chart, the Highest and Lowest Temperatures Chart, and the Daily Precipitation Chart. All of the charts for one day are arranged on a single page of this publication. They are copied from open station weather maps prepared by the National Meteorological Center, Weather Bureau.

The symbols used on the Surface Weather Map and the 500 Millibar Chart are the same as those used previously in Daily Weather Map. An explanatory sheet is available, and single copies may be obtained by writing to Environmental Services Administration, Publication Services, Code 133, Washington, Maryland 20852. Bulk copies may be ordered from the Superintendent of Documents, Government Printing Office, Washington, D.C. 20402, at a cost of $3.75 per 50 copies. Checks should be made payable to the Superintendent of Documents.

The Surface Weather Map presents station data and the analysis for 7:00 a.m., EST. The tracks of well-defined low pressure areas are indicated by heavy lines; the locations of these centers at times 6, 12, and 18 hours preceding map time are indicated by small black squares enclosing white crosses. A series of precipitation areas is indicated by shading. The weather reports that are printed here are only a fraction of those that would be on the standard weather map, and on which the analyses are based. Occasional apparent discrepancies between the printed data and the standard map cannot be included in the published maps because of lack of space.

The 500 Millibar Chart presents the height contours and isotherms of the 500 millibar surface at 7:00 a.m., EST. The height contours are shown as continuous lines, and are labeled in feet above sea level. The lines are drawn at distinct lines in degrees Celsius. The height and wind direction are shown as small pennants below the point.

The Precipitation Areas Chart indicates the 24 hour ending at 7:00 a.m. of the reporting station. The maximum amounts in inches are plotted above the station name. The Precipitation Chart indicates the 24 hour period ending at 7:00 a.m. of the previous day. Amounts in inches are plotted above the station name.
