PROJECT 10073 RECORD

1. DATE - TIME GROUP: 14 October 66 15/0525Z

2. LOCATION: Sidney, Nebraska (3 Witnesses)

3. SOURCE: Military

4. NUMBER OF OBJECTS: One

5. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION: One Hour Plus

6. TYPE OF OBSERVATION: Ground-Visual (BX)

7. COURSE: Varied

8. PHOTOS: [ ] Yes [X] No

9. PHYSICAL EVIDENCE: [ ] Yes [X] No

10. CONCLUSION: AIRCRAFT

There was a small aircraft in a holding pattern over Sidney that was responsible for the sighting.

11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS:
Observers watched a round shaped object that had red lights flashing to green then to white. It had no exhaust, trail, or sound. The object was seen from about from about 0525 to 0710Z at an apparent altitude of 2000-3000 feet. It then rose to 10,000 feet and was last seen between the Little Dipper and the North Star.

Conversation with Capt Berkheimer of the 90th SAC, Warren AFB, Wyoming revealed that after the investigation he felt that the airmen were watching a small aircraft that was in a long holding pattern over the city of Sidney. His investigation revealed that there was such an aircraft over Sidney at the time of the sighting.

25 Oct. 66

Conversation with Capt. Dersheimer(90th SMW, Warren AFB, Wyoming) revealed that after his investigation he arrived at the conclusion that a small aircraft was responsible for the sighting. He said that he found that there was a small aircraft in a long holding pattern over Sidney, Nebraska which was air borne at the time of the sighting.

He had said that the airman out in that area on night patrols are seeing all kinds of lights. They need some instruction in the area of identification of objects.
