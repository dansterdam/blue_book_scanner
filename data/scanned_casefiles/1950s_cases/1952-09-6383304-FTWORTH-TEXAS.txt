AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

LOCATION: FT WORTH TEXAS
DATE OF OBSERVATION: 13 Sept 1952
DATE OF REPORT: 13 Sept 1952

REPORT BY: Captain Edward A. Fayman
INTERVIEW: W/Sgt George A. Rice's

Source at 2710 Hemphill, Ft Worth, Texas, at approximately 1317002 observed a stationary object just to the east of the moon. The object was whitish in color, round and clear shaped, no sound, trail or exhaust was noticed. After observation of approximately 3 minutes, object moved so rapidly source was unable to follow with the eyes. Source estimated 7 to 11 miles altitude.

Source, presently employed Department of Texas, a part time student at SW Baptist Seminary, Ft Worth, Texas. Source alleged familiarity with all types US aircraft.

Comments:
A check was made 1317:52 with Weather Officer, 2nd Weather Sq, CAFE.

One radiosonde balloon was released at 1315002 and another at 1315:02. The winds at 1317002 were 10 knots, 20,000 feet from 180°, at 25,000 feet, 10 knots from 270°. Mean rise on 13 Sept 52 was at 1312352 and at 1317002 would be approximately 54° above the horizon.

- No unusual aircraft flights were reported by any military agency during the sighting period.
