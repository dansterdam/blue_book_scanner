[Signature]
Harold L. Shimer
Captain, USAF

Commanding Officer
Williams Air Force Base
Chandler, Arizona

Dear Sir:

Last night, 27 Jan 53, at 1015 hrs, I sighted in the eastern sky an illuminated object moving in a horizontal direction at a constant course with a sustained altitude of 8,000 to 10,000 feet; at an estimated distance of not less than seven nor more than ten miles and traveling at 350 to 400 miles per hour.

At the above mentioned time, I happened to be outside the house when an airplane overhead proceeded eastward caught my attention. I followed its flight. Like usual, I dispatched an unarmed white light in its direction. The object was an illuminated object. It appeared somewhat brighter and larger than any of the stars in the area and was of a yellowish-orange color. At first I thought it to be a conventional flying aircraft. However, as I continued watching, it became evident it was not landing. It was moving broadside to its color, south. By curiosity prompted me to hurry into the house for binoculars (8x50), and after securing them, I was able to pick up the object again and get a better view.

From the binoculars I could see that the object consisted of a large spot of light with a smaller light by comparison trailing it which I took to be the tail of the object. Judging from the position of the smaller light to the larger one, the object would be of considerable length. It moved too fast for a blimp and emitted too much light for a jet. Moreover, a full moon was out which as the illumination would leave no doubt of a conventional motor type plane all but invisible. Neither the large nor small light blended nor moved around like heat.

I was able to follow it turn the classes approximately five minutes before it became faded disappeared.

Perhaps the pilot or copilot or both of the plane that first caught my attention motored the object. I believe the plane was a regularly scheduled airliner. It is a possibility the object moved the way it did to avoid contact with the plane.

There is a section of the Air Force that was set up expressly to study and evaluate such phenomena, but I do not recall its location or name. I am sending this to you for possible investigation.

I am not seeking notoriety or publicity; I hope perhaps someone else now and reported it. Last night after finally losing the object I took down some notes from which I have written this.

Should the Air Force have further questions
not herein called on me.
