10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Small milky white object shape of northrup flying wing with 8 ft wing span heading east in level flight appeared to be hanging in air. Observed from a/c at 25,000 ft heading west. Weather clear. Wingman of pilot observing object did not see object. No pick-up on GCI scopes. Observer states that object passed 100 ft over and 100 ft left of a/c. Wingman on left wing. No trail sound or exhaust. Speed undetermined. Object disappeared in distance. Made 180 deg turn in effort to catch or see object proved negative.

While flying CAP south of MPLS under CCI Control, Flight of 2 F-51's, Subway
Red "A", at FL/V 1720 at 21533 24 November 1951 saw a small white object, shape
w. Northeast, flying wing, color white, approximately 80 wing span. Heading of
object past - level - flight - appeared to be hanging in the air. Altitude 25,000 ft.
21 Flight was leading. Weather CAV, vis. approximately 3/5 miles at 25,000 ft.
Wingman did not see object. CCI controller had nothing other than the 51's on the scope.
Object passed approximately 100 ft. over and to left of 21 Flight leader, Wingman
on left wing.

Checked MPLS ARTCC for balloon flights and received word that last one was
released from University airport at 24/1445, landed 24/1951 250 miles ESE of MSP
Checked Lowry MSP to see if any classified flight plans had been received
during last 24 hours. Answer was negative.
