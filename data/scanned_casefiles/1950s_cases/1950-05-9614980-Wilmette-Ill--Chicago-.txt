ALFOB-I                                                                26 June 1950

SUBJECT: Unidentified Aircraft

TO     : Commanding General
          Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
          Dayton, Ohio
          ATTN: MOI

1. In compliance with letter, Department of the Army, GSUSA, GSOID 452.1, dated 25 March 1948, subject: Unconventional Aircraft, control number A-1917, the following information received from the Fifth Army Regional Office, Chicago, Illinois, is forwarded.

"On 31 May 1950, Dr. _______ of Evanston, Illinois, was interviewed and stated in substance: At 2045 hours on the night of 26 May 1950 he and his wife were seated in their parked car on the shore of Lake Michigan just south of Wilmette Beach in Washington Park, Wilmette, Illinois, when the _______ Called the doctor's attention to a strange object in the sky over the lake. The object was traveling south at a tremendous rate of speed and at first Dr. _______ thought that it was a 'shooting star.' While watching it Dr. _______ realized that the object was not a star but appeared to be some type of aircraft. The object was 'elliptical' shaped approximately 50 to 100 feet in diameter and appeared to be traveling on a set course 6 to 8 miles from shore. The object was in sight for three or four seconds and traveled 50 or 60 miles in that time. Altitude was estimated as 25 degrees above the horizon. When last seen the object had changed course and was headed out over the lake. The object fluoressed with a pink hue. The flight of the UNIDENTIFIED AIRCRAFT was also witnessed by the occupants of a car bearing a 1950 Illinois license plate number _______.

2. Reference paragraph 2, letter from this office, ALFOB-I, Subj, "Flying Saucer," 25 April 1950. Request information as to continuance of reporting of this type of information.

FOR THE COMMANDING GENERAL,

[Signature]
ROBERT F. BELL
Colonel, GSC
AG of S, G-2
