1. Following is the detail information regarding the radar sighting of unidentified object.

a. Object sighted at approximately 1040 hours, on 27 March 1952, over MOTBU Peninsula, Island of OKINAWA at 26° 27'N, 127° 57'E.

b. Weather in that area at the time of sighting was 6/10 stratocumulus, base 3,000 feet - tops 4,500 feet, visibility 20 miles. During the day there was no precipitation and there were no clouds at or near 13,000 feet.

c. The sighting station was located in the gun park at EMANESE, OKINAWA, 26° 21' N - 127° 50' E. Approximately 15 miles from the point where object was first sighted.

d. When first sighted object was stationary, then started to move heading was 220 degrees. Speed of object when started moving was approximately 50 mph. This rate of speed was arrived at by following: distance between first and last point was 16.9 miles, length of time traced was two (2) minutes.

e. Altitude at time of first sighting: 13,900 feet.

f. There was only one object sighted.

g. Object was last observed at 26° 26'N, 127° 41' E at 1040 hours.

h. Object was observed for approximately ten (10) minutes and tracked by radar for approximately two (2) minutes after it started to move.

i. Corporal [REDACTED], 665th AC&W Squadron

The radar operator making the observation, is considered by his Commanding Officer to be a superior soldier and radar technician and found at all times to be outstanding, reliable and trustworthy. He has been a radar technician a total of about 2½ months, is twenty-six (26) years old, and has approximately fifteen (15) years of military service.

j. Observation was made on SCR 584 B type radar set, serial No. 754, and was in good condition.

k. No photographs were taken of object.

l. One F-80C and 1 F-80I were flying and could have been picked up by radar, but no object was detected.

2. Twentieth Air Force radar station on YONTAN Mountain was inoperative at this time and the radar station on MOTBU Peninsula did not pick up this object.
