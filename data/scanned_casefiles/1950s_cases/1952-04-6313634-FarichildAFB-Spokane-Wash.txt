AF FORM 112-PART II                                                JN CLASSIFIED
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT                                UNCLASSIFIED

1st 376th Air Division
934 Bombardment Wing II
Seattle, Washington                                                24 April, 1952

1. Early the evening of 24 April, 1952, a civilian employee of a Bombardment Squadron, home on leave, at Fairchild Air Force Base Officer Housing Area, The information provided below is a condensed summary of the report dated 27 April, 1952.

2. The object was round, 4 to 5 size as viewed slightly lower than the classic of a small saucer, smaller than a dime. It could have been either a disc or a sphere. It was initially, however, and is now, still considered to be a disc. The color was white or very light gray. At the object was turning, occasionally tilting and then more at its slowing surface.

3. The witness was standing on the back stoop of his home, watching alternately, over and about the field, when he noticed this red light. At its position - - it was a full disc, very bright, with a white center. The man stated that he had seen the same thing three (3) times before and one (1) time afterwards, apparently all in the past year. He stated that the light was of much greater intensity than the navigation lights of an aircraft. At first he thought it was a plane, but as he watched, the impression of being much farther away than any of those directly in view. The object's motion was very slow, with a clear (three days) and appeared three times higher on the horizon than the mountain.

4. Time of sighting: Between 2040 and 2145 hours. Length of observation seven (7) minutes.

5. Manner of Observation: Visual from ground.

6. Address of Witness: Fairchild Village (Officers Housing Area) Fairchild Air Force Base. Made at a time heading of 042 degrees at an altitude of 2 degrees, 54 Min. Point of observation was approximately 1/4 mile from the tower.

7. Person making Observations: 1/b (Aviation employee, home on leave, Wing II, His duties cannot be given here as a security precaution)

8. Reliability of Observer: On a scale of total credibility, the witness was extremely serious and reliable. He was extremely cautious about making any statement and made a detailed manner. The object was also seen by his wife, a neighbor's wife and by two other civilians.

9. Comments: Both observing the object from the same location at the reporting civilian's home, no additional information could be added. The witnesses were unable to contribute any further details to this report.

10. Weather and Wind: Landing at 2130 hrs. (Below time of sighting)
