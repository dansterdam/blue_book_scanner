21st District OSI (T3), Langley AFB, Hampton, Virginia
SUBJ: Sighting of Unidentified Object Near Blackstone, Virginia

Since 1 January 1954, stated that at 1715 hours on 23 January 1954, while he was at home, he looked into the sky and noticed a bright, white shapeless object which appeared to be at an altitude of between twelve (12) and fifteen (15) thousand feet. Said there were a few white clouds in the sky and the object had a constant shine and remained stationary in the sky at about a thirty (30) degree angle above the horizon and due north of Blackstone.

A civilian arrived at CAA Interstate Aviation Communications station at Blackstone Army Air Field and advised the two (2) CAA employees on duty of the observation. According to the CAA log, the unknown object in the sky. According to 1721 hours, a C-47 flew over the station. The pilot of the aircraft was notified of the object in the sky and he further advised that the unknown object was between thirty (30) and forty (40) thousand feet in altitude. The pilot reportedly did not know the nature or identity of the object.

Evacuate Plane, Huber 8154, enroute to Andrews Air Force Base. Further advised that the CAA Office at Blackstone notified Washington Air Force Ponte Traffic Control, National Military Flight, Middletown, Pennsylvania, of the sighting.

And last noticed, the object at 1730 hours. According to the agent created no noise, no smoke, no vapor trail and he advised that the object was much more prominent than any aircraft. Said that he did not have any knowledge of the size of the object nor of its exact shape.

6. On 29 January 1954, Chief of the Facility, Blackstone CAA Office, was interviewed at Blackstone Army Air Field. He advised that he had been working for the CAA since 1938. Advised that he first saw the object at 1723 hours on 23 January when the CAA employees on the field called his attention to the sighting. According to the object appeared to have a bright metallic surface. According to the unknown object was between forty (40) and forty-five (45) degrees above the horizon and due north in direction.

Stated that the object was five (5) to ten (10) miles away at an altitude of approximately thirty (30) to forty (40) thousand feet. Stated that behind the object was a light layer of cirro-stratus clouds. Stated that a pilot saw the object in an altitude of thirty-five (35) to forty (40) thousand feet and they thus helped him establish the approximate altitude of the object.

Advised that the object was stationary. He further stated that the object was stationary and he could not determine the size or shape of the object, and he further advised that there was no noise, no smoke or trail, or disturbance in the sky.
