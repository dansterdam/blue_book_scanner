ROUTINE 319.1                                                5 AUG 1957

SUBJECT: Report on Unidentified Flying Object

TO:    Director of Intelligence
        DCS/O
        Headquarters, USAF
        Washington 25, D.C.

1. In accordance with letter, Hq, USAF, AFOIN-C/OC-2, subject: Reporting of Information on Unidentified Flying Objects, dated 19 Dec 51, the following information is submitted concerning an observation of an unidentified flying object. Information in paragraph below is in the same order as per 4d of referenced letter.

    a. Object described as one white disc with no protuberances. The size and speed were not determined nor any trail or exhaust observed. No sound was heard and object disappeared from view as it was traveling away from observer.

    b. Object was sighted at 1000 hours on 1 August 1952 for approximately 1 to 1 1/2 minutes.

    c. Object was observed visually from the ground.

    d. Observation was made from Brooks Air Force Base. Object was west of the station traveling southwest. Altitude was said to be "high" but otherwise undetermined.

    e. Object observed by A/1C John R. Bell, AF3531776, and A/2C John R. Daniell, AF12126589, both from Hq Sq Soc, USAFSS, this station. Reliability of their statement (attached) is unknown.

    f. Brooks AFB 1000 hours weather sequence:
       E250/5 181/857/C/OC

    g. There appeared to be no activity or condition, meteorological or otherwise, which might account for the sighting.

    h. There is no physical evidence of the sighting.

4 August 1952

At 10 AM on 1 August 1952, as I was entering the Security Service compound with a group of airmen returning to work after a movie at the Base theater, someone called attention of the group to something in the sky. Upon looking up, I saw a round disc, white in color. It seemed to be at a high altitude and was moving at a high rate of speed in a southwesterly direction; it performed no maneuvers of any kind, only flew in a straight line. I watched it until 1200 until it was too far to be seen with the naked eye. I saw nothing that could be taken for wings or tail surfaces. The plane of the object seemed to be horizontal rather than vertical. It appeared to have some depth, possible to sit in comparison with the diameter.

JOHN R. HOLL
A/1C AF38531778
Hq Sq Sec, USAFSS

STATEMENT                                                4 August 1952

On Friday, 1 August 1952, at approximately 1000 hours, I noticed a flying object in the sky. The altitude of the aircraft was impossible for me to judge but appeared to be considerably above the cloud layer, which was reportedly 2500 ft. The relative speed appeared supersonic. The aircraft was about 25 to 30 degrees NW direction. It traveled a straight course on a horizontal plane. Its appearance was disc shaped with a ratio of diameter to width of about 6:1. Its two metallic or white in color. It remained in view for about 1 and 1/2 minutes.

[Signed]
JOHN R. DANIELL
A/2C AF11216589
Hq Sq Sec, USAFSS
