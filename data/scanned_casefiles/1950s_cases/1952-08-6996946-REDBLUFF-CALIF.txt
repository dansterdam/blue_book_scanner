AF FORM 112-PART 1
APPROVED 1 JUNE 1948

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

Country: United States
Subject: Unidentified Flying Objects (Short Sights, FLYOBREF)
Location: North Central California
Date of Incident: 1 August 1952
Date of Report: 7 August 1952
Source: Ground Observer
Prepared by: W. J. Weston, Asst. Dir. of Intell.

At 0215 Pacific Daylight Time, 1 August 1952, observers disclosed visually at 0215 Pacific Daylight Time, 1 August 1952, an object which was described as approximately the size of an automobile headlight. The object was observed to be moving at an estimated altitude of 3,000 feet.

The observers, Jerry Bowen, P.O. Box 367, Redding, California and Alice Rayeroft, Redding, California were located at a G.O.C. Post at Balls Bluff, California to be approximately 1/2 mile west of that post.

The weather officer reports the area as clear with visibility of 20 miles. There were 200 to 300 feet of clouds at 10,000 feet with winds from 210 at 30 knots.

Meteorological activity, physical evidence of the sightings and identification: One United Aircraft F-51 aircraft was in the area at the time of the sighting. This aircraft was known to have observed a short time before the object appeared.

Comments of Preparing Officer:
1. No information is available to determine the identity of the sighted.
