SUBJECT: Report of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFO)

TO: Commander
Headquarters Air Defense Command
Colorado Springs, Colorado

1. In compliance with Air Force Regulation 200-2 subject as above, dated 10 August 1954, the following report of UFO investigation is herewith submitted.

2. The sighting report covers two UFO sightings, one or incidents of the United States Army located temporarily at Camp Hood, Texas, and with the confines of a Military Police company area and another sighting of a luminous like object. Major personnel were at Camp Hood, Texas, relative to a demonstration of the luminous like objects have been noted on all sides.

3. This headquarters conducted a preliminary investigation in the local area including Fort Hood, Texas, a two mile installation under the 2nd Army and United States Army and Killeen area, with the following results:

a. No organizational training is now under way involving rockets with right account for the luminous like objects.

b. No report of similar sightings at this station or nearby.

c. No unidentified persons on the CIA roster noted.

d. No known weather balloons.

SUBJECT: Report of Unidentified Flying Object; Misc.

TO: Commanding General

1. In compliance with Air Force Regulation 200-2 dated 12 August 1954, the following report of unidentified flying object is herewith submitted.

2. The sighting records emanate from Killeen area, an operational area of the United States Navy located immediately adjacent to Fort Hood, and within the confines of Camp Hood, Texas. A military police company was operating a military police base. Subject personnel were at their regular duties and therefore some of these reports were coupled with other observations.

3. This headquarters conducted a preliminary investigation in the local area including Fort Hood, Texas, a two-day effort with United States Navy at Killeen area, with the following results.

4. Organizational training is now being conducted with rockets which might account for the unusual sightings.

5. No report of similar sightings at this station.
