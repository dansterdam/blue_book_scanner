AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

4th Flight Intelligence Detachment ANG 70                                                     DL-54

At approximately 0574, 19 September 1954, (Gorge is a name in 1954), I was telephoned the tower and asked to look into a sighting that possibly was an unidentified flying object.

I came over to the tower and after talking through the pinculars and told them that it does not look like a star because it was moving in the wrong direction. I then called Combat Operations and gave them the above information. I continued to look through the binoculars with the object still visible.

I would estimate that the object was at about 175 azimuth degrees from the tower. It appeared to be at a very high altitude and an about 50 degrees elevation through the binoculars. No streamers, tail, or specific structure could be seen.

Only a bright, white light, similar to bright sunlight reflected by an aluminum surface. With the binoculars the object appeared to be about the size of a pin-head.

At 0607H, 19 September 1954, from the tower I again observed a bright object in the sky approximately 750 yards to the ground and observed the object. Then positioned the object to me and made me the binoculars. I observed the object through the binoculars. I was not able to make out any specific shape or dimensions or to estimate its altitude.

I then went out to observe the object with the map. At this time the object was still in the sky and the bright light was still visible. I then went back into the tower and continued to observe the object in relation to the clouds. I then went back into the tower a couple of minutes and then went back out and could not locate the object. The object appeared to have moved, but only slightly. The sky was clouding up very rapidly, and at 0608H the object was obscured by clouds at about 110 degrees.
