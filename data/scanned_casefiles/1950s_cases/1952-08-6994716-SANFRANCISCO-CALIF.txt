5 August 1952

BY Lt. JAMES V. VERDERBER, Asst. Dir. of Intell.

DATE OF OBSERVATION 2 August 1952

LOCATION California and Powell Street in San Francisco, California

An unidentified object was observed visually at 0235 Pacific Daylight Time for approximately 30 seconds on 2 August 1952 as a very bright light, three times as bright as a star. The object was not moving. Observer stated only able to could not to a light on a building. The altitude was estimated only as high.

The observer was located at California and Powell Street in San Francisco, California. The object was to the northwest of the observer at 213 degrees. The observer was in the area, 213 First Street, San Francisco. He stated that the stars and moon were not showing because of a high fog layer.

The weather officer reported winds at 10,000 feet were 160° at 9 knots; at 20,000 feet winds were at 200° at 23 knots.

Meteorological activity, physical evidence of the sighting, identification-action, taken and aircraft in the area were reported as negative.

Summary of Preparing Officer:

1. No information is available to determine the identity of the sighted object.
