AN OBJECT WAS SIGHTED IN THE SKY BY THE NAVY AIR STATION ON WHIDBEY ISLAND, WASHINGTON AT 1104 PST 16 OCT. 1951. WHILE F-84 AIRCRAFT WERE ON A LOCAL MISSION WERE SOME TO INVESTIGATE, THE FLIGHT OF AIRCRAFT SIGHTED THE OBJECT AND WAS TOLD TO PURSUE A POINT FOR A PERIOD OF FIFTY MINUTES. THE OBJECT WAS ABOVE THE LAND AND NOT CHANGE IN SIZE AS THEY FLEW WEST.

A NAVIGATION FROM THE 62ND GROUP CARRIER CALCULATED THE POSITION OF VENUS WAS 180 DEGREES POSITION AS THE TRUE AND MEAN POSITION OF THE OBJECT WAS WHILE FIVE DEGREES OF MEAN VENUS WAS CALCULATED TO BE.

THE REPORTING OFFICER'S OPINION IS THAT THE OBJECT SIGHTED WAS THE PLANET VENUS.

29TH AIR DIVISION (DEFENSE)                                        PAGE    1    OF    1    PAGES

A.   THE SHAPE OF THE SIGHTED OBJECT WAS ROUND OR SPHERICAL AND A LITTLE SMALLER
     THAN THE SIZE OF A DIME. THE COLOR WAS A MEDIUM GRAY AND SEEN WELL OVER
     G.G.O.T. THERE WERE NO OTHER DESCRIPTIVE FEATURES. THE OBJECT WAS NOT
     DESCRIBED. THE FLIGHT PHASE HAD NO VIEW FOR A PERIOD OF 30 MINUTES.

B.   ORIGINAL SIGHTING WAS BY NAVAL AIR STATION AT 1101 PACIFIC STANDARD TIME.
     LAST SIGHTING WAS AT 1317 WHEN THE PHOTOS ABANDONED CEASE TO NOTIFY TO BASE.

C.   ORIGINAL SIGHTING FROM GROUND WAS VISUAL. THE HIGHER PILOTS SIGHTING PLUS
     GROUND VISUAL SIGHTING WERE CONCURRENT. WHEN FIRST SIGHTED, THE OBJECT WAS
     SEEN WAS 150 MILES. THE ORIGINAL PICK UP BY NAVAL AIR STATION WHIDLEY
     ISLAND WAS AT 1245 WITH A GROUND VISUAL.

D.   THE LIGHTS FIRST SIGHTED WERE OUT AT 176°45', 125°30', THEN WEST OVER
     OLYMPIC MOUNTAINS AND THEN OVER PACIFIC OCEAN TO 125°05', WHICH WERE THEN
     SEEN OVER AREA NEAR TACOMA. ON SAME OCCASION WAS ANOTHER AIRCRAFT. THIS
     AIRCRAFT AND WAS CONSIDERABLY HIGHER. AT 1245 PST ONE PILOT WAS ON A HEADING
     OF 215° MAGNETIC-FLYING AT 20,000 FT (GEOGRAPHICAL POSITION OF AT TACOMA WAS
     16 MILES S.S.E. OF AREA WASHINGTON) WHEN SIGHTED. WHEN THE OBJECT OF
     DISTANCE WAS POSSIBLE.

E.   OBSERVERS WERE FOUR FIGHTER PILOTS. A MAJOR, TWO CAPTAINS AND A 1ST LT.

F.   THE WEATHER WAS CLEAR AND SEE MILES AT 45,000 FT. DURING THIS PERIOD WERE
     FROM 340° TO 000.

G.   THE PLANET VENUS IS VISIBLE IN THIS AREA DURING THIS PERIOD.

H.   PICTURES TAKEN FROM ONE FIGHTER AIRCRAFT SHOW THE OBJECT.

I.   THE FIGHTER AIRCRAFT FOLLOWED THE OBJECT IN A WESTERLY DIRECTION, BUT
     OBJECT SEEMED TO REMAIN IN THE SAME POSITION AND THE SAME DISTANCE AWAY.

J.   THE THREE F-94's WERE AIRBORNE AT THIS SIGHTING AND THEY WERE
     SENT IN DIRECTION OF THE FLYING OBJECT. THEY FLEW ABOUT 10 MILES OFF
     THE COAST AND WERE IN CLOSE AIRCRAFT VICINITY.
