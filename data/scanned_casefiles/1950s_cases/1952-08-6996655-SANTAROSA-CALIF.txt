SUMMARY: 228th OTD R-290 FLYOBREPT, 4 August 1952

An unidentified object was first observed visually at 1315 Pacific Daylight Time, and a third time at 1515 PDT. It was observed approximately for five minutes each time. It was described as a round disk resembling a ball, silver in color, and it had an estimated speed from 50 to 70 miles per hour. No audible sounds were reported. At first it was on an irregular course, later it was on a straight course. The object was estimated at an altitude of 15,000 feet.

The [redacted] and [redacted], Santa Rosa, California, at the GOC Post in Santa Rosa, California, were located at Gen. Rte. No. 1725. The object appeared to be 4 miles northwest of the GOC Post.

The weather was reported as very clear with winds Southwest to Northeast at 15 miles per hour.

Meteorological activity, physical evidence of the sighting, identification of the terrain and aircraft in the area, were reported as negative.

Comments of Preparing Officer:
1. No information available to determine the identity of the object sighted.
