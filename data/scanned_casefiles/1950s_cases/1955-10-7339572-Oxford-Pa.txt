WHEN INVESTIGATED EFFORTS:

1. A forecaster on duty at the US Weather Station, McGuire AFB, New Jersey (GeaRef GJAL 1513) was contacted for confirmation of the winds aloft over southeastern Pennsylvania on 3 October 1955. The forecaster was queried as to what weather station in said vicinity would be launched from to be in the vicinity of Oxford, Pennsylvania at the time in question. The answer was the Greater Pittsburgh Airport, Pennsylvania (GeaRef GJAL 1537).

2. A check with the US Weather Bureau at Greater Pittsburgh Airport, Pennsylvania revealed that weather balloons had been released from that station at the regular hours of 0400, 1000, 1600 and 2200 EST. The forecaster on duty stated that only under very unusual conditions would one of their weather balloons drift as far east as Oxford, Pennsylvania. If for some reason the balloon did not burst at the designated time and altitude, it would be possible that a balloon from Greater Pittsburgh Airport could have drifted with the winds into the general vicinity of the UFOB sighting.

3. The Ground Meteorological Section, 24th MA Group, near Swarthmore, Pennsylvania (GeaRef GJAL 4153) was contacted and the following information was received: Due to the winds aloft, at all altitudes, blowing from west to east, it would have been impossible for the balloons released by this section or any other weather station east of the UFOB sighting to be in the vicinity of Oxford, Pennsylvania on 3 October 1955.

4. GOC Filter Center at Trenton, New Jersey (GeaRef GJAL 1513) was contacted but had no information concerning any sightings in the vicinity of Oxford, Pennsylvania on 3 October 1955. The Trenton Filter Center contacted the GOC Filter Center at Baltimore, Maryland (GeaRef GJAL 2319) with negative results.

5. The Flight Service Center at Olmsted AFB, Middletown, Pennsylvania (GeaRef GJAL 1910) was contacted and revealed no information on the unidentified flying object observed in the vicinity of Oxford, Pennsylvania on 3 October 1955.

6. The Base Operations Officer at Lakehurst Naval Air Station, New Jersey (GeaRef GJAL 4102) was contacted and a check of the records revealed that none of the ships (Blimps) were flying in the vicinity of the UFOB sighting on 3 October 1955.

COMMENTS OF PREPARING OFFICER:

1. In the opinion of the Preparing Officer, the UFOB sighting reported by the observer near Oxford, Pennsylvania at 03/1615Z Oct 55 was probably a radiosonde weather balloon drifting with wind.

a. The appearance of the UFOB from the observer's description and memory sketch seemed to bear strong resemblance to a radiosonde weather balloon.

b. The direction and speed of the winds aloft seems to extend the possibility of a radiosonde weather balloon from the Greater Pittsburg Airport being sighted in the vicinity of Oxford, Pennsylvania on 3 October 1955.

c. The fact that the UFOB was not sighted by other observers suggests that it was an indefinable object.

d. SOURCE stated that "weather balloon equipment" had been located in the vicinity of the UFOB sighting in the past.

2. The observer's description of shape, size, distance, speed, and direction, plus the absence of sound, seems to rule out the chance of the UFOB being self-propelled, therefore increasing the change that it was probably a friendly object.
