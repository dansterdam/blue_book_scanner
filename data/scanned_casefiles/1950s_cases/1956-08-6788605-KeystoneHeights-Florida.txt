SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object.

TO: Director, Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. SYNOPSIS: Unidentified flying object sighted by Naval Officer in vicinity of Keystone Heights, Florida, on 4 August 1956 at approximately 2200 hours.

2. DETAILS: Lieutenant _______, Naval Air Technical Training Center, Naval Air Station, Jacksonville, Florida, on 6 August 1956, telephonically advised Special Agent LEROY CROSLEY of the Jacksonville OSI Detachment, that he has seen an unidentified flying object at approximately 2200 hours, 4 August 1956, in the vicinity of Keystone Heights, Florida. He noted a small ball of flame travelling horizontally with several small balls of flame behind it, similar to a rocket. It was stated that the object was visible for approximately two (2) seconds until it disappeared behind trees. The object as moving from East Northeast to West Southwest. It was not the opinion that the object was a meteor or other natural data available.

3. ACTION: Commander, Naval Air Station, Jacksonville, Florida; Commander, Orlando AFB, Florida; Air Technical Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio; ATIC 2-3; Commander, Air Defense Command, Ent AFB, Colorado Springs; Colorado Springs; Commanding Officer, McGill AFB, Florida notified.

4. This report is submitted pursuant to authority contained in paragraph 300(2)(c), AFR 205-1.
