1. Description of object sighted:
   Number: 1.
   Shape: round.
   Size: appeared to be 3 or 4 inches in diameter.
   Color: yellowish-golden, similar to hot metal.
   Speed: rough estimate - 500 MPH.
   Heading: south to northeast.
   Maneuverability: Constant speed and altitude. No maneuvers except one sharp turn of approximately 135°.
   Altitude: estimated 2000 feet.
   Sound: none.
   Exhaust trail: short trail or tail observed during object's turn only. Trail was the same color or darker than object.

2. Time of sighting: 0400, 18 April 1952 for approximately one minute.

3. Manner of observation: visual from a ground position.
   Mr. [redacted] was awakened at 0400 hours, 18 April 1952 by a barking dog. He went to the window to determine the cause of the disturbance. There were no lights burning in the house. While looking out of the window his attention was attracted by a bright aerial object in a northerly direction, which appeared to be about 3 or 4 inches in diameter and of yellowish or golden color. The object seemed to come toward the observer, where it turned and went out of sight in a straight line toward the northeast. Mr. [redacted] then possibly saw a shooting star, airplane or searchlight and gave no suggestion of what it might have been.
