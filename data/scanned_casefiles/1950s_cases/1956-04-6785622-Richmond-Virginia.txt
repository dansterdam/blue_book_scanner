PROJECT 10073 RECORD CARD

1. DATE
21 April 1956

2. LOCATION
Richmond, Virginia

3. DATE/TIME GROUP
GMT 21/15397 to 18362

4. TYPE OF OBSERVATION
Ground-Visual

5. PHOTOS
No

6. SOURCE
Civilian - five people - GOC
Military - two people - GOC

7. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION
approximately one hour

8. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
One object, round on top coming to a point at the bottom, size of a basket ball at arm's length, light translucent silvery color, and appeared to be a balloon. Moved slowly to the northeast at a high altitude & gradually disappeared. Seen through 2 telescopes, one of 35 power, 1 light aircraft fm a nearby field sent to 6,000 feet to observe the object but seemed no closer than when on the ground. F-94C made short search of area with negative results.

9. NUMBER OF OBJECTS
One

10. COURSE
northeast

11. COMMENTS
Concur with the Commander, Richmond Air Defense Filter Center, that this sighting was undoubtedly caused by a balloon. Received by letter, 26 April 1956.

12. CONCLUSIONS
Was Balloon
Probably Balloon
Possibly Balloon

No Report In File
