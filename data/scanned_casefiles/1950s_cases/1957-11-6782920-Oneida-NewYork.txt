Dear Colonel Bugh:

This is to advise that at 7:40 p.m. on November 13, 1957, Mr. [redacted] telephonically contacted the Albany office of the Federal Bureau of Investigation and advised that he was an executive with the [redacted] Motor Company, Canastota, New York, and had a conversation with a prominent New York, New York, to their residence in Wampsville, New York, advised him that, who had seen a flying saucer.

He stated that his wife is a very level headed individual, had not been drinking, and that he had no reason to believe that she did not see the object and described it as a flying saucer.

Mr. [redacted] put his wife on the telephone and she advised that at about 6:30 p.m. on November 13, 1957, while she was returning from Canastota to Wampsville, New York, she had seen in the distance a flying object which she described as the size of a dinner plate. She stated that she had seen the object approximately two miles in the distance and that this object was traveling very fast and from left to right.

She advised that this object was moving very fast and that when she came closer to this object, it was traveling slow.
