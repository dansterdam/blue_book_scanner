10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Witness sighted object from car. Stopped and viewed object described as round, about 200 ft in diameter, cylindrical and had two white trails attached. Object hovering. Estimated altitude at 2,000 ft. Object turned in a Northerly direction and moved at incredible speed, becoming a speck in about 1 minute. Total sighting lasted about 2 minutes. No A/C in vicinity of object. No reflection from sun on the object.

11. COMMENTS
Possibility exists that object was ball launch, however the report did not contain launch data from the Air Base. All other data adjacent to sighting. Duration short that this was a balloon observation, however since the duration was short and launch data not included the case is carried as UNIDENTIFIED.

1. 1st Lieutenant Griffith Air Force Base, Home, New York, and attached for duty at HQ, SMA/MA, reported that on 13 March 1951, at approximately 1520 hours PST, while driving his automobile in a westerly direction on the main road adjacent to McClellan Air Force Base, and with Mrs. _____ as passenger, sighted an object estimated to be at 2000 feet altitude, hovering in the vicinity of the city of Sacramento, California, approximately seven (7) miles distant from the point of observation.

2. Lt. _____ stated the size of the object especially drew his attention and that he stopped his car and stepped to the ground to better view the object and to determine what it was.

3. Lt. _____ described the object as approximately 200 feet in diameter and 90 feet deep, cylindrical, with what appeared to be twin tails attached, color, white.

4. As he watched the object he states it turned in a northerly direction away from the city and appeared to move at incredible speed without sound or trails of any sort and then from his vision in less than a minute. The overall time he witnessed the operation was a duration of approximately two (2) minutes. No other aircraft were in the vicinity of the reported object. No solar reflections appeared from the object which might tend to distort its size.

5. Mrs. _____ confirms the lieutenant's observation.
