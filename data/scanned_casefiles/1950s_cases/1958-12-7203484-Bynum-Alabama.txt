SUMMARY: (State summary of report. Give disposition in brief, and conclusions if any.)

The enclosed information is a report of an incident involving a flying object by Mr. [redacted], a civilian guard at Anniston Ordnance Depot, Bynum, Alabama. Five days were lost before the enclosed information reached this office. Therefore, this report is forwarded without the benefit of any personal interviews, to preclude further loss of time.

Mr. [redacted] sighted an oval object on a clear moon-lit night. He followed the object, which was estimated at 75 ft. altitude, for a distance of about 1/2 mile, at which time it vanished in a terrific burst of speed.

The officer who actually debriefed [redacted] considers the information B-3.

UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT REPORT

1. Description of the object:
   a. Shape: Oval
   b. Size: 6 to 8 feet in diameter and about 4 feet in height
   c. Color: Unknown; object was discernible from reflected light of moon
   d. Number: 1
   e. Formation: NA
   f. Features: none
   g. Sound: None
   h. Other Features: None; report states there were no exhaust pipes, no propellers, and apparently no noise.

2. Description of Course of Object:
   a. What first called the attention of observer to the object:
      Moonlight reflected from the object
   b. Angle or elevation and azimuth of object when first observed:
      75 feet in air - no other info.
   c. Angle or elevation and azimuth of object upon disappearance: UNK
      sighted, object was moving at approximately 10-15 mph at an altitude of 75 feet. Subject was in car at the time with lights out. He left his lights out and followed the object for about 1 mile (altitude and speed of object during this maneuver not stated). Subject then became scared and backed into a driveway to turn around. Object then stopped in mid-air and hovered over an igloo (warehouse) for a few seconds, after which it left.

   d. How did the object disappear? At a terrific rate of speed
      (comparable to a jet) to the northeast
   e. How long was the object visible? Only estimate possible is to assume a travel of the object for 3 miles at a rate of 10-15 mph

3. Manner of Observation:
   a. Ground-visual
   b. Optical aids: None
   c. NA

4. Time and date of sighting:
   a. Zulu date-time group: 060915Z Dec. '58  0415 - 5 = 0315
   b. Light condition: Night

5. Location of observer: Anniston Ordnance Depot, Bynum, Alabama

6. Identifying information on observer:
   a. Civilian Guard, Anniston Ordnance Depot
   b. Mr. Loren W. Vaughn, Special Agent, CIC, FT. McClellan, Alabama. Resultant info was passed by telephone to L/Col Roy A. Lange, District Commander 8th OSI District (TO); Maxwell AFB, ALA, at 081730Z Dec. '58. Info was passed to this office at 111702Z Dec. '58. Mr. Vaughn considers the information as B-3.
