In accordance with AG Letter 200-5, dated 29 April 1952, the following report was submitted by 1/Sgt Warren H. McFarland, Hq & Hq Det, 2nd Bombardment Sq (L), Rapid City Air Force Base, Denver, South Dakota.

1. Orange colored discs that closed 12 to 14 objects were visible in two (2) formations. To some unknown personnel, objects path was straight and level, as they continued in flight they gave an appearance of a line of lights. Speed of objects was not given.

2. 27 July 1952 at 2200 MST. Were visible for about 5 to 6 seconds.

3. Were seen from a car, parked in drive-in theater. Drivers of the car were down.

4. Three and one-half miles east of Rapid City, S. Dakota. Objects were out of observer's at about 5 miles, altitude between 12,000 to 15,000 feet, traveling in easterly direction.

5. Observer is considered reliable; he has had previous experience as aircraft crew member and is presently working in armament and electronics. Observer was accompanied by a young lady who verified the objects.

6. Weather was clear. No wind.

7. None

8. None

9. None

10. One C-47 aircraft was flying southwest, appeared to be in flight pattern for Rapid City Air Force Base.

GEORGE W. VANCOUVER
1st Sgt., USAF
