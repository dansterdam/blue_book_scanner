NORTH ATLANTIC

Hydrographic Bulletin

NORTH ATLANTIC

Third Officer J. H. Greenberg of the American SS. American
Manufacturer, Capt. R. O. Patterson, Master, reports observing
the eclipse of the moon on January 13-19, 1951, on a voyage from
New York to the United Kingdom.

The eclipse commenced at 2345 G. M. T., January 18. At 0205
G. M. T., January 19, an estimated 98 percent of the moon was in
shadow and continued for 15 minutes, when it commenced emerging from the shadow. The eclipse ended at 0425 G. M. T.,
January 19.

At 0205 the vessel was in lat. 46°55' N., lon. 35°45' W. The
weather was clear with a slight sea.

NORTH PACIFIC

Hydrographic Bulletin

LUNAR ECLIPSE
NORTH PACIFIC

Second Officer G. R. Geiser of the American SS. Hoosier Mariner, Capt. C. A. Ryan, Master, reports the following observation:

At 0105 G. M. T. on January 19, 1954, in lat. 9°31' N., lon. 86°14' W., a lunar eclipse was observed. The moon's bearing was 073° true at an altitude of 26°. This appeared to be slightly less than a total eclipse. The wind was light and variable, sea slight, sky cloudless.
