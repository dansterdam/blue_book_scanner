11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
PAA Flight in Berlin Corridor observed 4 - 3 MIG's. Source also observed a falling obj 5 - 7 miles to the right of his a/c, while at 5,000 ft. Obj appeared to be falling vertically with trail much like a flare. Glow was intense and concealed the confirmation of the obj. No smoke, just red 1 like flame. When obj struck the ground there appeared to be a boiling action and black smoke immediately followed. At this time the view was obstructed by clouds.

COMMENTS:
Apparently a munitions test of some sort with a short burning interval.

Subject: Activity in Soviet Zone Germany

4 October 1954                                                1 Oct 54

Edward J. Pilecki 1st Lt. USAF                                Capt. Pan American Airlines

1. On 1 October 1954, Captain Rickard, Pan American Airlines pilot offered to this office the following information:

a. PAA outbound flight #125 from Berlin to HAHN/MAIN via the south, north of the mountains, ZERBST (5700/1700) when Captain Rickard's MIG-15 type aircraft were flying N to S in the area. The MIG-15 type aircraft were flying in formation of 3 or 4 aircraft. Altitude of PAA aircraft was 7,000 feet climbing. Captain Rickard claims another PAA (9000) aircraft was on PAA flight approached ZERBST, source also observed unidentified object falling toward the side of a wedge area at approximately (5200/1700). The unidentified object was reported by the PAA pilot as a falling object, and at time of first visual contact 5,000 feet. Source observed no aircraft or contrails in the area of the falling object. Source stated that the unidentified object struck the ground trail observed behind unidentified object. Then object disappeared which had a boiling action. A dense black colored smoke immediately followed. At such time source's view was obscured by sand which lasted only a few seconds. After some passed, they saw no further evidence on ground in aforementioned area and no aircraft was observed flying in it area.

/s/
EDWARD J. PILECKI
1st Lt. USAF
Air Intelligence Officer
