1. SUMMARY: Mr. [redacted], Tulsa, Oklahoma, viewed at approximately 1100 hours, sometime during the summer of 1953, a took a photograph of an unidentified flying object which appeared in the sky on the report curtailed.

2. DETAILS: Mr. [redacted], residence as shown, who is employed by the [redacted] Department, Tulsa, Oklahoma, reported to the 10th District Office of Special Investigations Detachment Commander, Tulsa, Oklahoma, that he had been to lunch and was walking near the restaurant (name unknown) in Muskogee, Oklahoma, when he saw on 60-foot square in the same. He had in his possession a super Thema type camera and took a picture of the object, which was estimated to be traveling at a speed of 300 to 500 miles per hour and was at an altitude of approximately 6000 to 7000 feet. He described the object as being approximately 75 to 80 west in diameter, traveling on a horizontal plane, motionless, and traveling in a southern direction. Arrived with his photograph in this incident for fear of losing involved.

[Redacted] described the day as being bright and sunny, with a few white clouds in the sky. He had a shutter speed of 1/500 of a second and an f stop of 2nd 2nd on the camera. He further advised that the object was only in his view for a few seconds.

The original negative furnished by the informant has been reproduced at this district and is the enclosure. The original having been returned to [redacted] per his request. [Redacted] did state that the negative had been secured from Kodak Supply Company, Rochester, New York.
