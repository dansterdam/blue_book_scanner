1677

POLO LONDON
MOBILE, ALA., JULY 1--(UP)--BROOKLEY AIR FORCE BASE REPORTED TODAY THAT AN "UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT, BRILLIANT AND SILVER COLORED" WAS TRACKED BY RADAR OVER MOBILE AND THE ALABAMA-MISSISSIPPI GULF COAST LAST NIGHT.

MAJ. JAMES ZICHELLI, PUBLIC INFORMATION OFFICER AT BROOKLEY, SAID THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE A JET-TYPE AIRCRAFT OF NEW DESIGN WITH SIGHT SOUND WINGS."

FIVE OTHER RESIDENTS OF ALABAMA AND MISSISSIPPI ALSO REPORTED SIGHTING THE OBJECT.

ZICHELLI SAID THE OBJECT APPEARED ON THE BASE CONTROL TOWER RADAR SCREEN AT 8:50 P.M. CST. HE SAID RADAR OPERATORS REPORTED IT COULD NO SOUND AND LEFT NO TRAIL BUT GAVE EVIDENCE OF DEFINITELY BEING MANEUVERED.

THE OBJECT FIRST WAS REPORTED BY MRS. [REDACTED] OF MOBILE. LATER, MRS. [REDACTED] PASCAGOULA, MISS., NOTIFIED AUTHORITIES THAT SHE HAD SEEN IT AT APPROXIMATELY 7 P.M.

THE DESCRIPTION FURNISHED BY THE WITNESSES AND THE AIR FORCE MATCHED, ROUGHLY SAID.

MAJ. ZICHELLI REPORTED THAT THE OBJECT WAS TRACKED ON THE RADAR SCREEN APPROACHING MOBILE FROM THE SOUTHEAST, AFTER CIRCLING THE CITY ONE TIME, IT DISAPPEARED IN A NORTHEASTERLY DIRECTION, THE OFFICER SAID.

THE AIR FORCE COULD GIVE NO EXPLANATION OF THE INCIDENT.
