SUBJECT: Reporting of Information of Unconventional Aircraft Sighting

T.: Commanding General, Air Materiel Command
Wright-Patterson AFB Dayton, Ohio
Attn: MCIS

1. The following is a report of M/Sgt. [redacted] as given to this officer. M/Sgt. [redacted] is assigned to the 12-341 station of the 12th Weather Squadron located at the Niagara Falls Airport. The time of the sighting was 0420B 1 June 1951. Bearing of the object was C80° from this base. Distance and altitude of the object could not be definitely ascertained by the witnessing observers. Nearest prevailing at the time was 10,000 ft. broken clouds, 4 miles visibility with smoke, winds were NSW at 3 knots.

2. M/Sgt. [redacted] although not on duty at the time was leaving the building in which the weather station is located. Upon leaving the door out of the building, M/Sgt. [redacted] noted an object, and then by tapping on the window of the weather station, motioned for the two airmen on duty to come outside also. The following is a description of the object as given by M/Sgt. [redacted]: The object appeared to be of the saucer type. The center appeared to be larger and brighter than the are shaped wings protruding from the center. Both the center and the outer circumference were a glowing yellow-orange color. No sound was heard nor was any propulsion or trail noted. Movement of the object was vertical and eastward. Object first appeared about 20° above the horizon. A definite oscillating motion was noted. Object disappeared into the overcast after approximately 30 to 40 seconds.

3. The following is a resume of statements by Sgt. [redacted] and PFC. [redacted], the witnessing airmen referred to above. They observed the object east of the base but were not able to estimate the altitude nor the distance of the sighting. Its flight path appeared to be easterly and vertically, although its vertical movement was not as great proportional as its easterly movement. "A definite counterclockwise gyro-like rotation was noted. It was observed by these two airmen for approximately 25 to 30 seconds. No trail or exhaust was observed.
