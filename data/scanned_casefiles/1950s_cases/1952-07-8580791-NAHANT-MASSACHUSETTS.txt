UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT RECORD CARD

NAHANT, MASSACHUSETTS

Date: 23 Jul 52     Length: 3 min
Source: Coast Guardsman     Photos: Two
Evaluation: Unknown

Two disk-shaped, bluish lights approximately 9 ft in diameter, traveled side-by-side a relatively high ratio of speed at an altitude of 1000 to 2000 ft. Objects then made a sharp turn, gained altitude, and disappeared.

UNCLASSIFIED

19 AGT 24.152

29 JUL 1952

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters USAF
Washington 25, D. C.

SUBJECT: On 24 July 1952 a report was received at Detachment 24 Lab District GK (IC) USAF, Boston, Mass., relative to a sighting of two (2) unidentified objects by a Seaman at Nahant Coast Guard Station, Nahant, Mass. on 24 July 1952. Details developed that occurred during sighting.

Details: On 24 July 1952 a seaman first class (P) GAY, reported to OIC, Station that two (2) unidentified objects had appeared over the Nahant Coast Guard Station at 0615Z, 24 July 1952. On 24 July 1952, Seaman JULIUS R. DORRIS and others interviewed the following information.

Dimensions: Two (2) white lights approximately 5 feet in diameter, hovering at first, then moving in a form-like drift at an altitude of 1,100 to 2,000 feet.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
DOD. DIR. 5200.10.
