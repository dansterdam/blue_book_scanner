The Minneapolis Filter Center received the report of this sighting by Maj. Carl Fralvad the information to us at the 31st Air Division. The details are written below.

DESCRIPTION
An object like nothing the observer had seen before. It was clear, round. It had no shape. It made no sound. It gave off a high reflection from the sun. It looked like a solid cloud. Object traveled about 30 miles due North during period of observation.

TIME
29/0120X. Duration of observation was about one (1) minute.

MANNER OF OBSERVATION
Visual.

LOCATION OF OBSERVER
Near Waskish, Minnesota, which is about 30 miles north of Bemidji, Minn.

LOCATION OF OBJECT
Estimated altitude was 10,000 feet.

IDENTIFYING INFORMATION OF OBSERVER
None other than mentioned above.

WEATHER
High scattered clouds at approximately 25,000 feet above terrain. Visibility over 15 miles. Winds at all levels to 30,000 feet, from 320 to 330 degrees, at 10 to 20 knots.

ANY ACTIVITY OR CONDITIONS WHICH MIGHT ACCOUNT FOR THE SIGHTING
None known.

PHYSICAL EVIDENCE
No known physical evidence.                         DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS
