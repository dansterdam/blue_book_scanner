FROM: Office of Intelligence                                REPORT NO.
James Connally Air Force Base                               IR-57-52

The phenomenon sighted by Mr. [redacted] appeared as a group. Both objects being of a round or oval shape the size of a washbowl. The objects moved to be made of aluminum which shows in the sunlight. The objects were "flying in formation" and were estimated to have been traveling at a speed of 200-250 mph. There was no trail or exhaust noticeable. Type of propulsion was unknown. The objects were sighted.

The objects were sighted on 25 August 1952, at 1640. They were under the observation for approximately five (5) seconds. They disappeared either in a cloud formation or at a distance where he could no longer see them.

Mr. [redacted] was observing a military aircraft that was flying east at coordinates low altitude when he sighted the objects. The objects had already passed overhead. The objects were seen at an angle of 45° above the horizon and in the northwest. After intercepting the observer, Mr. [redacted], the altitude was approximately 10,000 feet.

The observers were Mr. [redacted] and his son [redacted]. The observation was made from their home, Waco, Texas. The geographic coordinates at this location are 31° 29' North, 97° 07' West.

Mr. [redacted] is very reliable but inexperienced. He is assistant Vice-President of the First National Bank, Waco, Texas. He is in the Army for three (3) years and four (4) months. His high character, "Salvage Officer", "Salvage Officer", and "Reclamation Officer". His high-cast rank was 1st Lt. He spent all his assignment time in the Zone of Interior.

The weather condition in this area at 1625 as recorded by the Base Weather Station were: Scattered Clouds at 5000, Winds from east northeast at 9 miles per hour and temperature 109°.

There were no recorded meteorological activities or conditions at that time which would account for the sighting.

No physical evidence of the sighting is available or in existence.

There was no interception or identification action taken on this object.
