10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
One dark round object, size of a basketball at arm's length. Object had an exhaust - ball shaped, tapered. No pencil thin object was first seen at 30 dgr elevation, 132 azimuth and disappeared at 30 dgr elevation. After observation, ball flew in a straight flight, path and disappeared in an orange-yellow puff of explosion, after having been observed visually for 1 to 3 seconds.

11. COMMENTS
Description, duration, and manner of disappearance indicate sighting was probably caused by a meteor.

12. CONCLUSIONS
Was Astronomical
Probably Astronomical
