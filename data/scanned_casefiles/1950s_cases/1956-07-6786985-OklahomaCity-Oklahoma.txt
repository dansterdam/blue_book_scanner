PROJECT 10073 RECORD CARD

1. DATE: 16 July 1956

2. LOCATION: Oklahoma City, Oklahoma

3. DATE-TIME GROUP:
Local: 17/0403z
GMT: 17/0403z

4. TYPE OF OBSERVATION: Ground-Visual (checked)

5. PHOTOS: No

6. SOURCE: Civilian (GOC)

7. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION: two to three minutes

8. NUMBER OF OBJECTS: three

9. COURSE: E to W

10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING:
Three lights each group apparently made up of two lights, size of silver dollar held at arms length. Steadily burning lights heading west. Lights were estimated to be at 5,000 feet. Sound heard was described as a rhythmic beat and almost similar to a rumble. One observer described it as a pulse.

11. COMMENTS:
Received by letter from Condr Ground Observer Squadron of Oklahoma City. Concur with the opinion of the reporting officer that this sighting was caused by the aircraft landing at Will Rogers Field with landing and navigational lights at the time of the sighting.

12. CONCLUSIONS: Possibly Aircraft (checked)

ATC FORM 320 (REV 26 SEP 52)
