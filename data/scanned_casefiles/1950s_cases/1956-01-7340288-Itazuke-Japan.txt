SUMMARY:

1. While flying on a mission on Project 119-1, the crew of aircraft Center 40 sighted an object above their aircraft traveling in an opposite direction during daylight hours.

2. The object appeared to have motion. It was first seen directly above the aircraft at an unknown azimuth and the two estimates of length of time in sight varied from 15-seconds to 45 seconds. One observer estimated the object's position at 1000 feet above the aircraft to well over 5,000 feet.

3. To shape of the object is described by one observer as that of a cigar.

4. No significance can be attached to this sighting.

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

1. The aircraft commander and co-pilot of Aircraft C Center 40, of Detachment 1, 764 Troop Carrier Sq, Kadena AB, Okinawa, Japan reported the sighting of an unidentified flying or moving object. Capt Edward J. AO 818203, the aircraft commander, stated he saw an object, which was not an aircraft, moving directly overhead about 1,000 feet above his aircraft and in the opposite direction at approximately 18/0100Z. He estimated the sighting lasted approximately 15 seconds while he turned his aircraft in a northerly heading. The weather was clear at the time.

2. 1st Lieutenant Wilder, Richard A., AO 302511, the co-pilot, stated he observed a fast moving object at an altitude much higher than 15,000 feet, directly over and traveling a southerly heading, i.e., opposite the heading of his aircraft. Object looked white and appeared to have the shape of a large weather balloon were at 40,000 feet. He stated that the sighting lasted approximately 15 seconds. He observed nothing in the object's wake. Lt. Wilder stated his aircraft was at 10,000 feet at the time, the weather above was clear and steady.

3. 2nd Lt. Smith, William W., AO 20659, the navigator, stated he did not observe anything unusual during the entire flight. At 139.05 latitude, 140 degrees - 10 degrees north feeling 270 degrees, craft appeared to the navigator to be a white object.
