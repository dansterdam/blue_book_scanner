10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Blue light observed in sky to NW in flight to SE. Five minutes light orange glow in sky as witness approached; glow size increased and color became bright orange. Witness observed kettle shape object sitting on ground 10-15 yds from edge of road. Described as 25 to 30 ft in diameter and appeared to be made of case iron. About 1 ft from top was row of port holes 10 inches in diameter. Orange glow came from the holes. Boiler port holes was another row about the same size.

11. COMMENTS
(Cont.) Bluish flame projected from these holes. No light or activity in or around object. No known or predicted method. One witness. Reported sighting on 26 Jul 1952. (Months after sighting)

1. SYNOPSIS: On 26 July 1952, this office was advised by the 
Omaha Field Office, Federal Bureau of Investigations, that on 24 July 
1952, [redacted] of Lincoln, Nebraska, reported to this office that on 
December 1951, at approximately 0200 hours, he observed both in flight 
and on the ground near Peru, Nebraska an unidentified flying object.

2. DETAILS: On 26 July 1952, this office received information 
as follows from the Omaha Field Office, Federal Bureau of Investigations:

    a. On July 24, 1952, [redacted] of Lincoln, Nebraska, reported to 
this office that in December 1951, while he was returning to Filmore, 
Nebraska at approximately 3:00 a.m., he noticed a blue light in the 
sky to the northwest which shot across the road and disappeared to the 
southwest. The object was near Hagar, Nebraska, near Lincoln, 
Nebraska, on Highway 73 and south of Nebraska City, Nebraska, and he 
had turned around and headed south to Auburn, Nebraska.

    b. [redacted] advised that approximately five minutes later 
at a location northwest of Peru, Nebraska, he saw an orange glow in the 
sky. In said that as he approached the glow he became very large and 
very bright orange. [redacted] stated that he saw a stationary object 
sitting on the ground approximately ten or fifteen yards from the edge
