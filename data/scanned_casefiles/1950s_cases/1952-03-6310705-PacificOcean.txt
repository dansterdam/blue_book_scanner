SUMMARY: (Clear concise summary of report, use additional paragraph. List enclosures in lower left. Begin text of report on this line)

1. The Intelligence Officer, 1500th Air Transport Wing, MATS, has reported the following sightings made by aircrews of the 57th Strategic Reconnaissance Squadron on routine weather flights.

a. At 2130Z on 8 March 1952, a crew observed three puffs of white smoke at an unspecified altitude at position 35°23'North, 159°23'West. At the time of observation the aircraft was at 18,500 feet of altitude. The crew reported that there were no clouds in the area and that the sea was calm. A search was made of the region but no surface vessels were sighted.

b. At 2321Z on 12 March 1952, a crew observed an oil slick at 38°43'North, 159°52'West, when the initial sighting took place. The aircraft was at an altitude of 13,500 feet. The aircraft descended to 500 feet and at this level made four passes over the slick. Ten or the 11 crew members observed the oily substance which floated over an area of about ten miles in diameter. The crew stated that two was a clear and dark. There was no clouds at 25,000 feet in the vicinity of the sighting. The crew also declared that they noticed five blocks of sea gull, such containing about 15 gulls, in the area of the oil slick.

2. In each of the above cases the Hawaiian Sea Frontier was provided full details by the aircrews.

APPROVED:
ARTHUR G. LOWE                                                          WILLIAM L. TRAVIS
Captain, USAF                                                           Colonel, USAF
