1. Attached hereto for your information is one copy of the report of investigation by Special Agent CHARLES F. ALBRIGHT, 19th OSI District, file 24-151, dated 11 August 1952, subject as above.

2. Investigation was initiated by the District Commander, 19th OSI District, Travis Air Force Base, predicated upon receipt of letter from Dr. _______ concerning the sighting of a bright luminous object traveling in the sky.

3. _______ was interviewed and stated the object was sighted at approximately 0300 hours, 20 April 1952 from his country home, Solano County, California. The object was traveling in a straight line from the northwest horizon to the southeast horizon. The altitude and speed of the object were not estimated. _______ observed no aerodynamic features of the object and stated it did not appear to be a meteor.

DETAILS:

1. This investigation initiated by the District Commander, San Francisco, California, dated 23 April 1952, concerning the sighting of a bright luminous object traveling in the sky.

AT SAN FRANCISCO, CALIFORNIA:

2. On 30 July 1952, [redacted], residence address, was interviewed at his place of business. [Redacted] stated that on the morning of 23 April 1952 he sighted an object in the sky of unconventional design.

He gave the following information concerning the object: Object appeared to be a bright circle. The object was clearly defined but not extremely sharp and did not appear to be "blurry" or as though the object were a shining light. The object appeared to be in a vertical position in the sky. The object was luminous and white-white in color. Only one object was seen and no accompanying features observed. No trail or exhaust was noticed, nor was any sound heard. [Redacted] could offer no information as to the manner of propulsion. The speed of the object was not constant nor definite. With this object, viewed from the northwest horizon to the southeast horizon in approximately one to two minutes. The object moved in a straight line and performed no maneuvers. This object disappeared almost abruptly three times while in transit. This disappearance and reappearance did not occur in a regular fashion or coincide time interval. The disappearance occurred each time as though a 10-15 second interval. The object did not fade away and [redacted] does not believe the object moved behind anything during the times it was not observable. After the third disappearance, the object was lost to view. During the time observed, the object did not change its size or shape. The object showed no variation in color and did not appear to have light or dark areas. [Redacted] was not positive what the object was but appeared to be shining, of its own light and does not believe the light was reflected.

The object did not appear to be rotating. [Redacted] stated that at the time of sighting, the object was alone in the sky. [Redacted] was located on [redacted] in Montezuma Slough approximately one mile northwest of Denverton Landing in Solano County, California. Nearest identifying land marks are: Kirby Hines and Tule Lake. He was not advised the latitude and longitude of this residence [redacted]. He has since sold the house. The house is approximately 5 to 7 acres in area. The window was opened but screened. [Redacted] observed it was made with the naked eye, and no binoculars were used. [Redacted] advised he did not recall the exact time but stated it was between 0500 and 0700 hours.
