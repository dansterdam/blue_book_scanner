SUMMARY OF INFORMATION:

The following information has been received from Cpl [redacted], Alaska General Depot, Ft. Richardson, Alaska, stated that in the company of Sgt [redacted] he was returning from Anchorage approximately 2240 hrs, 16 Oct 51 and headed towards Ft. Richardson. A [redacted] approached from the left in the sky, and at first appeared to be an airplane, but after closer observation, it seemed more like a star. The object was traveling from the left of the moon to the right of the moon, and was bluish-white in color. The object was observed for approximately three or four minutes. On the morning of 17 Oct 51, Ford was talking with [redacted], Office Manager, Alaska Freight Lines, Anchorage, and he mentioned the incident, where upon [redacted] suggested that someone else might have seen the object and reported the fact to the newspaper. In the company of Strock, Ford visited the office of the Anchorage Daily News, and they were informed that no similar objects had been reported. (2-6)

The following information has been received from Sgt [redacted], Alaska General Depot, Ft. Richardson: stated that on 16 Oct 1951, he and Cpl [redacted] were returning to Ft. Richardson from Anchorage, Alaska, and at approximately 2225 hours, Cpl [redacted] exclaimed, "what is that". Looking in the direction that Ford was pointing, he saw an object in the sky. This object was then, to be seen as a first magnitude star. After watching, it came into plain view. It looked more like a star. The object was moving from the North towards Anchorage. The object did not move in a glide or steady pace, but it would stop intermittently, and as it stopped, it would blink. For a period of five seconds. The object was bluish-white in color, and Harman was unable to estimate the size of the object or its altitude. (2-6)

NOTE: Sgt [redacted] seemed amazed that he was contacted regarding this incident, as he said that he had not mentioned sighting the object to anyone.
