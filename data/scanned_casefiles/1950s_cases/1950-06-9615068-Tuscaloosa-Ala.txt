SUMMARY OF INFORMATION

UNCLASSIFIED                                                                   23 June 1950

CIC Field Area Office #4, Birmingham, Alabama

Subject: Unconventional Object in Air.

At approximately 1500 hours, 23 June 1950, a telephone call was received from the Editor, Tuscaloosa Sun, Tuscaloosa, Alabama. He stated that at about 1300 hours, 23 June 1950, 20 to 30 people saw, over the outskirts of Tuscaloosa, Ala., what they described as a flaming cross, red in color, moving slowly across the sky at an altitude of about 5,000 feet. It was visible for about 30 minutes, and then disappeared into a thundershed cloud, which had come up over the object.
