SUBJECT: UNCONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT

TO:     Commanding General
        Air Materiel Command
        Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
        Dayton, Ohio
        ATTN: MCIS

1. This District Office is in receipt of a letter from Mr. Paul J. Sato, Special Agent in Charge, Federal Bureau of Investigation at Albany, New York, which is quoted for your information:

"Miss [redacted] of [redacted] telephone [redacted] advised this office on July 14, 1951, that she and members of her family had observed two silver objects in the sky, appearing to be constant of Rensselaer, New York, at approximately 2:00 PM on this date.

"Miss [redacted] stated that they had maneuvered in the sky for approximately five minutes, and come down quite low, and then disappeared high into the sky. She could not describe these objects, other than that they did not appear to be airplanes."

2. No investigative action is contemplated by this District Office, and this information is forwarded to you for any action you deem advisable.

3. Your attention is invited to the provisions of paragraph 42, AFR 205-8, dated 20 January 1950.
