TO: Commander
Air Technical Intelligence Center (ATIC)
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio

1. On 18 October 1958, Mr. [redacted] who is identified above telephoned this District Office. He advised that at 2335 hours on 17 October 1958 he noted a round object as large as the head of a pin and blood red in color. This object was about 500 feet above the ground and appeared to be on the New Jersey side of the Hudson River. The object remained in sight about 5 minutes. The weather was overcast but visibility was good. He stated he has read a great deal concerning flying saucers and asked "why the government doesn't tell the people all about them".

2. [redacted] did not have additional information to offer and additional investigation will not be conducted by this District Office.

3. This investigation comes within the purview of paragraph 4c, AFR 205-8, 20 January 1959.

4. This letter is classified [CONFIDENTIAL] pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 30c (2)(e), AFR 205-1 because it contains investigative information which requires protection against unauthorized disclosure.
