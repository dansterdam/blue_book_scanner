1. SYNOPSIS: Interviewed on 20 April 1955, stated that on 27 April 1955 he had sighted an unidentified flying object
while on duty as a member of Ground Observer Corps and unidentified flying object described
as a bright light, varying in color and shape. Report from U. S.
Weather Bureau concerning weather on 27 April 1955 set forth.

2. DETAILS:

a. On 28 April 1955, was interviewed
by Special Agent JOHN G. HOFFMAN at his place of employment, the Police
Corporation, 4700 Wissahickon Avenue, Philadelphia, Pa. He stated
that while on duty as a member of the Ground Observer Corps on the preceding
evening, he had sighted an unidentified flying object.

b. The object first sighted was somewhat elongated (cigar-shaped) and for brief
intervals appeared elongated (cigar-shaped) and once appeared in the shape
of a boomerang. According to the observer, the object was between the size
of a dime and quarter when first sighted and when last seen about the size
of a pinhead and appeared like a distant star. With reference to color,
PATTERSON advised that the object first appeared as a brilliant white spot
and alternated to red and green in that order. The observer could not
could distinguish no features or details and could notice no tail, trail,
or exhaust.
