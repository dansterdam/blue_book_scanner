AETOI 1-52 Y/T A/C    PAGE 2    OF 2    PAGES

1. On 17 April 1952, the was driving on U.S. Highway Number 28 of Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan, at approximately 1500 hours on that date and at a point approximately eight (8) miles distant of Sault Ste. Marie, he observed a round, pink, stationary object at an altitude of five thousand (5000) feet to eight thousand (8000) feet approximately eight (8) miles to the north. The object appeared to have a diameter equal to that of a B-29 type aircraft or larger.

Further states that he continued driving but glanced from his car window from time to time in an attempt to determine the size, shape, color, distance, height and route of travel of the object so that he could report it to the authorities at Camp Tuscola.

2. Rumors have spread to a small number of people in Sault Ste Marie but investigation revealed that no other person or persons had seen the phenomenon.

3. The investigator, Captain Woodman, reports that the sky was clear with the temperature at fifty (50) degrees.

COMMENTS of Preparing Officer:

1. The Preparing Officer is of the opinion that the object sighted could be any one (1) of the following:

a. Weather balloon
b. Kite
c. Friendly aircraft
d. Sun reflected from distant cloud
e. Sun reflected from Lake Superior
f. Sun reflected from mist formed over St. Mary's River Falls at Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan.
