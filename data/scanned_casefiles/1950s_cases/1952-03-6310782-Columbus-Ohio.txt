SUMMARY: (Give below brief synopsis of incident. Give location in brief narrative form. State date, time, exact location, names of personnel, aircraft, etc.)

On 13 March 1952, a report was received from Mr. Harry Franzen of the Columbus Citizen newspaper in Columbus, Ohio, telephone AD 1111, that an unidentified aerial object was sighted northeast of Columbus between 1835 and 1845 on 12 March 1952.

The object was described as being a long shiny and wingless object, tapering from nose to tail with a pinkish red flame from the rear. It was traveling SW and made a circle north.

Lt. [redacted] went to Columbus on 13 March 1952 to interview the observers.

At North American, Inc. [redacted] was contacted prior to seeing Mr. [redacted] for plant protection, was contacted and had immediately recognized it as a North American B-45 from lockborne AFB Flying at an altitude of approximately 28000 to 30000 feet. The vapor trail was struck by the rays of the sun, which was setting at the time, giving off an orange or reddish color. Mr. [redacted] had the following observations to corroborate his story:
