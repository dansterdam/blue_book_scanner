11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
Round, white object was observed through a four power telescope shifting back and forth, rising and moving in the horizon. Observer stated that the object was at 10,000 ft and other details were in disagreement. Altitude was 10,000 ft, 11.1 altitude balloons were reported in vicinity of the sighting. Balloons were at a heading of 5 deg. "This alone were 10 deg at 10,000 ft and 350 deg at 20,000 ft."

General Remarks:
The four power scope used was a rifle scope. One observer reported the object was glowing and lighted. No interceptive data was attempted, no physical evidence was produced, no photographs or sketches were submitted. However, with altitude balloons were reported in the general vicinity of the sighting. Seattle NAS WH/Dr Radliffe reported that at 1830EST a balloon which they were tracking was on a balloon to station bearing was 05°, a class B site. At 1900EST the balloon to station bearing was 60°, and at 1920PST the balloon to station bearing was 56°, still a class B site. Several aircraft were flying locally at the time of sighting but none were reported at high altitude.
