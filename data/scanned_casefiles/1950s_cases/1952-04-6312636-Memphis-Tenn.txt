1. The Maxwell Flight Service Center, Maxwell AFB, Alabama, received a report
from Operations, U. S. Naval Air Station, Memphis, Tennessee, that U. S. Naval
and S. [redacted] had sighted an unidentified flying object while over the
U. S. [redacted] Tennessee area at approximately 1340, 14 April 1952. The sighting
was 1340, 14 April 1952.

2. No sketch or photograph of the object is available. Other details concerning
the object sighted are:

a. Number:                   One.
b. Shape:                    Inverted Bowl with slots running vertically from
                             top to bottom.
c. Size:                     Approximately 3 feet long and 1 foot high.
d. Color:                    Bright.
e. Speed:                    Rapid pace.
f. Maneuverability:          Not maneuvering.
g. Heading:                  300 degrees.
h. Altitude:                 2000 feet.
i. Sound:                    Not audible.
j. Exhaust trail:            None, glowing.

3. The two naval officers were flying in a heading of 13 degrees at 2000 feet,
45 to 60 seconds.

4. The U. S. Weather Bureau, Memphis, Tennessee, reported the weather at the
time of the sighting as 4200 feet overcast with visibility of 15 miles.
