3. Phenomenon detected by Radar set similar to AN/APQ-11 w/AIO installed on nose of B-36 equipment flight test aircraft. Phenomenon was detected at same relative position by AN/APS-23. Another is energized when the radar set looks on a target, and another is energized when the Mitchell camera was running. The Mitchell camera was mounted on the nose turret, and boresighted to the tracking line of the AN/APQ-11 antenna. The turret is elevated to the antenna, and follows in azimuth and elevation. Although the light on the O-15 film indicated that the Mitchell camera was running, nothing visually distinctive could be found on the film.

4. Phenomenon observed 10 miles East of Dallas, Texas, to 80 miles Northeast of Dallas North and South heading at 40,000 ft., and descends to 15,000 ft. in the area surrounding Lake Dallas. Phenomenon was no longer detected below altitude of 15,000 ft. Phenomenon was tracked at different azimuth locations plus or minus 300 and minus 50 to minus 15 evaluation. Although no intensive visual search was made phenomenon was not seen by the pilot or any crew member.

The phenomenon was present during the period of time when the B-36 fighters were making simulated attacks against the B-36. It was determined later that the interior performance from the AN/APQ-11 was obviously due, at least partially, to the same type of phenomenon that was detected and tracked for 30 minutes after the fighters left the area to return to their base (Bergstrom AFB).

The AN/APQ-11 operator's signed statement (Incl. 3) and the AN/APS-23 operator's signed statement (Incl. 4) give further details.

5. Both radar operators have been operating and flight testing their respective equipment for several years. Radar set AN/APQ-11 was instrumented with an O-15 camera (described in 3 above). The O-15 film was not clear, but does indicate presence and trackability of phenomenon.
