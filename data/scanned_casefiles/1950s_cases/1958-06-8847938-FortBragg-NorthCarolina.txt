1. DATE
20 Jun 58

2. DATE-TIME GROUP
Lost 21/0.052

3. LOCATION
Fort Bragg, North Carolina

4. PHOTOS
Yes
No

5. LENGTH OF OBSERVATION
10 mins

6. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Circular obj, size of full moon, light silvery, lower section surrounded by a thin greenish haze. At first obj was motionless, then began slight oscillating motion & increased to great speed & disappeared. Observed at 35 deg elevation.

7. TYPE OF OBSERVATION
Military

8. NUMBER OF OBJECTS
one

9. COURSE
North

10. COMMENTS
Possibly retraction of moon, with haze giving strong appearance. There are no first mag stars in the general area reported at time of night. 35 deg above horizon also indicates refraction or mirage are present. Winds are from the West ruling out a wind-blown obj. Most plausible explanation is a light reflection of some sort, however there is insufficient data to substantiate this.

11. CONCLUSIONS
Possibly Balloon
Possibly Aircraft
Insufficient Data for Evaluation

APR 200-5, 5 February 1958

SUMMARY:

An Unidentified Flying Object was observed between 0405/2 and 0415/2 21 June 1958. It appeared as a circular object of light silver color with a thin greenish haze surrounding the bottom of the object. It was at first motionless then moved at great speed on a course of 360 deg until it faded from sight. Weather at the time of the eight was good visibility.

It appears at this time that a phenomenon occurred.
