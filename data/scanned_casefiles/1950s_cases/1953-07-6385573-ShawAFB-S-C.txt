1. Two circular shaped objects of indistinguishable size were observed. One of the objects was larger than the other two, with the proportion of a 10 to 6 inches in size. The objects were at a 50 cent coin, two larger objects, located in a very bright intense light, being mistaken as first for an aircraft landing lights. A streak of light, similar to a vapor trail, appeared sporadically. The two smaller objects, one appearing directly on the 11 o'clock area and the other directly below. The streak and the two smaller objects appeared to be stationary and were within 20 degrees.

2. The large object was first observed at approximately 0200 hrs, 2 July 1953 and was observed for two hours. The other objects appeared at approximately 0200 and 0250.

3. The observations were made visually from the ground.

4. The observers were located one quarter mile north of Shaw AFB, S. C., 330 58' N 80° 29' W. The large object first appeared at tree top height, and was approximately 150 to 200 feet in elevation, with altitude unknown. (Observers states objects appeared to be in base leg of landing pattern with altitude unknown.)
