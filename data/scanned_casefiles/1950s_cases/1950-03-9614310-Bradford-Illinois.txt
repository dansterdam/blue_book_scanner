AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

USA USA-MTRS.

Unidentified Flying Objects

11 April 1950

A. G. Love, 1st Lt, USAF

Report contains information on seven alleged sightings of unidentified flying objects forwarded by various EATS Flight Service Center units. The sightings were all in the US and occurred during March 1950.

APPROVED:
JOSEPH G. BAUER
Lt Colonel, USAF
Chief, Intelligence Division

At 0850c on 18 March 1950 a flying object was observed over Bradford, Illinois, by the pilot of aircraft N5052. The name, address, and occupation of the witness are unknown, but the aircraft owner is reported to be Hubbinger Company, Keokuk, Iowa.

The pilot reported the sighting through the Burlington (Iowa) radio and the information was relayed to the Wright-Patterson Flight Service Center at 10830c, 18 March 1950, through the Chicago Air Route Traffic Control Center.

No photographs or sketches of the object were received by the Wright-Patterson Flight Service Center, but the pilot declared the object to be shaped like a saucer. The object's speed was estimated at 200 miles per hour at an altitude of 8,000 feet. No information could be obtained regarding the size, color, maneuverability, sound, or other details of the flying object.

Comment of Wright-Patterson Flight Service Center: The weather at Bradford, Illinois, on 18 March 1950, was: Estimated 25,000 feet broken clouds, 12,000 feet scattered clouds, 12 miles visibility.
