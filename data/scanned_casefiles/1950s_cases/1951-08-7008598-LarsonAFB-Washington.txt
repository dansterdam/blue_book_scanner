Appendix III

LARSON AFB, WASHINGTON - 26 August 1951

On 26 August 1951 at 0823 25", an unidentified flying object was detected by an AN/GRD-4 and AN/GRD-1 radar sets. The object was tracked continuously for a period of six minutes and made a three ground course of 090 mgh. The object was on a course of 240°, 30 mph, and altitude between 11,200 feet was obtained. The observer was questionable due to brief length of time the object was detected.

The F-86 aircraft were scrambled, but radar contact with the object was lost before the aircraft were airborne. A visual search was conducted from 17,000 to 25,000 feet with negative results.

The operator of the radar set, an air force sergeant, is considered to be an expert operator.

Weather

Weather conditions at the time of sighting were not favorable for anomalous "forward" propagation.

Status of Investigation

Review of this incident by the Electronics section of ATIC concludes that the return was possibly due to interference. This was concluded because of the apparent path of the object, directly across the station, and in fact that the target was observed on only the low beam of the AN/GRD-1 radar set.
