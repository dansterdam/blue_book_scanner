UNIDENTIFIED OBJECT CITED OVER
ORANGEBURG, SOUTH CAROLINA

The ATLANTA JOURNAL, 10 March 1950, carried the following article:

"ORANGEBURG, S. C., March 10 - Scores of Orangeburg residents added to the growing number of flying saucer reports with the description of a disk which hovered over the city for 15 minutes, then disappeared leaving a vapor trail.

"Six members of the staff of the ORANGEBURG TIMES and DEMOCRAT said they saw the disk Thursday night after Mrs. Donald Law, wife of the city editor, told them about it. They said it was about the size and color of a new moon, 'only brighter'.

"Publisher [redacted] said the disk appeared to turn slowly in the air, from vertical to parallel with the horizon and then vertical again, before disappearing in the western sky. [Redacted], a composing room employee, said he and four other persons saw the disk while driving on a highway 15 miles out of Orangeburg.

"Another flying saucer was reported Thursday by residents of Van Nuys, Calif., who told police they saw a bright disk about 300 feet in the air. All witnesses said they were sure it was not an aircraft of any known variety.

"Composer [redacted] and actor [redacted] and his wife said they studied the disk through a telescope. [Redacted] said it appeared to be 50 feet in diameter.

"At the same time Mexican and U. S. Government officials in Mexico City denied a report that a flying saucer had crashed near the capital with a 23-inch man aboard.

"However, the Mexican government newspaper, EL NACIONAL, quoted a famous 'reputatable astronomer' as saying the numerous disks reported over Mexico 'carry visitors from Mars'.

"The unidentified Mexican scientist wrote that the manner of flight and proportions of the disks it obvious that they carry another world. He said his claim would be confirmed in the next future 'when one of the disks is chased to earth by some military plane.'"

Flying Saucers Back!
Seen Over Columbia

By CHARLES SANDERS
Flying saucers made a vigorous and hardy appearance in the bright light in America. One even came equipped with a midget pilot.

In South Carolina, more and more citizens from various sections reported spotting the bright, silvery object floating across the sky about 6:30 P.M. yesterday.

In Columbia, Mrs. John C. Brown and her husband and daughter saw the object while they were en route to Columbia from Dentsville. Mrs. Brown said the object appeared like a silver sunset, with a wide streak, a section like a fine thread, and another wide arc.

Sam Oliver and Bob Gates, two residents of the Forest Hills area also reported seeing the "saucers". The pair said the disc appeared to be over the Palis Grounds about 5:15 p.m. They described it narrowed down to a point.

"I don't know how many miles long it was," Mr. Oliver said. "I thought it was a plane sky-writing, but it didn't move."
