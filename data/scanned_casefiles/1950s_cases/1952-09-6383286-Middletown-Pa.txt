ON 13SEPT 1952 AT 0002Z OBSERVERS FROM DAUPHIN AND ADJACENT COUNTIES SIGHTED OBJECT IN SKY. NUMBER OF BASE EMPLOYEES SIGHTED SAME OBJECT AND REPORTED IT TO BE A METEOR TRAVELLING FROM SOUTHEAST TO NORTHWEST. APPARENT SIZE WAS APPROXIMATELY 25 INCHES IN DIAMETER NEAR GROUND. LAST SIGHTED AT 40-10N 76-40W APPEARING TO GO BELOW HILLS TO WEST OF FIELD ACROSS SUSQUEHANNA RIVER. LIGHT WAS BLUEISH WHITE TURNING TO YELLOW. FAST RATE OF SPEED. NO SOUND. SLIGHT TAIL SAID BY OBSERVERS TO BE MERELY DUE TO GREAT RATE OF SPEED.

WEATHER AT TIME OF SIGHTING CLEAR WITH 7 MILES VISIBILITY SURFACE WINDS CALM 2000 FT 260 DEGREES AT 7 KNOTS. NO AIR TRAFFIC IN AREA AT TIME. ALL OBSERVERS MAKING REPORTS WERE ON GROUND. OBSERVERS APPEAR TO BE RELIABLE. AT LEAST TWO ARE BASE GUARDS.
