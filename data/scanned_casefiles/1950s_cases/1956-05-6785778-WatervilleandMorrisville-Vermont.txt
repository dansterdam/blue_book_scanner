SUPPLEMENTARY INVESTIGATIVE REPORTS:

1. A telephone call to the Burlington Airport control tower was made to determine if there were any aircraft in the area on the dates and times of sightings. Tower gave a negative report.

2. Phone call was made to the Burlington weather station. No balloons releases were made at the time of sightings or immediately prior to the time of sightings. Personnel at the station informed the investigator that the winds at altitude were from the NW on both dates, and it was possible for balloons to travel from the north central part of the states into the area.

3. A check was made with the 764th AC&W Squadron, St. Albans, Vermont. Intelligence personnel informed the investigator or several projects being conducted involving the use of balloons. Investigator was shown track logs made on the dates of the sightings, but no entries were made which would identify the objects sighted.

COMMENTS of Preparing Officer:

1. It is the opinion of the Investigator, from facts obtained, that sightings were caused by a cluster of weather balloons, with telemetering instruments attached. These balloons could possibly be used in conjunction with OPERATION SKYHOOK, as the prevailing winds would cause them to be blown over the area where they were sighted. The weather station at Burlington, Vermont has seen similar sightings within the past eight (8) months and has determined them to be weather balloons.

COMMENTS of Approving Officer:

Concur with the opinion of the Preparing Officer that the sightings described in this report were caused by clusters of weather balloons. Telemetering instruments attached to the suspension train of each balloon could account for reports of "gondolas" beneath the objects.
