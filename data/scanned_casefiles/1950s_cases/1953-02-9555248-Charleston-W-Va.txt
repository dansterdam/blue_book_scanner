AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

FROM: 783rd AC&W Sqdn, Char. W. Va.    REPORT NO: R-11-53    PAGE 2 OF 2 PAGES

The following report is prepared in accordance with par 70 AFR 200-5 dtd 29 April 1952.

1. Object in question was oblong in shape, approximately 2 miles in width and 7 miles in length. Preceding the object and lying in a straight line along the path of movement was a faint "String" approximately 30 miles in length. The object moved at a constant rate of speed of 70 KPH and moved a constant, straight path of 070 degrees.

Pickup was established at 310 degrees at 220 miles; how much further out it tracked is unknown at this station due to normal equipment coverage.

Fade-out occurred in like appearance to the gaining or losing of altitude of a target out of the coverage lobe pattern of this station.

2. Object was first detected at 2030 Zebra with the four "corners" laying in SL 5095 - EL 4026 - FL 0520 - FL 1135. Fade was at JM 1611, time 2145 Zebra; shape was fused was slightly elongated; approximate 1 mile wide and 3 miles long with the disappearance of the "string" at start of fade.

3. Observation was made on the AN/FPS-3 search radar scopes using lower beam long range setting.

4. Observation was made from P-43 located 6 miles from Charleston, West Virginia on a heading of 350 degrees. Pickup was 310 degrees at 210 miles. Fade was 359 degrees at 165 miles. Speed 70 knots, course 070 degrees.

5. Object was observed throughout entire life by Operations Officer, Radar Maintenance Officer, both NORAD's and the entire shift. Reliability of this group is considered excellent; experienced very broad in the field of radar.

6. Weather appeared to be clear. Winds aloft unknown.

7. Unknown.

8. Scope photographs were taken at 5 minute intervals.

9. Interception action was taken by P-42.

10. There was no air traffic in that general area to our knowledge.
