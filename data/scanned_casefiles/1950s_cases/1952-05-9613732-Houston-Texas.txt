Statement of facts received from interrogation of Capt. J.G. Bourging, AC44-14670, and Capt. E.G. Sherry, USAF. Both men are on active duty with 3609th New Wing Sq. (Mil) Ellington AFB, Houston, Texas, and are at present pursuing a course in Aerial Reconnaissance School Program.

On the evening of the 20th of May, 1952, the two officers accompanied by Mrs. E.G. Sherry, wife of the observer, were stationed at home of Capt. J.G. Bourging at 7416 Stella Drive, Houston, Texas. They had completed supper practices and were engaged in watching the television when all suddenly saw a bright object in the sky. The object was first sighted through the living room window, then (through) the side door facing west. The object was traveling at a high rate of speed, then slowing down to almost a stop, then moving in a zigzag pattern. The point of origin was from the southwest, moving toward the northeast. Share was no local aircraft activity. The object was visible to all three observers. Physical evidence sought as photographs to work by sighting. There was no indication of altitude of objects. The skies were clear with wind at surface light, generally 10 knots per hour. The surface air was warm, between 70° and 75°.
