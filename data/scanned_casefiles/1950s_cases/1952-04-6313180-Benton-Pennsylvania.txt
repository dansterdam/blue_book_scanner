6950TH AIRCRAFT CONTROL AND WARNING SQUADRON
Benton, Pennsylvania

17 June 1952

SUBJECT: Reporting of Information on Unconventional Aircraft

TO: COMMANDING GENERAL
Air Material Command
Wright-Patterson AFB
Dayton, Ohio

ATTN: MCIS

1. In compliance with ADC LETTER 200-1, dated 11 April 1952 the following report is submitted:

a. The target appeared to be a normal flight of 3 aircraft flying in formation showing dark a IFF (code 2). This station does not have a IFF. The track showed a low van average of about 400 knots and the altitude was 12,000 feet. This track appeared intentional, but clear when sighting. They showed as 3 solid vertical lines on the PPI and faded quickly off visual. This track was similar to one referred to in letter of 19 April 1952 concern. Ser. 4-17 Serial number 9513, Track 1-154-4, with exception of speed. At the time of this occurrence aircraft Track 1-50 was a taking off in a normal fashion.

b. The target was picked up at 1550Z 17 April 1952 and appeared on our PPI scope for a period of 22 minutes.

c. Ground Electronics order (GE/CRO-63)

d. The station is located at Latitude 41° - 21' - 25.3" North, Longitude 76° - 11' - 36.9" West. The target was located at 249° 05' nautical miles from the station. It was made in a southerly direction at approximately 12,000 feet.

e. Captain [REDACTED] observed the track on a PPI scope.

f. Unknown

g. A cold front was passing over the station area, causing temperature inversion.

h. Negative
