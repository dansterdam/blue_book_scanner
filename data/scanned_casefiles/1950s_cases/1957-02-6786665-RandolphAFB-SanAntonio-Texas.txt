1. Description of the object:
   a. Round.
   b. Three of the observers, S/Sgt Simpson, A/2C Bowman, and A/2C Durham,
      thought it was the size of a pea, while another observer, A/1C Watland thought it
      was the size of a basketball.
   c. All observers state that it was a bright, white light.
   d. There was only one object.
   e. N/A
   f. N/A

   g. Two observers, S/Sgt Simpson and A/2C Bowman, say there was no trail
      or tail. One observer, A/2C Durham, states there was a small reddish-green trail
      directly behind the object. One observer, A/1C Watland, saw a long trail that
      faded out about one-half to three-fourth of a mile behind the object.

   h. No sound was discernible.
      1. None.

2. Description of course of object:
   a. The two observers that saw the object first, A/2C Bowman and A/1C
      Watland, were watching three F-84's making a landing.
   b. Angle of elevation was 30 degrees and the azimuth was 320 degrees when
      first observed.
   c. Angle of elevation was 30 degrees and the azimuth was 140 degrees upon
      disappearance.
   d. Straight and level flight path with no maneuver.
   e. The object gradually disappeared into the distance.
   f. Estimated at 5-10 seconds from first sighting to last observance.

3. Manner of observations:
   a. Ground - visual by observers, Simpson, Watland, Bowman and Durham.
      Air - visual by Major Brandt and Captain Church.
   b. No optical aids were utilized.
   c. The air-visual sighting was made from a C-47, #24073, that was flying
      2300 feet to 2500 feet on a heading of 170 degrees, at a speed of 140 knots. The
      aircraft was from Randolph Air Base, Texas.
