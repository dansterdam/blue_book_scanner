COUNTRY: United States

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SUBJECT: (UNCLASSIFIED) Unidentified Flying Objects Sighting (Shark Tail, NYXAAA)
AREA REPORTED ON: Middletown Air Material Area FROM (Agency): Intelligence Office, Cleveland Air Materiel Area, Middletown, Pa.
DATE OF REPORT: 22 Jul. 1952 DATE OF INFORMATION: 20 Jul. 1952 SOURCE: William Sharman
REFERENCES: (Confirm name, address, previous report, etc. as applicable)

SUMMARY: (Enter entire summary of report. Give significance in final one-sentence paragraph. List features at lower left. Begin list of report on AF Form 118- Part II)

At 0200 EST, 20 June 1952, Sgt. Francis Young, a ground observer, of the Harrisburg, Wilkes Barre, York area, reported observing unidentified flying objects.

Sgt. Young reported that strange lighted objects were observed south of his station at North Street in Harrisburg, at intervals of nine to twelve minutes. Some of these objects were reported to be in a formation in trail. The objects were clearly visible to Sgt. Young, however no exhaust or sound was noted. They were estimated to be at a very high altitude.

Ceiling and visibility at the time of observation were unlimited.

No aircraft were reported in the vicinity at the time.

Sgt. Young's observation was verified by another unidentified air man.

A full report of this incident is being forwarded by the Filter Center through the 28th Air Division, its higher Headquarters. This report is being submitted by this Headquarters only because we transmit the original teletype.
