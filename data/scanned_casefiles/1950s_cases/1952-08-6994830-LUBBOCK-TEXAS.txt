SUBJECT: PHENOMENA

Information contained in this report was obtained from Lubbock, Texas. Interview was conducted by Lieutenant John M. Marillo, acting Wing Intelligence Officer.

1. On 2 August 1952 from 0857 hours to 0903 hours, one (1) unidentified flying object was sighted. The object sighted was spherical in shape with no apparent size or motion. Estimate of object to be approximately twenty-five (25) inches. Some estimate the size of object to be approximately one (1) foot.

2. The object then sighted was hanging in mid air. The only movement was approximately straight up and down. Informant stated for (6) minutes and object was stationary, then it disappeared into a cloud bank. He timed object sixteen (16) minutes. After object started to move until it disappeared and took sixteen (16) seconds. There was no connection made with object.

3. Object was viewed with the naked eye. No optical or electronic equipment used.

4. Observation took place at 2934N 102W 30m in the city of Lubbock, Texas. Object appeared approximately five (5) miles from east point of observation at approximately 10,000 feet.

5. Information concerning the reliability and experience of the observer indicates that he is a reliable individual. The informant was with B-25 aircraft in the general area at time of sighting. Also a check was made at Municipal Airport and there were L-5 type aircraft in area at time of sighting.

6. The weather conditions at 0857 hours on 2 August 1952 were: temperature 78.1 degrees, visibility 15 miles with scattered clouds at 20,000 feet.

7. There could be no definite meteorological activity or conditions to which this sighting could be attributed.

8. Physical evidence such as negatives, photographs, etc. were nonexistent.

9. There was no interception or identification action taken.

10. Aircraft from Reese AFB and Municipal Airport of Lubbock were known to be in general area at time of sighting.

COMMENTS OF Preparing and Observing Officer:
Mr. [REDACTED] is well educated and highly intelligent individual. He was very sincere in his efforts to cooperate and assist this organization.
