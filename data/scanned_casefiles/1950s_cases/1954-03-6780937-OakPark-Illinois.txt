APR 200-2, 26 Aug. 52

SUMMARY:

On 17 March 1954, at 0122, the following report was received from G.O.Cs.,
Detachment 2, Chicago, Illinois:

A round object, resembling a silver fire ball, was visually sighted, approximately at GR 0857 in the vicinity of Oak Park, Illinois. Object was traveling North to South, at a very high altitude, giving no actions or characteristics, but object was estimated to be at a very high altitude, disappearing shortly after being sighted. Observed was a light and 0050c.

The weather conditions at 0502 were as follows: Sky Conditions: 250, Scattered; Visibility: 7 miles; Temperature: 44°; Dew Point: 20°; Winds were West Northwest at 6 knots.

Radar at the 755th ACEW Squadron, Williams Bay, Wisconsin was unable to pick up this object. Interpretation of identification was not taken.
