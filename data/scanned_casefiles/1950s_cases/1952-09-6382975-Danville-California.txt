DETAILS:

1. This investigation opened at the direction of the District Commander, predicted upon information furnished by [redacted], Security Division, Los Alamos Field Office, Atomic Energy Commission, Los Alamos, New Mexico, concerning the observation of an unidentified aerial object by [redacted]

2. Inspector, U.S. Atomic Energy Protective Force, Los Alamos, New Mexico, reported on 5 September 1952, that he had sighted an unidentified aerial object, round in color, at 2137 hours on 5 September 1952, from his assigned station #320, Los Alamos, New Mexico. Confirming the object approached his position from the North proceeding downward to an elevation of about 15° above the horizon. As reported the object was visible to him for approximately three (3) seconds, disappearing into the South. No evidence of trail or exhaust reported by him. Remarks were "not a conventional aircraft, too fast. It dived in a straight line, not an arc free from falling object."
