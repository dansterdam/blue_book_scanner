18 August 1952

STATEMENT OF OBSERVER

I, William F. Van Cleemputte, 1st Lieutenant USAF AC 20C1828, 1146th Fighter-Bomber Group, make the following statement concerning a certain unidentified object observed by me on 11 August 1952:

My observation took place at 1045 hours PT on the date in question. At the time I was driving southeast on Main Street on George AFB.

The bright object appeared almost directly in my line of travel at about 20,000 feet, and seemingly well away over the range of mountains to the west and southwest of the station. It did not seem to have any noticeable motion.

I had intention of going through the main gate and parking out of traffic where I could better observe the object with field glasses. Before I could get to the gate, the object had disappeared.

The object was visible to me for 3 or 4 minutes. It is possible that the object was round but the brightness could have given it that illusion.

[Signed]
WILLIAM F. VAN CLEEMPUTTE
1st Lieutenant, USAF

18 August 1952

STATEMENT OF OBSERVER

I, Amos F. Kies, Colonel, USAF 4597A, 116th Fighter-Bomber Group, make the following statement concerning an unidentified aerial object observed by me on 11 August 1952:

I sighted the object while flying as number 2 man in a formation of F-51s. The time was 0800 hours PST on the date indicated.

The object was bright and shiny and appeared to be hovering or in a slight climb away from me. It was about 10 miles away at about 15 to 20,000 feet altitude.

I observed it for about a minute until I lost sight of it while making a turn in formation. I had just seen the object but did not see it again.

AMOS F. KIES
Colonel, USAF
