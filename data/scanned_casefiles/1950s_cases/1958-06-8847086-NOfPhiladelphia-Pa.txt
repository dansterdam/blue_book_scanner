b. Description of Course of Object:

(1) What first called the attention of observer to the object - related that he just happened to look to the horizon east, of his home and saw instant the object.

(2) Angle or elevation and azimuth of the object when first sighted - Approximately 30° above the horizon. E/S/

(3) Angle or elevation and azimuth of object upon disappearance - 45° above the horizon.

(4) Description of flight path and maneuvers of object - Then sighted, the object was approximately 30° above the horizon. The object reportedly climbed steadily in a straight course until it was approximately 90° above the horizon, at which point it hovered momentarily, then descended to approximately 45°. The ascent was made in approximately five (5) minutes and the descent in approximately three (3) minutes.

(5) How did the object move - The object moved in a straight course in a southerly direction for about three (3) minutes, then traveled west and upward until it went out of sight.

How long was the object visible - Approximately fifteen (15) minutes.

c. Manner of Observation:
(1) Ground-visual.
(2) No optical aids were used.

d. Time and date of Sighting:
(1) Between 081655Z and 081710Z June 1958. 16'55"= 11:55
(2) Day.

e. Location of Observer: Approximately 40° 16' north and 75° 07' West.
