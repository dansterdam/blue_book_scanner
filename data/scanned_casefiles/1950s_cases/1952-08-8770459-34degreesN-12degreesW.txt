TR-555-52

Unidentified Flashes on Surface of Sea
Western Atlantic Off Portrean Coast

3 September 1952

J. M. Penny, Major, USAF

Intelligence Officer, HQ CGTD
NATS, Kelly AFB, San Antonio, Texas

24 August 1952

6th AC 50, 17034 HQ, MATS, Brooklyn
Air Force Base, Alabama

1. At approximately 2330Z on 24 August 1952 while piloting a C-124 two a/c on a transport mission from Lajas Air Force Base, Mores to Nouasseur Air Base, French Morocco at an altitude of 7500' on a course heading of 118 degrees at 250 miles off coast of North Africa coordinates 34° 38' N - 12° 16' W, the pilot, Major Hugh L. Symes and Co-pilot, Captain Charles R. King, of the 6th Air Transport Group, MATS, observed a distinct greenish blue, fan shaped flame estimated to be 50 to 75 miles from the aircraft at an angle of one o'clock. Five minutes later a second flash was observed at the same location.

2. When the aircraft arrived in the area of the incident a thorough observation was continued to ascertain presence of surface vessels with no results. Area was free of any signs which would indicate fire or explosion of any type.

3. Aircraft continued to destination at Nouasseur and reported incident to MATS liaison officer on duty.

APPROVED:

J. M. PENNY
Major, USAF
Division Intelligence Officer
