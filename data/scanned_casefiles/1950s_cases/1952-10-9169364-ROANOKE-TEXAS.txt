AFSGCI                                                                5 November 1952

SUBJECT: Reported Sighting of Unidentified Aerial Object

TO:   Commanding General
       Fourth Army
       Fort Sam Houston, Texas
       ATTN: AC of S, G-2

1. Reference letter your Headquarters, AG 350.09 AKADE-I, dated 2 February 1951, Subject: Intelligence Activities.

2. In compliance with par 6a(5), above referenced ltr, the following report concerning sighting of unidentified aerial object is forwarded for your information:

   a. Date and Time Observed: 1618 hours, 26 October 1952.

   b. Location: US Highway 77
               (13 miles north of Ft Worth, Texas)

   c. Observers: 2 Women, 1 man in car.

   d. Description: Object appeared the size of a large dinner plate, Bright Silver; Light, initially sighted about 350 due east, 2 miles over open country, object traveled south, object was in sight 2 minutes, object turned and climbed like a top, object appeared to cover 500 in approximately 90 seconds object just seemed to fade out of sight.

   e. Action taken: Telephone check was made with Post Field Service, Carswell Flight Service, Fort Worth, Texas with negative results.

                                                        H. P. CLARKE JR.
                                                        Lt Col
                                                        AC of S, G-2

HEADQUARTERS
THE ARTILLERY CENTER
FORT SILL, OKLAHOMA
Office of the AC of S, G-2

ATCH: 3 November 1952

SUBJECT: Reported Sighting of Unidentified Aerial Object

TO: Commanding General
Fourth Army
Fort Sam Houston, Texas
ATTN: AC of S, G-2

1. Reference letter your Headquarters, AG350.09 AKADG-I, dated 2 February 1951, Subject: Intelligence Activities.

2. In compliance with par 6a(5), above referenced letter, the following report concerning sighting of unidentified aerial object is forwarded for your information:

a. Date and Time Observed: 1618 hours, 26 October 1952.
b. Location: 2 miles south of Roanoke, Texas on Highway 77.
(18 miles north Ft. Worth, Texas)

c. Observers: Driver's wife pointed out object and asked what it was.

d. Description: When first observed, object appeared cigar shaped and about 2/3 width of moon, very brilliant blue reflection, initially sighted about 300 above horizon, object was observed 300 from north, object appeared to be 3 miles from observer, object traveled straight up then went southerly losing some altitude then moving sou'h east, object was in sight for 3 minutes, object appeared to cover a 600 in approximately 1 minute and 20 seconds, object went out of sight to the South East.

e. Action taken: Telephone check was made with Post Field Airbase, and records were checked from Carswell Flight Service, Fort Worth, Texas with negative results.

[Signature]
H. P. CLARKE JR
1st Col
AC of S, G-2
