Luke AFB, Phoenix, Arizona                IR-4-52        PAGE    2    OF    2    PAGES

Major William R. Castorline, AC 016 209, Base Operations Officer, was contacted by telephone by 1st Lt. William J. Laidlaw, AO (Berne Glendale 9993) at 1040 on 5 April 1952, who reported a sighting.

U.S.S.F. for this area made a follow-up investigation and accumulated the following information:

1. Three flying objects were viewed by 1st. Lt. Wm. J. Laidlaw and 1st. Lt. R. (illegible name) at the above address.

2. 1st. Lt. Laidlaw and 1st. Lt. Stokes were interviewed and described the objects as large and circular. No wings, shape, or other identifying characteristics of conventional flying or other were visible. The color was a dull red, resembling aged aluminum.

3. The first object was sighted and disappeared. Immediately, 2 more were sighted traveling in the same direction as the first. The 3 objects formed a large "V" formation.

4. The approximate altitude was presumed to be over 40,000 feet, and the speed, about half the rate of a "falling rock". The direction of flight was northerly.

5. The weather conditions at the time of the sighting were reported as follows: Ceiling - 25,000; Visibility - 45 miles; Wind - calm.

6. Two F-86s landed at this base shortly after the reported sighting. The pilots reported that they had flown their last leg mission, Arizona (west) of Phoenix, and had flown no coverage of the area. Two miles west of Phoenix.
