10. BRIEF SUMMARY OF SIGHTING
Object was dark and cylindrical with short reddish tail. In a position approx 1 deg to 2 deg above and to the north of Vega. Traveling at a very slow speed compared to the speed of a meteor or falling star. It appeared to be a rocket in controlled flight. Disappeared about 4 deg elevation.

1. PRELIMINARY: Second Mate aboard S. S. HAWAII LOGGER reported sighting unidentified airborne object described as dark and cylindrical with short and reddish tail, similar to rocket in controlled flight; observed at 0214, 12 February 1951, two vessels at position 21°32'N, en 157°06'W. (Lash Naval District, Washington, D.C., Memorandum)

2. DETAILS: District Intelligence Officer, 14th Naval District, under date 08 February 1951, forwarded to this office (1) copy of communication to Fleet Intelligence Officer, Clarksdale, Enclosure: "Unidentified celestial object; report sighting of," herein reported in substance.

Movement Report Center, Hawaiian Sea Frontier, advised at 1040 on 24 January 1951 that 2nd Mate aboard S. S. HAWAII LOGGER, had reported sighting unidentified airborne object appearing to be a rocket in controlled flight. The vessel was on course 260° at position 21°32'N and 157°06'W at 0244 W. TAYLOR was on watch, standing in starboard wing on top bridge, when he first noted the airborne object.
