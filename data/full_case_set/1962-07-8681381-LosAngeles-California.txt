a. Description of Objects:
(1) She described the objects as being in the shape of a hish-wing.

(5) The objects displayed flashing lights as if the illumination rotated or revolved.
(6) The objects disappeared from view suddenly.

b. Description of Course of Objects:
(1) Her attention was called to the objects by the sound.
(2) The objects were headed west, in the flight path circling in a counterclockwise direction. At one time the objects hovered approximately 50 to 55 persons. In both instances, the objects disappeared from view disappearrently completely disapparendy from the same area they were previously.
(3) It was not possible to determine in what direction or manner the objects disappeared.

c. Manner of Observation:
(1) It was a round-the-clock observation with no optical aids used.
