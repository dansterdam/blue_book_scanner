DAILY WEATHER MAPS
WEEKLY SERIES MAY 5-11, 1969

The charts in this publication are a continuation of the charts furnished daily to the Weather Bureau public. Surface Weather Map presents shown as dashed lines, and are labeled with figures indicating the velocity of the wind in degrees Celsius. The arrows show the wind direction and speed at the surface. The Isobars shown are the 1000 millibar level. 

The Highest and Lowest Temperature chart presents the maximum and minimum values for the 24-hour period ending at 1200 a.m. EST. The names of the reporting points can be obtained from the Surface Weather Map. The maximum temperature is plotted above the station location, and the minimum temperature is plotted below this point.

The Precipitation Areas and Amounts chart indicates by means of shading the areas that had precipitation during the 24 hours ending at 7:30 a.m. EST. Amounts, in inches, to the nearest hundredths, are indicated at reporting stations.
