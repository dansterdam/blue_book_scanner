HEADQUARTERS
AIR MATERIEL COMMAND
WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE
Washington 25, D. C.

In Reply Refer To:
RCEA 79: APS 55 11 May 1949

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects

TO: Commanding General
Air Materiel Command
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton, Ohio
AFSPA 50(32)-3

1. Reference is made to letter from your headquarters, RCEA 79: APS 55, dated 9 March 1949, subject "Unidentified Flying Objects." This first indorsement thereto by this headquarters, dated 30 March 1949, is herewith acknowledged.

2. In this indorsement 173 cases were found to have been checked against routine weather-balloon ascents made by the Air Force, Navy and other agencies, and these cases were included.

3. It is recommended that the "Guide to Investigation of Unidentified Aerial Objects" contained in letter 352-59, Directorate of Flight Safety Research, dated 31 December 1948, should be modified so that vertical and horizontal motions are distinguished. It is also recommended that the criteria used in the "Guide" to determine whether or not an object is qualified as a "Radar Contact according to Radar Criteria" should include a statement of the type of radar set used.

FOR THE CHIEF, AIR MATERIEL COMMAND

/s/ W. A. Vest
W. A. VEST
Lt. Col., USAF
Adjutant General
