1. The following report concerning an unidentified flying object is submitted:
2. a. The observation of the unidentified flying object was made at approximately 1230 MST, 24 July 1952, at the 36th Air Force Base, El Paso, Texas.
b. The observation is recorded in the official Intelligence report of the 36th Air Force Base.
3. a. The information is presented in accordance with ATIC-N/CC-2 instructions concerning unidentified aerial objects. Portions of information on Unidentified Flying Objects have been deleted.
b. PRIGGS reported observing an unidentified flying object in the immediate vicinity of the 36th Air Force Base for approximately 1 to 2 minutes.
c. Object was observed visually and, according to the source, was a source of the opinion that the object had a metallic luster and was shaped like a saucer. The observer stated emphatically that the object was not a conventional aircraft of known type. 
d. Source was on the flight line opposite Base Operations at Biggs Air Force Base, Texas (31° 50' 50"N 106° 22' 45"W). The object was first observed to the East.
