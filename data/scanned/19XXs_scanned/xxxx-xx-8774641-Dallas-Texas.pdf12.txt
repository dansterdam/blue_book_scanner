SUBJECT: (Unclassified) Report of Information on Unidentified Flying Objects

TO: Director of Intelligence, D/SI
Headquarters USAF
ATTN: AFOIN-C/00
Washington 25, D.C.

1. In compliance with letter, Headquarters, USAF, AFOIN-C/00-R, dated 10 December 1951, the following information is reported to this headquarters by Mr. James Kelly, of the Dallas Radar Station, as forwarded.

a. Description: Two orange lights, moving at jet speed, no sound, one behind the other, traveling northwest and then southwest and after approximately 1 minute straightened out and continued toward the southeast approximately brighter than returned to its original glow. Altitude estimated at 1500 feet.

b. Time Sighted: 23:35, 27 August 1952.

c. Manner of Observation: Surface-to-air.

d. Location of Observer: W Long 96°45', N Lat 32°51', Gesl Transtel Drive, Dallas, Texas. Object first sighted approximately one-half mile to the west and 40° elevation.

e. Identifying Information on Observer: Mr. James Kelly, Technician Trainee at Gesl Telephone Company. Wife, Mrs. Kelly, works as telephone operator at Gesl's Dallas Home Office. Wife does not work. Average Intelligence.

f. Weather at Time of Observation: CIE 035/8/9/8

g. Other Activity or Condition: None reported.

h. Existence of any Physical Evidence: None reported. 

i. Interception or Identification Action Taken: None

j. Location of Air Traffic: Very much aircraft activity in area going in and out of Love Field, but the object sighted was no normal aircraft.
