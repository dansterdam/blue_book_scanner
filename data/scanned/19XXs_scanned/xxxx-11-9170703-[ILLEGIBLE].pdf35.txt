SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Unidentified Aerial Objects
Over Glendale, California,
24 November 1952

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. SYNOPSIS: At approximately 1548 hours, 24 November 1952, numerous unidentified aerial objects were observed over East Glendale, California, near the Grand Central Airport. The objects were described as spherical in shape, size unknown, emitting a light of their own, color dull gray aluminum, and were traveling from the northeast to a southwesterly direction at a very high rate of speed, approximately 2,000 feet on a course of southeast at approximately 200 miles per hour. The observations lasted for approximately 5 minutes, after which the objects changed from a spherical to a flattened or elliptical shape, disappearing in the area of the Verdugo Mountains to the northeast at a very high rate of speed.

2. DETAILS: Information pertinent to this matter was furnished by the informant to the Los Angeles office of the Federal Bureau of Investigations, and to this office by letter, the body of which is quoted below:

"At approximately 3:48 P.M. Monday, Nov. 24, 1952 at the intersection of San Fernando Road & Grandview Ave in East Glendale, California, I and four other people observed for about five minutes a formation of approximately 15 aerial objects that I can only describe as spherical and emitting a light gray aluminum in color. They were traveling in a southwest direction at a terrific speed, estimated to be approximately 2,000 feet altitude and 200 mph. After several minutes the objects seemed to change from a spherical to a flattened or elliptical shape and disappeared toward the Verdugo Mountains to the northeast at a very high rate of speed."
