HEADQUARTERS
4620 COMBAT OPERATIONS GROUP (SAC)
LARSON AIR FORCE BASE, WASHINGTON

BO
9 AUG 1954

Report of U.F.O. Sighting

to: Commanding Officer 
Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio, 46433

1. At approximately 1115 hours on 29 July 1954, a telephone call was received from Sgt. Clarence C. Chiles, 401st TACRON, WKAF [Unclear], Savannah, Georgia, who reported sighting of an Unidentified Flying Object in the area of Lake Chatuge. The object, observed and described by Sgt. Chiles and his co-pilot, Lt Colonel Gwendlyn W. Cornelius, was classified by them as an Unidentified Flying Object. The object was reported to be traveling in a westerly direction at a high rate of speed and disappeared from sight in a northwest direction.

2. A check with the Seattle Air Defense Sector, local weather stations, and local control towers disclosed that there was no activity being observed in the area.

3. In an interview with the Clayton County Sheriff, the following information was obtained: The object was sighted by Sgt. Chiles and Lt Colonel Cornelius at approximately 0230 hours on 29 July 1954. They claimed the object was a disc-shaped glowing craft, approximately 40 to 50 feet in diameter, and traveling at a high rate of speed. It was a bright reddish-orange in color and had no visible means of propulsion. As the object passed over their aircraft, they stated they felt a blast of hot air. They reported the object to be traveling at an altitude of approximately 5,000 feet. He stated that this was the only report of the sighting in this area on the morning of 29 July 1954. He stated that the object was highly maneuverable and that it must be pointed out that Sgt. Chiles and Lt Colonel Cornelius are considered to be credible and reliable observers.

4. In an interview with Mr. [Redacted], one of the witnesses to the sighting, the following information was received: During the sighting, the witnesses stated they were flying at an altitude of approximately 2,000 feet when the object first came into view. They stated the object was approximately a mile ahead of their aircraft and was traveling at a high rate of speed, estimated to be approximately 500 to 700 miles per hour. The first thing they noticed was a bright light coming toward them. As the object came closer, they could see it was disc-shaped and glowing a reddish-orange color. They stated the object appeared to be approximately 40 to 50 feet in diameter. As the object passed over their aircraft, they stated they felt a blast of hot air. The witnesses stated the object climbed rapidly and disappeared from sight within a matter of seconds. They estimated the sighting lasted approximately 10 to 15 seconds. Mr. [Redacted] stated that the attitude of the observers indicated that they were telling the truth and were not joking or making any false claims.
