APPENDIX H AFR NO. 80-17, UNAIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS
AIR FORCE REGULATIONS DEPARTMENT OF THE AIR FORCE
WASHINGTON, 8 November 1960 

UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS (UFO)
AFR 80-17, 26 September 1960, is changed as follows:
5. REPORTING AND DEVELOPMENT. The UFO program will be conducted under the auspices of the Air Force, with participation by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and the University of Colorado. This program calls for continued investigations, studies, and analysis of UFO information by specific scientific organizations. The University of Colorado, under a research agreement with the Air Force, will undertake a comprehensive study of the UFO phenomenon. This program will include:
a. Examination by the University of Colorado of special photographic and related evidence of UFO sightings.
b. The initiation of special field investigations whenever this is deemed necessary by the University of Colorado in the light of the evidence.
c. The utilization of the Condon Committee, a special advisory group for the UFO study composed of scientists from various disciplines, to assist in the evaluation of evidence and in the planning of investigations.
d. The preparation of a final comprehensive report on the University of Colorado's findings and conclusions concerning the UFO phenomenon.

5. Negative or Unexplainable Data. Whenever a UFO report is received which contains data that cannot be positively identified and explained, the report will be forwarded to the University of Colorado for detailed study and analysis. It will be the responsibility of the University of Colorado to conduct further investigations or analyses as required.

BY ORDER OF THE SECRETARY OF THE AIR FORCE

J. P. McCONNELL
General, U.S. Air Force
Chief of Staff
