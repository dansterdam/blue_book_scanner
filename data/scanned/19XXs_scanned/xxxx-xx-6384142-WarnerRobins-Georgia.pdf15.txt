a. Observers: 
b. Location: Warner Robins, Georgia

Wind - from 330 degrees at 5 knots
Ceiling - unlimited - 310 degrees K.

2. Objects observed by a representative, this office, at 11:00 hours on 17 September 1952:
3. Objects: A few (5,000) at an altitude of approximately 25,000 feet, dispersed in a circular pattern, appeared to be in formation, no visible means of propulsion or power, and no sound was heard. 
4. Objects: A few (2) angular, and described as metallic and shiny in appearance. 
5. Observers watched the objects for approximately 15 minutes and the objects were traveling at a high rate of speed, said to be in excess of 1,000 miles per hour.
6. Visibility was good and the objects were observed for approximately fifteen (15) minutes before disappearance. There was no noise, vibration, smoke, or odor. 
7. Identities of the observers are not given.
