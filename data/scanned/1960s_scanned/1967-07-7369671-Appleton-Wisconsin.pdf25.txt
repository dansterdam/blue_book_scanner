(Report on Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object) 

Date of Sighting: July 21, 1967 
Time of Sighting: Approx. 11:45 p.m. CDT
Location of Sighting: Appleton, Wisconsin
Weather Conditions: Clear and starry sky

On July 21, 1967, at approximately 11:45 p.m. CDT, Mr. Carl Appleton observed an unidentified flying object in the sky over Appleton, Wisconsin. 

Mr. Appleton stated that he was outside his home, looking at the clear, starry night sky, when he noticed a bright light moving rapidly across the sky. He described the object as round in shape, with a bright, bluish-white light emitting from it. The object appeared to be moving at a very high rate of speed.

Mr. Appleton estimated the object was several thousand feet in altitude and was traveling in a southwesterly direction. He stated that the object was visible for approximately 10-15 seconds before disappearing from view.

Mr. Appleton reported the sighting to the local police department, who in turn notified the proper authorities. An investigation was conducted, but no definitive determination was made as to the identity of the unidentified flying object.
