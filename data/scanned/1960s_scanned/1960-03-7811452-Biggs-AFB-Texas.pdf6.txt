BIGGS AFB TEXAS 
7 MAR 60
SUBJ: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT 
TO: COMMANDER 
AIR DEFENSE COMMAND 
ENT AFB, COLORADO 
1. AT APPROXIMATELY 1935 HOURS 7 MAR 60 A ROUND, BRIGHTLY LUMINOUS OBJECT WAS SIGHTED BY TWO AIRMEN AND ONE ENLISTED MAN IN THE VICINITY OF BIGGS AFB, TEXAS. THE OBJECT WAS FIRST OBSERVED AT APPROXIMATELY 20 DEGREES AZIMUTH AT AN ELEVATION OF 45 DEGREES. IT TRAVELED IN A NORTHWESTERLY DIRECTION FOR APPROXIMATELY 30 SECONDS AND THEN APPEARED TO STOP MOMENTARILY BEFORE ACCELERATING RAPIDLY AND DISAPPEARING FROM SIGHT. 
2. WEATHER CONDITIONS AT THE TIME OF THE SIGHTING WERE CLEAR WITH LIGHT WINDS. VISIBILITY WAS EXCELLENT. 
3. ONE COPY OF THIS REPORT IS BEING FORWARDED TO THE USAF SPECIAL PROJECTS OFFICE, WRIGHT-PATTERSON AFB, OHIO. 

(signed)
CHARLES A. SNITKER, Major, USAF 
Chief, Operations Division
