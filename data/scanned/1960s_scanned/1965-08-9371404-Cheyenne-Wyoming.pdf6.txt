1. Reference AFR 200-2, as amended. The following described unidentified flying objects are an addition to our report of 9 August 1965.

2. Description of Objects: The reported objects are plotted on the attached photographs as described in subparagraphs below:

a. Reported by civilian guard at Sioux Army Depot to Golf Launch Control Section at 2220 local time. The object sighting consisted of two objects moving rapidly at an altitude of 5,000 to 7,000 feet above the horizon. See plot number 1.

b. Reported from Cheyenne, Wyoming by six military personnel with the aid of binoculars. See plot number 2. The object sighting was an illuminated object which appeared to the southeast and traveled westward, disappearing to the southwest very late and reappeared again.

c. Objects reported by three airmen of a Geodetic Survey Team, who observed them with the aid of a theodolite (calibrated to a 40 power telescope). See plot number 3.

(1) Objects appeared to be twice the size of a pinhead, appeared in the middle of the sky, dipped and moved on course as indicated. When viewed through the theodolite, these objects grouped and varied in size.
