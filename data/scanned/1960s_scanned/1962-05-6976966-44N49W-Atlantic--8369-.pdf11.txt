26 MARCH 1962
SUMMARY OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT REPORT
1. On 16 December 1961, this Command received a report of an unidentified object in the vicinity of latitude 44°49'N, longitude 49°00'W in the Atlantic Ocean. The report was received from the U.S. Coast Guard via the U.S. Navy.
2. According to the report, the object was first sighted at about 1730E on 14 December 1961 by the crew of the U.S. Coast Guard Cutter 'Eastwind' at a range of approximately 45 miles.
3. The Eastwind was proceeding on a northeast course when the crew observed a bright light in the sky which appeared to be hovering in one position at an approximate altitude of 4,500 feet. The light was described as being extremely bright, changing in intensity from a very bright glow to an even brighter flash.
4. At one point the light appeared to move rapidly in a horizontal direction and then return to its original position.
5. The object was observed for approximately one hour and 45 minutes at which time it moved out of sight below the horizon.
6. From the available information, it is not possible to identify the object observed.
