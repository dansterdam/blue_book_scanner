UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT (Report of Sgt Elmer J. Olson)
In accordance with paragraph 14, AFR 200-2, 20 July 1962, the following report is given:

a. Description of the Object:
(1) Shape - Appeared to be a high flying aircraft leaving a fluorescent contrail. Object was not visible.
(2) Size compared to a known object - Unknown.
(3) Color - Solid bright white contrail.
(4) Number - One.
(5) Formation - N/A.
(6) Any discernible features or details - No.
(7) Tail, trail or exhaust - Fluorescent contrail glowed long time.
(8) Sound - None.
(9) Other pertinent features - None.

b. Description of Course of Object:
(1) While walking the dog, observer glanced upward and saw the contrail.
(2) 49° Elevation - 99° Azimuth.
(3) 30° Elevation - 120° Azimuth.
(4) When first observed, the object was leaving a contrail traveling to west 49° to 30° elevation. Object ceased making contrail at 30° elevation. It's elevation decreased to about 20° and the contrail was wider than the original sighting. At elevation 30°, the object appeared to be approximately 20 minutes drifting due South.
(5) Contrail disappeared instantly to the Southeast.
(6) First time approximately one to two minutes. Second about 45 seconds.
