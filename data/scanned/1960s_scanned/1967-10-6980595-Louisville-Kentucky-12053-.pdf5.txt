REPORT OF INVESTIGATION

A INCIDENT OCCURRED AT LOUISVILLE, KENTUCKY, ON 6 OCTOBER 1967, INVOLVING THE SIGHTING OF AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT BY A CIVILIAN WITNESS.

INVESTIGATION OF THIS INCIDENT WAS CONDUCTED BY THE UNITED STATES AIR FORCE UNDER PROJECT BLUE BOOK. THIS REPORT SUMMARIZES THE FINDINGS OF THAT INVESTIGATION.

WITNESS: MR. JOHN DOE, A RESIDENT OF LOUISVILLE, KENTUCKY.

DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT: ON 6 OCTOBER 1967, AT APPROXIMATELY 8:00 P.M., MR. DOE REPORTED OBSERVING A BRIGHT LIGHT MOVING ACROSS THE SKY OVER LOUISVILLE, KENTUCKY. MR. DOE STATED THAT THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE DISC-SHAPED AND WAS ACCOMPANIED BY SMALLER OBJECTS. THE OBJECT WAS OBSERVED FOR APPROXIMATELY 15 MINUTES BEFORE DISAPPEARING FROM VIEW.

INVESTIGATION: INVESTIGATORS FROM PROJECT BLUE BOOK INTERVIEWED MR. DOE AND EXAMINED THE SITE OF THE SIGHTING. NO PHYSICAL EVIDENCE WAS FOUND, AND NO OTHER WITNESSES WERE IDENTIFIED. CHECKS WITH LOCAL AUTHORITIES AND AVIATION SOURCES REVEALED NO REPORTS OF UNUSUAL AERIAL ACTIVITY IN THE AREA AT THE TIME OF THE INCIDENT.

CONCLUSION: BASED ON THE INVESTIGATION, PROJECT BLUE BOOK CONCLUDED THAT THIS INCIDENT COULD NOT BE EXPLAINED AND SHOULD BE CLASSIFIED AS "UNIDENTIFIED."
