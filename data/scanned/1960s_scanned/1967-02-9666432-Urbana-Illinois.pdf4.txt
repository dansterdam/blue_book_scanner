On 12 February 1967, at about 20:00 hours, 2d Lt JAMES A. MAYNE, JR., 32nd Air Base Squadron, flying a C-130 transport aircraft from Ashcroft Air Force Base Douglas, Arizona, while traveling north on U.S. Highway 95 at about 20,000 feet, observed a red-orange ball, about 35 to 40 angle subtended, at 320° azimuth, 20° elevation. It extinguished it was over an area between the highway and the Douglas Golf Course. Duration of sighting was approximately 1 to 2 minutes.
