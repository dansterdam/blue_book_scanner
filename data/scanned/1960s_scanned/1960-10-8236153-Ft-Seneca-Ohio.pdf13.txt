SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object
TO: COMMANDER
AIR TECHNICAL INTELLIGENCE CENTER
WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE, OHIO
1. At approximately 1000 hours on 22 September 1960, an Unidentified Flying Object (UFO) was observed by two members of the 98th BOMARC Missile Squadron, United States Air Force, at the Fort Seneca, Ohio, BOMARC Missile Site.
2. The UFO was first sighted by Airmen First Class JOHN DOE and RICHARD ROE, who were on perimeter patrol duty at the time. They reported that the object was silver-colored, round or oval in shape, and appeared to be hovering approximately 600-800 feet above the ground and a mile to a mile and a half from their observation point. The object remained in sight for approximately five minutes and then disappeared from view in a rapid horizontal movement.
3. Airmen Doe and Roe immediately notified the Missile Site Commander, who in turn notified higher headquarters. An investigation was immediately initiated and additional information is being developed.
4. It is requested that this Headquarters be informed of any further developments in this case.
BY ORDER OF THE SECRETARY OF THE AIR FORCE
JOHN R. MILLER
Colonel, USAF
Director of Intelligence
