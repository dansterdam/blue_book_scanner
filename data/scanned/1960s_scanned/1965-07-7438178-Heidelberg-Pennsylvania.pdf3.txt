UNCLASSIFIED
S 17002
SUBJECT: SIGHTING OF A STRANGE OBJECT IN THE SKY
1. ON 7 JULY 65 I RECEIVED A TELEPHONE CALL FROM MASTER SERGEANT JOHN S. FRAZIER OF AFM 15 DETACHMENT, HEIDELBERG, GERMANY. HE REPORTED THE FOLLOWING:
A. ON 6 JULY 65 AT APPROXIMATELY 2200 HOURS, MASTER SERGEANT FRAZIER AND STAFF SERGEANT ROBERT L. STEWARD WERE DRIVING ALONG ROUTE 4-14 NEAR HEIDELBERG, GERMANY WHEN THEY OBSERVED A STRANGE OBJECT IN THE SKY.
B. THE OBJECT WAS DESCRIBED AS RESEMBLING A LARGE ORANGE SPHERE EMITTING A BRIGHT ORANGE GLOW. IT APPEARED TO BE HOVERING OR MOVING VERY SLOWLY THROUGH THE SKY. SIGHTING LASTED APPROXIMATELY 1-2 MINUTES.
C. NEITHER OBSERVER HAD EVER SEEN ANYTHING LIKE THIS BEFORE. THEY WERE VERY EXCITED BY WHAT THEY SAW.
2. I HAVE ACCEPTED THE OBJECT SIGHTING AS SUFFICIENT TO WARRANT A FIELD INVESTIGATION ACCORDING TO AFR 200-2. THIS OFFICE WILL CONDUCT A COMPLETE INQUIRY AND SUBMIT A DETAILED REPORT AS SOON AS POSSIBLE.
