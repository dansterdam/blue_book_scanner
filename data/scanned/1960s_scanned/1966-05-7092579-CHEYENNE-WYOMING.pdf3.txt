SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects (UFO)

TO: AFSC (FTD)
Wright-Patterson AFB Ohio 45433

1. Reference AFR 200-2, as amended. The following described unidentified flying objects are reported as observed by two different groups on 28 May 1966. Both groups observed from the same location in the southern part of Cheyenne, Wyoming.

2. Description of Object: Except for difference in size and details, the descriptions appear to be the same. All sightings were from the ground and the aid of binoculars.

3. Two teenage boys were in the back yard observing the stars when they observed the objects. One object was circular and they called their father to verify the sighting. The object was round, about size of a dime held at arm's length, and moving erratically from north to south. After five minutes, object suddenly "blacked out" and was no longer visible.

4. The second observation was by two men, one a member of the reporting organization. The size of the bright object was estimated to be 25-cent piece held at arm's length, and moving rapidly in a southeasterly direction. The object changed to a blue-to-white light was very noticeable. There was no sound, and the observers able to determine its altitude. After five minutes, the object suddenly disappeared.

3. The weather was clear.

4. Weather balloons were released at 2300 hours MST by the local station.

5. Report was compiled by Major Elva D. Brown, Intelligence Officer, with over 19 years of military experience.

FOR:  CONLEY B. STROUD, JR., Colonel, USAF
Commander, 809th Combat Support Group
