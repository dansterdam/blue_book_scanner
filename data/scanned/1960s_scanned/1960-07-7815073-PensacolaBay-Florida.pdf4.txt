UNCLASSIFIED REPORT
U. S. NAVAL INTELLIGENCE

PENSACOLA, FLORIDA

SUBJECT: Unidentified Aerial Object, Pensacola Bay, Florida
DATE OF OBSERVATION: 3 Aug 1959, 0900L

(1) Details of Incident:

(a) Sighting: Object observed by pilot of P2V-5 aircraft. Object was sighted at 0930L, 3 Aug 1959, while aircraft was on routine training flight over Pensacola Bay, Florida.

(b) Description: A silvery, disc-shaped object, estimated to be approximately 30-40 feet in diameter, was observed hovering over the water. The object appeared to be approximately 2,000 feet above the bay.

(2) Additional Details:
(a) Assistance in identifying and describing the object was requested from the crew of an accompanying P4M-1 aircraft. This aircraft, however, did not observe the object.
(b) The P2V-5 pilot attempted to close on the object, but it immediately accelerated and pulled away at a high rate of speed, estimated at over 1,000 knots.
(c) The P2V-5 pilot reported the incident to the Naval Air Station Control Tower, which in turn reported the incident to the UFO project of the Air Technical Intelligence Center, USAF.

TENTATIVE CONCLUSION: DID-LNG is tentatively to attribute the above to some type of natural or conventional aerial phenomenon, the nature of which is not clear from the information available at this time. No previous record of this incident was found in the reports.
