This document does not contain any photographs. It appears to be a memorandum from the Federal Aviation Agency dated August 15, 1967, regarding a UFO sighting near Dyke, Virginia on July 1, 1967. The memorandum is addressed to James C. Manatt, Colonel, USAF Director of Technology and Subsystems at the Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio. It states that there is no evidence of a controlled flight service at Charlottesville, Virginia, and that no UFO calls were made to this facility on that date. The memorandum is signed by P. E. Reed.

UNITED STATES GOVERNMENT
Memorandum
SUBJECT: UFO Observation, July 1, 1967
Reference Your Letter of August 14, 1967
FROM: Chief, Charlottesville FSS
TO: James C. Manatt, Colonel, USAF 
Director of Technology and Subsystems
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio
In reference to the UFO sighting near Dyke, Virginia, there is no
Control Tower at Charlottesville, Va. There is an FAA Flight Service
Station located there, and this could be the source of information
STATED in your letter. However, no reports of any UFO calls were made to this facility
on that date.
Sorry, we are unable to provide you with any additional information as
to the possible cause of the sighting.
P. E. Reed
