SUBJECT: Supplemental Physical Evidence on Unidentified Explosion and Smoke over Ras Tanura, Saudi Arabia, 23 February 1961

TO: Air Technical Intelligence Center (ATIC-TDXT)
Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio

1. The following information and evidence is forwarded for further consideration and analysis in connection with the unidentified explosion in the air over Ras Tanura, Saudi Arabia, on Thursday, 23 Feb 1961. This information was obtained by an observer, W. Green, GS, 21st Weather Sq, Det 12, this station, on 24 Feb 1961:

a. Photographs:
(1) Still photograph taken by observer in boat on Half Moon Bay, longitude 50 degrees 02' East, latitude 26 degrees 34' North, showing flash from explosion followed by series of minor concentrated explosions.
(b) Two prints. Negative unobtainable.
(2) Type and make of camera: AGFA-ISOLETTE-120-254.
(3) Type focal length and make of lens: 1 1/4.5 - 85 mm AGFA.
(4) Brand and type of film: Kodak Verichrome Pan.
(5) Shutter speed used: 1/100.
(6) Lens opening used: F-22.
(7) No filter used.
(8) Solid stand used: None. Held in boat, trolling slowly.
(9) No panning used.
(10) Camera pointing relative to true North, and angle to ground (west): North 45.

b. Material:
Five fragments (one metallic and 4 charcoal-like) picked up by another observer on beach pier of Ras Tanura, Saudi Arabia, reported falling approximately 15 minutes after first explosion.
