6 March 1960 an additional unidentified object was reported in the Nome, Alaska area by between Nome and Unalakleet, Alaska, a distance of 85 miles. The pilot and co-pilot of a light aircraft, Casper Hagen, were both flying this aircraft. This pilot was described as being a bright flash seeming to result from an explosion at around 5000 feet altitude, 35 miles south of Nome. Both the pilot and co-pilot reported they saw the flash and saw nothing further - no sound. The only passenger aboard the aircraft was asleep and did not observe or see anything. Two individuals on the ground reportedly saw a flash, a 14 year old girl in Nome, and the town marshal of Nome. Interviews with Nome, Fairbanks, and Anchorage, Alaska were being made by this town and reports are being forwarded in accordance with AFR 200-2.
