UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
This document is from the Federal Bureau of Investigation. It contains information related to an investigation and will not be destroyed.
Date: April 6, 1950  
To: Office of Special Investigations, Air Force
Attention: Chief, Counter Intelligence Division
From: John Edgar Hoover, Director
Subject: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS - GRAND BLANC, MICHIGAN
Reference is made to this Bureau's memorandum dated March 27, 1950, concerning information received from the Grand Blanc, Michigan, Police Department regarding reported observations of unidentified flying objects on April 7, 1950.
By letter dated March 21, 1950, Mr. C. E. Peterson, Chief of Police, Grand Blanc, Michigan, advised that local police officers had observed unusual lights in the sky on the evening of April 7, 1950. The lights were described as glowing and moving at a high rate of speed. In his letter, Mr. Peterson requested any additional information the Air Force might have in its possession concerning the reported observations.
This Bureau advised the Grand Blanc Police Department by letter dated April 3, 1950, that the information had been furnished to the Air Force Office of Special Investigations for any action deemed appropriate.
