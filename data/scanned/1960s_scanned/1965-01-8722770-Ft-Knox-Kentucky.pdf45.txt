SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object

TO: Commanding General
ATTN: Deputy Chief of Staff, Intelligence
Department of the Army
Washington, D.C. 20310

1. An unidentified flying object was observed by a military police patrol #5509 in the vicinity of Cornish Street, Fort Knox, Kentucky, at about 0300 hours, 9 January 1965.

2. The object was described as a bright, rapidly moving light or object, moving in a northeasterly direction at an estimated altitude of 3,000 to 5,000 feet. It was observed for approximately 30 seconds and seemed to disappear over the trees in the distance. No other details were observed.

3. The sighting was confirmed by Mr. J. E. Oliver, Project Engineer, Ft Knox. He stated that the object was definitely not a known aircraft or any other aerial vehicle in his opinion.

4. Weather data during the sighting was: Temperature - 17 degrees F. Winds from the northwest at 10 knots. Overcast cloud cover at 10,000 feet.

5. Recommend no further investigation or action on this report.

/s/ George C. Weede, JR.
Colonel, Infantry
Assistant Chief of Staff, G-3
