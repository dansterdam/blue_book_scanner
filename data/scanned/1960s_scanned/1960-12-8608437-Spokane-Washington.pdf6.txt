ANALYSIS OF REPORTS OF UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECTS

8 DECEMBER 1960

REPORT ON UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT
 
3 DECEMBER 1960
SPOKANE, WASHINGTON

On 3 December 1960, at approximately 0520 hours, the reporting witness, a man, observed an unidentified flying object near Spokane, Washington. The object was described as oval in shape, white in color, and giving off a bright light. The witness estimated the object to be about 40 feet in diameter and at an altitude of 500 to 1000 feet. The object was observed for approximately 3 minutes before it suddenly accelerated and disappeared.

The witness, whose name is withheld for privacy reasons, was driving his car on a rural road near Spokane, Washington, when he first observed the object. He stopped his car and watched the object for the duration of the sighting. No other witnesses were reported. The object did not interfere with the operation of the witness's car.

The weather at the time of the sighting was clear, with moderate winds. No other aircraft were observed in the area at the time.

The reporting witness was interviewed by Air Force personnel and provided a signed statement describing his observations. Based on the witness's description and the circumstances of the sighting, the object was determined to be unidentified.
