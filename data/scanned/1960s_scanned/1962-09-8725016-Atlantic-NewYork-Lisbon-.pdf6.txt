SECTION HANDWICK GEN. GAS REPORTING PROCEDURE
Send Ail Reports to: DCS/INT (AFODC-INT)
Headquarters, U. S. Air Force
Washington 25, D. C.
THIS IS NOT AN ORDER - THIS IS A RECOMMENDED PROCEDURE
1. IMMEDIATE REPORTING REQUIRED:
(a) Sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFO) which appear to represent a possible threat or hazard to flights in your area.
(b) Sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFO) which display technological capabilities which far exceed the known capabilities of any conventional aircraft. 
2. ROUTINE REPORTING REQUIRED:
(a) Sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFO) which appear to represent no potential threat or hazard.
(b) Sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFO) which display no technological capabilities beyond those of known conventional aircraft.
FROM: The Assistant Chief of Staff, Intelligence, USAF
TO: All Air Force Commanders and All Air Force Intelligence Officers
SUBJECT: General Gas Reporting Procedure
1. The purpose of this procedure is to establish a standardized system for the immediate and routine reporting of sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFO).
2. All Air Force personnel and components are directed to comply with this procedure.
3. This procedure is applicable worldwide, including overseas commands.
4. The immediate reporting procedure outlined in paragraph 1 above is mandatory. The routine reporting procedure outlined in paragraph 2 above is recommended.
5. Reports submitted under this procedure will be evaluated and analyzed by the Directorate of Intelligence, Headquarters USAF.
6. Inquiries concerning this procedure should be addressed to the Assistant Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Headquarters USAF.
BY ORDER OF THE CHIEF OF STAFF:
FREDERIC H. SMITH, JR.
Major General, USAF
