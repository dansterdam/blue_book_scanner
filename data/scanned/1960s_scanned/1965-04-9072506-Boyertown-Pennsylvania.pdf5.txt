IN THE MATTER OF THE REPORT OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT AT BOYERTOWN, PENNSYLVANIA ON APRIL 9, 1965.
INFORMATION RECEIVED FROM M/SGT. HARRY E. STRICLAND (sic), USAF, DETAILED TO THE U.S. AIR FORCE PROJECT BLUE BOOK, WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE, OHIO.
ON APRIL 9, 1965, M/SGT. STRICLAND RECEIVED A TELEPHONE CALL FROM MR. CHARLES A. HOWE, RESIDENT OF BOYERTOWN, PENNSYLVANIA, REPORTING A SIGHTING OF AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT OVER BOYERTOWN ON THAT DATE.
MR. HOWE STATED THAT AT APPROXIMATELY 7:50 P.M. THAT EVENING, HE AND HIS WIFE WERE OUTSIDE THEIR HOME WHEN THEY OBSERVED AN EXTREMELY BRIGHT LIGHT IN THE SKY, APPROACHING FROM THE WEST. THE LIGHT APPEARED TO BE PULSATING AND CHANGING COLOR FROM WHITE TO RED TO GREEN. IT WAS MOVING IN A EAST-NORTHEAST DIRECTION AND AT TIMES APPEARED TO STOP AND HOVER.
MR. HOWE STATED THAT HE OBSERVED THE OBJECT FOR APPROXIMATELY 10 MINUTES BEFORE IT DISAPPEARED FROM VIEW. HE ALSO STATED THAT HE HEARD NO SOUND ASSOCIATED WITH THE OBJECT.
M/SGT. STRICLAND STATED THAT HE WOULD IMMEDIATELY DISPATCH AN INVESTIGATOR FROM PROJECT BLUE BOOK TO CONDUCT AN ON-SITE INVESTIGATION OF THE REPORTED SIGHTING.
