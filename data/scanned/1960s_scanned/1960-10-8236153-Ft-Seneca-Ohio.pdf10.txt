October 8, 1960

MEMORANDUM FOR THE RECORD

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object Sighting, Ft. Seneca, Ohio

1. On 7 October 1960, at approximately 2030 hours, Mr. George C. Keiser, a resident of Ft. Seneca, Ohio, reported seeing an unidentified object in the sky over Ft. Seneca. Mr. Keiser stated that the object was first observed at an azimuth of approximately 270 degrees and an elevation of 15 to 20 degrees. The object was described as a bright, glowing ball of light which was stationary for about 10 to 15 seconds, then suddenly accelerated upward at a very high rate of speed and disappeared from sight. The object was observed for a total of approximately 30 seconds.

2. According to Mr. Keiser, the object was observed through a window of his residence, and no sound was heard. The sky was described as clear with 7/8 cloud cover at an altitude of approximately 10,000 feet. The wind velocity was estimated at 5 to 10 knots from the southwest.

3. Mr. Keiser was interviewed by the undersigned on 8 October 1960. He appeared to be a reliable and competent witness. The interview was conducted in the presence of Mrs. Keiser, who corroborated her husband's account of the observation.

4. This report is being submitted for your information.
