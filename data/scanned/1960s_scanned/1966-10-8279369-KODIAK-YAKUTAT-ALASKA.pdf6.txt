15 Dec. 66

From 02222 to 06512 9 Oct. 66 AFR radar tracked two slow moving objects which were moving toward the southeast at an average speed of 25 knots. A message was sent to investigate. The radar lost contact with the objects in the vicinity of Cape Romanzof, Alaska. The aircraft pilots were flying in bad weather and on three passes were unable to see the objects.

Investigating official from Elmendorf AFB, Alaska believes the objects to be balloons. Several balloons have been recently seen in the Alaska area. This balloon could have the one observed at 1045Z/6 Oct. 66 Prevailing winds from the Cape Romanzof area to 02002/10 Oct. 66. At altitudes from 25,000 to 35,000 feet the winds were about 35 knots and could have carried an object from the Cape Romanzof area to Kodiak during this time frame.
