SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects (UFO)

TO: AFSC (ETID)

1. Reference AFR 200-2, as amended. The following described unidentified flying objects are an addition to our report of 6 August 1965:

2. DESCRIPTION OF OBJECTS: The reported objects are plotted on the attached maps with descriptions as referenced in subparagraphs below: 

Control: Individuals observed with binoculars.
Time: 2345-2350 hours, 3 Aug 1965.
Duration: Approximately 5 minutes.
Number of Objects: 3-5 objects observed.
Size: As compared to a star, the objects appeared to be the size of a silver dollar at arm's length.
Color: Bright, white color.
Motion: Moving separately in various directions, hovering, then accelerating rapidly.
Sound: No sound heard.

3. Reference is made to certain radar and visual sightings and observed by nine military personnel. See plot number 2.

When observed, the size of a star appeared to be four times larger. Moved slowly at first, then accelerated rapidly and disappeared.

Visual description: White light, sharp and clear in outline. Moved silently and smoothly.

EVALUATION: Adequate observational data. Objects were tracked by a Geodetic Survey Team, who considered the objects unusual and unable to identify. The Air Force Investigation Group has requested additional information from the base.
