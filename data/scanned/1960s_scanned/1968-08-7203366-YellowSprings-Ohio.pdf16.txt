Greene County, Ohio, named for General Nathanael Greene, one of the heroes of the American Revolutionary War, is located south to the north line of the new state. The oldest settlement in Greene County sprang up near Wilberforce. The first white settlement in the county was a community of 1100 persons around 1798. The first county business was transacted in a small log cabin on the banks of the Little Miami River, finished in 1803 at a cost of $1.50. The county seat of Xenia was established in 1807. Xenia derives its name from the Greek and signifies hospitality.
