U.S. DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE/Environmental Science Services Administration/Environmental Data Service
DAILY WEATHER MAPS
WEEKLY SERIES DEC. 16-22, 1968
The charts in this publication are a reproduction of the principal charts of the Weather Bureau publication, Daily Weather Map. They include the Surface Weather Map, the 500 Millibar Chart, the Highest and Lowest Temperatures Chart, and the Precipitation Chart. All of the charts for one day are arranged on a single page of this publication. They are copied from operational weather maps prepared by the U.S. Weather Bureau. The symbols used on the Surface Weather Map and the 500 Millibar Chart are explained in the legend.
The Surface Weather Map presents the station data and the analyses which the analysts are based. Occlusions and fronts are shown as dashed lines, and are labeled to indicate the wind direction and speed at the 850 millibar level.
The highest and lowest temperatures during the period ending at 1:00 a.m. e.s.t. The ranges of the reporting stations may be obtained from the Surface Weather Map. The minimum temperature is plotted above the maximum temperature for the same station.
The Precipitation Chart indicates by means of shading the areas that had precipitation during the 24 hours ending at 1:00 a.m. e.s.t. Amounts in inches to the nearest tenth are shown.
