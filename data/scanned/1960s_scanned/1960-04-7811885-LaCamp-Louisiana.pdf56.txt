21 April 1960

MEMORANDUM FOR RECORD

SUBJECT: Report of Unidentified Aerial Phenomena

1. On 13 April 1960, a report of an unidentified flying object sighted in the vicinity of Lake Charles, Louisiana (XXXX XXXX) local on 13 April 1960, was reported to the Air Intelligence Division, LCLD Tr. 

2. The object was reported to have been seen by one witness, in a rural area west of Lake Charles, Louisiana. The witness stated that the object was first sighted in the southwest sky and appeared to be hovering or stationary. The object then moved in a northerly direction and descended to about tree-top level, and then rapidly gained altitude and disappeared from view. The witness stated that the object had flashing lights and that four or five explosions was associated with the sighting.

3. The Wing Intelligence Officer of the LCLD Tr visited the area of the sighting and determined that the object had not been seen by any other persons in the area. An investigation was conducted on foot and by helicopter failed to reveal the object reported.

4. Investigation of the points of impact revealed evidence that a foreign metallic substance had been deposited on the ground. The analysis of this evidence is that the material is inert. (See Atch 1, Point 6, Photo 3) The condition of the ground at the points of impact was undisturbed. (Atch 1, Point 7, Photo 7) Samples of this evidence has been recovered and sent to ATIC.

5. On 21 April 1960, Major Friend of the Aerial Phenomena Section, AMC-AFSF, visited the LCLD Tr. Major Friend was assisted in the investigation by representatives of the intelligence division. Major Friend obtained negative results in his attempt to locate the object reported. No other sightings of the object were reported during the period of his investigation.
