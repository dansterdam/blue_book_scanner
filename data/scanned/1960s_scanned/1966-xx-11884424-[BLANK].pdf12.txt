AIR FORCE REGULATION
NO. 80-17
Washington, D.C. 15

Research And Development

UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS (UFO)

This regulation establishes the Air Force program for investigating and analyzing Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs) in the United States. The purpose is to determine if the UFOs represent (1) a possible threat to the United States, (2) an advanced technology developed by a foreign nation, or (3) phenomena which could become the subject of scientific study and further research.

SECTION A - GENERAL PROVISIONS
1. Establishment of Terms. To insure uniform usage of terms in UFO investigations and analyses, the following definitions are established:
a. Aircraft, aircraft lights, astronomical bodies, balloons, clouds, comets, missiles, planets, satellites, searchlights, weather phenomena (cloud, rain, wind, etc), are examples of familiar objects.
b. Objects which are not readily identifiable as familiar objects are classified as Unidentified Flying Objects.

SECTION B - PUBLIC RELATIONS AND RELEASES
1. Response to Inquiries. Procedures for handling inquiries, including news releases, concerning UFO sightings are outlined in this section.

SECTION C - PREPARING AND SUBMITTING REPORTS
1. General Information. This section covers the preparation and submission of reports regarding UFO sightings, including required data elements and reporting procedures.
