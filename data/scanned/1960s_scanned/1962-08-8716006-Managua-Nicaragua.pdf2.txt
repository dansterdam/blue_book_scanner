The image does not contain a photograph. It appears to be a telegram sent from Managua, Nicaragua on August 13, 1962 to the Secretary of State. The telegram discusses the sighting of an unidentified flying object over Managua by the ARMA. The object was observed passing from northwest to southeast and moving at a high but undetermined speed, without noise. The telegram also mentions that the object left a luminous trail which appeared to be a chemical propellant. The telegram is marked as "UNCLASSIFIED" and "REPRODUCTION FROM THIS COPY IS PROHIBITED UNLESS "UNCLASSIFIED.'"

INCOMING TELEGRAM Department of State
8-7-62 
ACTION AP IN 15:2049 (14 Aug 62) G/SO
FROM: Managua   
TO: Secretary of State
NO: 58, August 13, 6 p.m.
PRIORITY
UNCLASSIFIED PASS DA FOR ACSI; INFORMATION J-2 CINCARIB, G-2 USARCARIB
CITE CX-13
ON 8-13-62, AUGUST AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT WAS OBSERVED OVER MANAGUA BY ARMA. PASSING FROM NORTHWEST TO SOUTHEAST, OBJECT APPEARED BRILLIANT AND VERY RAPID AT UNDETERMINED SPEED, WITHOUT NOISE. OBJECT LEFT TRAIL OF LUMINOUS GLOW WHICH APPEARED TO BE CHEMICAL PROPELLANT. SEEN BY OTHER UNQUALIFIED OBSERVERS RESULTS LOCAL RUMOR THAT OBJECTS SOVIET RECONNOITERING AND PROBABLY WAS REPORTED AS SUCH TO INTERNATIONAL PRESS. ICGP-3.
SMD:JVD/la
NOTE: Relayed to CINCARIB, USARCARIB 8/14/62
