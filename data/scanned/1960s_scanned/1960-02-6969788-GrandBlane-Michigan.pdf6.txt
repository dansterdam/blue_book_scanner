AEROSPACE TECHNICAL INTELLIGENCE CENTER
UNITED STATES AIR FORCE 
WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE 
OHIO

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object, Joseph Perry

1. Forwarding attached copy of letter to ATIC from Higgins, Michigan, regarding "Unidentified Flying Object," dated 3 April 1960.

2. The color slides submitted by the following individuals have been analyzed at this laboratory.

3. The slides are of the image obtained on the moon and contain no outstanding features.

4. Only one slide contains anything unusual. There are say seven distinct objects in question and only one or two of these appear to be identical with the remaining objects. The largest spot in question is 10 degrees across and is shaped like a bright star. 

5. It is the opinion of photographic experts that the slides are of a natural phenomenon and show no evidence of a structured or controlled object.

6. Suggest the slides be returned to Mr. Perry. The subject's attention is invited to the final paragraph of the attached letter.

FOR THE COMMANDER:

FRANCIS A. LEGGET
Colonel, USAF
Deputy for Missions and Components
