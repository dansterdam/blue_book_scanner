REPORT OF INVESTIGATION
EASTING. CASE PEXTO
9 September 1065
I. SUMMARY OP SIGHTING:
On the night of 3 September 1965, at approximately 9:30 p.m., two police officers observed an unidentified flying object over Exeter, New Hampshire. The object was described as a lgrge, bright light which hovered for several minutes and then suddenly moved, accelerating at a very high rate of speed. 
II. INVESTIGATION:
A. Detailed Eyewitness Accounts:
1. Patrolman Eugene Bertrand, Exeter, New Hampshire Police Department. Patrolman Bertrand stated that at approximately 9:30 p.m. on 3 September 1965, he was driving his patrol car on Route 101 near the town of Exeter when he observed a large, bright light in the sky to the north. The light appeared to be hovering and Patrolman Bertrand stopped his car to observe it more closely. He stated that the light was brighter than any of the stars in the sky and that it remained stationary for several minutes. Suddenly, the light accelerated at a very high rate of speed and disappeared from sight within a few seconds.
2. Patrolman David Hunt, Exeter, New Hampshire Police Department. Patrolman Hunt, who was on patrol with Patrolman Bertrand, corroborated Patrolman Bertrand's account of the sighting. Patrolman Hunt stated that he also observed the large, bright light in the sky and that it appeared to be hovering. He stated that the light suddenly accelerated and disappeared from sight within a matter of seconds.
III. CONCLUSION:
Based on the detailed eyewitness accounts provided by the two police officers, it appears that an unidentified flying object was sighted over Exeter, New Hampshire on the night of 3 September 1965. The object was described as a large, bright light which hovered for several minutes before accelerating at a very high rate of speed and disappearing from sight. No satisfactory explanation for the sighting has been determined.
