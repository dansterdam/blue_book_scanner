TYNDALL AFB, FLORIDA
8 MAY 1961
2. INTERROGATION OF WITNESSES:
A. CAPT JOHN GILMORE, 3575th PILOT TRAINING SQ, TYNDALL AFB.
CAPT GILMORE IS A PILOT INSTRUCTOR AND WAS FLYING A T-33 JET TRAINER ON A ROUTINE TRAINING MISSION ON 8 MAY 1961 BETWEEN 1130 AND 1200 HOURS. HE STATES THAT HE NOTICED A BRIGHT OBJECT IN THE SKY THAT APPEARED TO BE TRAVELING AT A HIGH RATE OF SPEED. THE OBJECT WAS SILVERY-WHITE IN COLOR AND SEEMED TO CHANGE COURSE SEVERAL TIMES. CAPT GILMORE ESTIMATES THE OBJECT WAS APPROXIMATELY 20 MILES FROM HIS POSITION AND AT AN ALTITUDE OF 30,000 FT. CAPT GILMORE WATCHED THE OBJECT FOR APPROXIMATELY 3 MINUTES BEFORE IT DISAPPEARED FROM SIGHT.
B. 1LT ROBERT JACOBSON, 3575th PILOT TRAINING SQ, TYNDALL AFB.
1LT JACOBSON WAS A STUDENT PILOT AND WAS ALSO FLYING A T-33 JET TRAINER ON A ROUTINE TRAINING MISSION ON 8 MAY 1961 BETWEEN 1130 AND 1200 HOURS. HE STATES THAT HE ALSO NOTICED A BRIGHT OBJECT IN THE SKY AND THAT IT APPEARED TO BE TRAVELING AT A HIGH RATE OF SPEED. 1LT JACOBSON DESCRIBED THE OBJECT AS SILVERY-WHITE IN COLOR AND SAID IT SEEMED TO CHANGE COURSE SEVERAL TIMES. HE ALSO ESTIMATES THE OBJECT WAS APPROXIMATELY 20 MILES FROM HIS POSITION AND AT AN ALTITUDE OF 30,000 FT. 1LT JACOBSON WATCHED THE OBJECT FOR APPROXIMATELY 2 MINUTES BEFORE IT DISAPPEARED FROM SIGHT.
C. CAPT GILMORE AND 1LT JACOBSON CONFIRMED EACH OTHER'S OBSERVATIONS OF THE UNIDENTIFIED OBJECT.
