11. BRIEF SUMMARY AND ANALYSIS
Observer watched a cluster of lights that hovered in the south by south-west for over an hour. After approximately 70 minutes the lights disappeared in the west. The observer did not see any motion during this cluster of lights, the object just seemed to disappear on the horizon. The object was observed by several persons and Mr. Selfridge AFB was called and asked if anything unusual on radar was given a negative answer.
Various astronomical bodies were in the western sky at the time of the sighting, including the star, Sirius, though did not act at approximately MGOL. Other bright stars were present.
