UNITED STATES AIR FORCE MISSION TO HAITI (CAIRO)
c/o AMERICAN EMBASSY
PORT-AU-PRINCE, HAITI
20 September 1962

(1) During the period 16-17 August 1962, a U.S. Air Force team visited the area of Port-au-Prince, Haiti, to investigate a reported sighting of an unidentified flying object.

(2) According to information received, the incident occurred at approximately 2000-2200 on 7 August 1962, the witnesses being a local resident and several members of the U.S. Embassy staff.

(3) Description of incident:
(a) Some lights were observed descending from the sky and approaching the witnesses. These lights were described as bright, pulsating, and maneuvering.
(b) As the lights approached, the gassy smell of the atmosphere was detected, and the surrounding area was illuminated, although the source of light was not clearly seen.
(c) The color was said to be a silvery-gray.

(4) The officers:
(a) The sighting was considered to be of some significance.
(b) There was no physical evidence of the incident.
(c) No sound was heard.
(d) The object was estimated to be the size of a small automobile.
(e) The duration of the sighting was approximately 5 to 10 minutes.
(f) There was a cloud or haze surrounding the object, probably indicating some type of field or disturbance of the atmosphere.
(g) The object was observed to descend and then accelerate rapidly and disappear from sight.
(h) No count was evident at any time.
(i) None.

(5) Conclusion of course of current:
(a) The object observed may have been of an extraterrestrial nature.
(b) The characteristics observed are similar to those reported in other sightings.
