DAILY WEATHER MAPS
WEEKLY SERIES JULY 8-14, 1968

The contents in this publication are a continuation of the Weather Bureau publication. Daily Weather Maps. The material in these maps is arranged as follows:
Chart the highest and lowest temperatures for the 48 contiguous States arranged on a single page of this publication. Isobars (lines of equal pressure) for the United States 850-mb level as analyzed by the National Meteorological Center Weather Bureau. The symbols and legends on these charts are the same as those used in the Daily Weather Maps publication. Copies of these Daily Weather Maps may be obtained without charge by writing to the Superintendent of Documents, Government Printing Office, Washington, D.C. 20402.
The Surface Weather Map presents the weather conditions and analyses for 7:00 a.m. E.S.T. The tracks of well defined pressure centers are indicated by numbered centers. The central pressure of these centers is given at 12, 18, and 24 hours. Frontal positions are indicated by standard Weather Bureau symbols. Precipitation areas are shown by conventional symbols.
The first map in the series is for Sunday, July 7, 1968 and the last for Saturday, July 13, 1968. The legends are located at the bottom of the maps. The isobars are drawn at 4-millibar intervals and the isotherms at 5°F. intervals. The light contours and isotherms of the 850-mb charts are drawn at intervals of 60 meters and 5°C., respectively.
Mailing list copies should be made payable to the Superintendent of Documents.
