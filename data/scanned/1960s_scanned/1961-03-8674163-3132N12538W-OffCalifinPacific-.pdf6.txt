LOS ANGELES, MARCH 1961
THE OBJECT WHICH WAS FIRST SIGHTED OVER THE PACIFIC OCEAN, ABOUT 10 MILES OFF THE COAST OF SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA, BY A 14-YEAR-OLD BOY AND HIS 13-YEAR-OLD BROTHER, BELIEVED TO BE ALUMINUM OR CHROMIUM-PLATED, SILENT AND STATIONARY, REMAINED IN VIEW FOR ABOUT 45 MINUTES.
