7 OCTOBER 1967
TUCSON, ARIZONA

SPECIAL REPORT
UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT (UFO)

1. DETAILS OF OBSERVATION:
On 7 October 1967 at approximately 1815 hours, Mr. Mike Cosgrove, an employee of the Tucson Gas and Electric Company, observed an unidentified flying object over the northwest section of Tucson, Arizona. The object was observed for approximately 10 to 15 minutes.
Mr. Cosgrove stated that the object appeared to be approximately 30 to 40 feet in diameter, circular in shape, and bright silver in color. The object hovered for several minutes, then shot straight up at a high rate of speed and disappeared from sight. No sound was heard during the observation.

2. SUPPORTING EVIDENCE:
Mr. Cosgrove immediately reported his observation to the Tucson Gas and Electric Company dispatcher, who in turn notified the local Air Force Reserve unit at Davis-Monthan Air Force Base. An investigation was initiated by the Air Force.

3. CONCLUSIONS:
Based on the information gathered, it is the conclusion of the investigating officer that the unidentified flying object observed by Mr. Cosgrove was most likely an advanced aircraft or experimental vehicle of some kind. The high rate of speed and abrupt vertical departure from the area are indicative of a highly capable and sophisticated aerial vehicle. However, the actual nature and origin of the object remain unknown.
