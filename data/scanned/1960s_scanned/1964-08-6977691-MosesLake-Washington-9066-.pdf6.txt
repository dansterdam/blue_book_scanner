REPORT OF INVESTIGATION

INCIDENT INVESTIGATED BY: J. ALLEN HYNEK, CONSULTANT, U.S. AIR FORCE PROJECT BLUE BOOK

SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT SIGHTING

DATE OF INCIDENT: AUGUST 6, 1964

LOCATION OF INCIDENT: MOSES LAKE, WASHINGTON

WITNESS: MRS. ROBERT TABER

TYPE OF INCIDENT: VISUAL SIGHTING

DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT: ON AUGUST 6, 1964, MRS. ROBERT TABER REPORTED SIGHTING AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT IN THE SKY NEAR MOSES LAKE, WASHINGTON. MRS. TABER DESCRIBED THE OBJECT AS SILVER IN COLOR, ROUND IN SHAPE, AND APPROXIMATELY 40 FEET IN DIAMETER. SHE STATED THAT THE OBJECT HOVERED IN THE AIR FOR SEVERAL MINUTES BEFORE FLYING AWAY AT A HIGH RATE OF SPEED.

INVESTIGATION AND ANALYSIS: PROJECT BLUE BOOK CONSULTANT J. ALLEN HYNEK CONDUCTED AN INVESTIGATION OF THIS INCIDENT. AFTER REVIEWING MRS. TABER'S ACCOUNT AND EXAMINING THE PHOTOGRAPHIC EVIDENCE, HYNEK CONCLUDED THAT THE OBJECT SIGHTED WAS MOST LIKELY A WEATHER BALLOON OR OTHER KNOWN AERIAL PHENOMENON. NO FURTHER INVESTIGATION WAS DEEMED NECESSARY.

CONCLUSION: THIS INCIDENT WAS CLASSIFIED AS "UNIDENTIFIED" BY PROJECT BLUE BOOK, THOUGH THE INVESTIGATING CONSULTANT BELIEVED IT TO BE A CASE OF MISTAKEN IDENTITY INVOLVING A KNOWN AERIAL OBJECT.
