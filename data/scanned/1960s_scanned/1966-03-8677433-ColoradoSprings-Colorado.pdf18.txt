WITNESS STATEMENT -- COLORADO SPRINGS, COLORADO
CASE 66-8677433  8 MARCH 1966
SUBJECT SIGHTING:        Unidentified Flying Object
REPORT RECEIVED:        8 March 1966
INVESTIGATOR:                First Lieutenant Paul Powers, USAF
WITNESS:                        Master Sergeant Edmond Lilly, USAF
WITNESSES OCCUPATION:     Personnel Technician
LOCATION OF SIGHTING:     Colorado Springs, Colorado
DATE OF SIGHTING:        8 March 1966
TIME OF SIGHTING:        0315 Hours (3:15 A.M.)
WEATHER:                Clear and calm
DURATION OF SIGHTING:    10 to 15 minutes
REPORTED TO:            This Air Force Base, 8 March 1966
DESCRIPTION OF OBJECT:
Master Sergeant Edmond Lilly, USAF, reported that he observed a bright, luminous object in the sky at approximately 0315 hours on 8 March 1966. The object was described as round or oval in shape, approximately 20-30 feet in diameter, and emitting a bright, pulsating light. The object was observed for a period of 10 to 15 minutes as it maneuvered silently in the sky over Colorado Springs, Colorado.
The witness reported that the object appeared to be at an altitude of approximately 1,000 feet and was observed to make several rapid, right-angle turns, as well as sudden stops and starts. Sergeant Lilly further stated that the object appeared to be metallic in nature and had no visible means of propulsion.
After approximately 10 to 15 minutes, the object accelerated rapidly and disappeared from sight in a northeasterly direction.
CONCLUSION:
This case remains UNIDENTIFIED. No explanation for the sighting has been determined.
