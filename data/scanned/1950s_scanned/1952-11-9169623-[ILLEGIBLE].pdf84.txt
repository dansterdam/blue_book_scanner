AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
Unidentified Flying Object (FLYOBJECT)
Newfoundland
16 November 1952
NATHAN T. NELSON, Captain, USAF
CO, McAndrew AFB, NE31
The Commanding Officer, McAndrew AFB, Newfoundland, made the following report on the sighting of an unidentified object:
1. The air defense radar at McAndrew AFB, Newfoundland, detected an unidentified object on their radar scope on 16 November 1952 at 0422A, 3rd shift. An Air Police on duty (Sgt) observed the object and tracked it visually. The object was described as bright, round, and later dropped from sight. It was oval-shaped, and appeared bright green.
COMMENTS OF PREPARING OFFICER:
1. Weather conditions prevailing during the period of sighting: Estimated 2000 feet broken, 10 miles visibility, temperature 37 degrees, wind from WNW at 15 knots.
2. There was no activity or condition, meteorological or otherwise, which might account for the sighting.
3. No interception or identification action was taken.
