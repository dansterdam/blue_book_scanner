UNCONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT
CHICAGO, ILLINOIS 2 JULY 1952

TO: DIRECTOR OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS 
DEPARTMENT OF THE AIR FORCE 
WASHINGTON 25, D.C.

1. ON 2 JULY 1952 AT APPROXIMATELY 9:12 P.M. CST, TWO OFFICERS OF THE CHICAGO POLICE DEPARTMENT OBSERVED A FAST MOVING, HIGH ALTITUDE, UNUSUALLY BRIGHT OBJECT TO THE WEST OF CHICAGO, ILLINOIS. THE OBJECT WAS DESCRIBED AS A "DEEP REDDISH-ORANGE" IN COLOR AND SEEMED TO BE DESCENDING RAPIDLY. IT WAS ESTIMATED TO BE APPROXIMATELY 30 DEGREES ABOVE THE HORIZON AT THE TIME IT WAS FIRST OBSERVED. THERE WAS NO SOUND ASSOCIATED WITH THE OBJECT AND IT DISAPPEARED COMPLETELY AFTER IT HAD DESCENDED BELOW THE HORIZON. THERE COULD BE NO ACCURATE CONFIRMATION OF ITS CORRECT SIZE, SHAPE, OR SPEED. 

2. DETAILS: TWO CHICAGO POLICEMEN, ACTING AS GROUND OBSERVERS, WATCHED THE OBJECT FOR APPROXIMATELY 60 SECONDS AS IT PASSED OVERHEAD FROM WEST TO EAST ACROSS THE CHICAGO AREA. IT WAS DESCRIBED AS BEING VERY BRIGHT, HAVING A DEEP REDDISH-ORANGE COLOR, AND TRAVELING AT A VERY RAPID SPEED AND APPARENTLY DESCENDING. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE LARGER THAN A CONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT AND OF UNCONVENTIONAL SHAPE, SEEMINGLY DISC-LIKE, AND LEFT NO TRAIL OR EXHAUST. IT APPEARED TO CHANGE DIRECTION SLIGHTLY TOWARDS THE SOUTHEAST AND THEN DISAPPEARED FROM VIEW BELOW THE HORIZON.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
