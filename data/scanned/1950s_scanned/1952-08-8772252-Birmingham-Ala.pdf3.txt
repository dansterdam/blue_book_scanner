Air Intelligence Information Report
The following is submitted in accordance with paragraph 7a, AR 380-5, 15 August 1952:
1. On 6 August 1952, at approximately 1325 hours, a red, oval-shaped object, estimated to be about 30 feet in diameter, was observed in the vicinity of Birmingham, Alabama. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of approximately 5,000 feet and was traveling at an estimated speed of 250 to 300 mph.
2. The object was observed by a Commercial Airlines pilot and co-pilot who reported that the object followed their aircraft for approximately 10 to 15 seconds, then accelerated and pulled away at a tremendous rate of speed.
3. No other aircraft was in the vicinity at the time and no unusual sounds were heard.
4. I know of no intentions or activity that can explain the objects.
5. I know of no material evidence or the existence or nature of this object.
6. The approximate position of the object was over Birmingham, Alabama, as approximately three miles southwest of the city.
Signed: Edward U. Graef, Major, USAF
Chief, Air Intelligence Information Division
