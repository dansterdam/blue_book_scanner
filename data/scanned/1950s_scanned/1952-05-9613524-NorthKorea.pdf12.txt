5. Extracted from Fifth Air Force Mission Summary, 19 May 1952: 
An unidentified object observed a bright object traveling at 30 degrees bearing at 10,000 feet at 2352L. Length of observation was 2 minutes. The object exhibited a brightness greater than that of a standard airborne interceptor and appeared very similar to a conventional jet aircraft in size and speed, yet lacked the typical vapor trail and engine noise. Intercept by Group considered, credibility of witnesses to be excellent. D/I Comment: The incident is of interest but at this time there is no additional information available to evaluate the nature of the subject. Reporting Information on Unidentified Flying Objects, dated 5 March 1952.
The estimate of the witnesses was that the flashes of light with no definite reference points makes the have considered the body discernible, but they could not make an accurate determine as to what might the speed, size, and alittude of the subject. It is believed that there have been a number of sightings similar to this one, with no definite indications of distance, speed and alittude.
