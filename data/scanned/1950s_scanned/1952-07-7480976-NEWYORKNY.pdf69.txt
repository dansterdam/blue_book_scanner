AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

On 3 July 1952 an unidentified object was observed by three observers at a position west of Orlando, Florida, on West Central Avenue. The observers were flying a small private plane when the object was sighted. The object was described as disc-shaped, silver in color, and appeared to have a diameter of about 30 feet. The object was travelling at a very high rate of speed, estimated attitude of 6000 Feet. 

The object was travelling from north to south. When first sighted, the object was higher than the plane. After keeping the object in sight for about 45 seconds, it disappeared. No sound was heard.

The object was dull gray in color and appeared to be a concave disc. The observers stated she had seen 3 to 5 similar aircraft in the air and the object was approximately 1/3 of the diameter.

Weather at time of sighting was CAVU with thunderheads in area. No interceptions were made, and no photographs were taken.

The 327 Tactical Wing of the Naval Air Station was flying in the vicinity of Orlando during period of observation but no planes from the 327th were reportedly involved.
