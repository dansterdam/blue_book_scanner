SYNOPSIS: Municipal Airport, Paris, Illinois, reported to the Office of Special Investigations, Chanute Air Force Base, that on 9 October 1951 at 1242 hours he observed an unknown unidentified aerial object directly overhead at an estimated altitude of 2,000 feet. The object was approximately one-half the size of a normal aircraft and was traveling in a southeasterly direction. The day was clear and informants vision was unobstructed. Informant advised that a pilot of a private aircraft in the vicinity at the time observed the same object approximately 1,000 feet above the ground and also noted it was traveling in a southeasterly direction. The object was last seen traveling in a northwesterly direction, south of the Atomic Energy Plant at Newport, Indiana.

2. DETAILS: On 9 October 1951, at approximately 1242 hours, JAMES HAGUE, Chief Aircraft Communicator, Civil Defense Administration, Municipal Airport, Paris, Illinois, reported to the Office of Special Investigations, Chanute Air Force Base, that he had observed an unknown unidentified aerial object at approximately 2,000 feet altitude, which he estimated to be approximately one-half the size of a normal aircraft. The object was traveling in a southeasterly direction and he stated that he had observed it for approximately 30 seconds. The day was clear and his vision was unobstructed. HAGUE advised that a pilot of a private aircraft in the vicinity at the time also observed the same object approximately 1,000 feet above the ground and noted it was traveling in a southeasterly direction. The object was last seen traveling in a northwesterly direction, south of the Atomic Energy Plant at Newport, Indiana. HAGUE stated that the object pursued a straight path and that he had the impression it was silent and streamlined. HAGUE estimated the object's speed at approximately 400 MPH. HAGUE stated that the object presented a silvery, metallic color and that he had the impression it was approximately the size of a one-half dollar piece at arm's length. HAGUE stated that the object pursued a straight path and that he had the impression it was silent and streamlined.
