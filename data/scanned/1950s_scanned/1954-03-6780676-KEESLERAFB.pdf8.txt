UNCLASSIFIED                                            14 MAR 1954
2800th AIR BASE WING (SAC)
KEESLER AIR FORCE BASE, MISSISSIPPI

SUBJECT: Unknown Flying Object Report

TO:       Director of Intelligence
          Headquarters, USAF
          Washington 25, D. C.

1. This report is submitted in accordance with the provisions of AFR 200-2.

2. Date and Time of Sighting:
   6 March 1954, approximately 1900 hours.

3. Location of Observer:
   Keesler Air Force Base, Mississippi.

4. Description of Object:
   The object was a diffused, disc-shaped object appearing to be about the size of a standard automobile tire at a distance of approximately 500 yards. It appeared to be at an altitude of about 2,000 feet and was traveling at a very high rate of speed in a south-easterly direction.

5. Course of Object:
   The object was observed for approximately 10 seconds as it traveled across the sky in a south-easterly direction.

6. Background:
   The weather was clear at the time of the sighting with unlimited visibility. The object was observed by two Air Force personnel stationed at this base.

7. Conclusion:
   It is the opinion of the observers that the object sighted was some type of unidentified flying object. No known aircraft in this area could have the characteristics described in paragraph 4 above.

8. This report has been prepared in accordance with the provisions of AFR 200-2. No further information is available.

                                        /s/ WILLIAM P. COLEMAN, Lt Col, USAF
