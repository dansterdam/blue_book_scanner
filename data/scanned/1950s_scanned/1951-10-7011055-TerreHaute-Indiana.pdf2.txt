SUBJECT: UNKNOWN SUBJECT
Unidentified Aerial Object Sighted Over
Hulman Municipal Airport, Terre Haute, 
Indiana, 9 October 1951

TO: Director,
Office of Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. SYNOPSIS: Chief Aircraft Communicator, CAA, Hulman Municipal Airport, Terre Haute, Indiana, reported to the Office of Special Investigations that on 9 October 1951 at approximately 1345 hours, he had observed a silver and metallic object directly overhead at Hulman Municipal Airport. The object was approximately the size of a half-dollar and was flying in a southeasterly direction. The day was clear and informant's vision was unobstructed. He stated that a similar object was sighted on the same date at approximately 13:45 hours and that it was approximately 5,000 feet in altitude. This object was last seen traveling in a northeasterly direction, south of Terre Haute, Indiana.

2. DETAIL: Mr. William Lear, Aircraft Communicator, Civil Aeronautics Authority, Hulman Municipal Airport, Terre Haute, Indiana, was interviewed on 12 October 1951 by Special Agent Albert V. Parrish, and stated that he had observed an unknown unidentified aerial object on 9 October 1951. The object was sighted directly overhead at Hulman Municipal Airport which is located approximately five (5) miles north of Terre Haute, Indiana. He stated that the object was approximately the size of a half-dollar and was silver in color. The object was moving in a southeasterly direction and was approximately 5,000 feet in altitude. Mr. Lear stated that the object pursued a straight path of flight; that he had observed a similar object on the same date at approximately 13:45 hours and that this object was last seen traveling in a northeasterly direction, south of Terre Haute, Indiana.
