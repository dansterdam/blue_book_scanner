TITLE: Sighting of Rockets on 18 November 1949 at 0345 Hours Greenwich Civil Time over Santa Monica, California

SYNOPSIS: This is an initial report.

Three officers of the 374th Reconnaissance Squadron (VLR) Weather, McChord Air Force Base, McChord, California, while airborne on a routine weather reconnaissance flight, sighted what appeared to be rockets at 0345 hours Pacific Standard Time, on 18 November 1949. From an altitude of 13,000 feet, the object was sighted at approximately 49 degrees north latitude, 121 degrees west longitude, a great rate of speed on a heading of 270 degrees. When first observed it was at an altitude of approximately 19,000 feet and disappeared in a layer of clouds with tops at 14,000 feet. The object appeared to leave a trail of flame about 20 feet in length, and accelerated to an estimated 10 seconds. No unusual disturbances in radio transmissions or reception. Radar not operating at time of sighting.
