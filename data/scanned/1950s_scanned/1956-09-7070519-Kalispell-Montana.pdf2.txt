1. The attached Air Intelligence Information Report (AF Form 112), as submitted by 4602d Air Intelligence Services Squadron, Malmstrom AFB, Montana, is forwarded for your information and necessary action.

FOR THE COMMANDER:

CHARLES W. GODSEY
Captain, USAF
Assistant Adjutant

"CMNI34 "SAUCERS" - GREAT FALLS, MONT., SEPT. 4-5 (INS) OFFICIAL AT MALMSTROM AIR FORCE BASE REPORTS OBSERVERS DESCRIBING ATTEMPTED INTERCEPTIONS OF FLYING SAUCERS SEEN NEAR KALISPELL, MONT. LAST NIGHT. FIVE PILOTS CHASED SAUCERS ABOUT 10-12 PM. THEY REPORTED MEN ON THE BASE TOWER SAID THEY COULD NOT PICK UP ANYTHING ON RADAR. THIS MORNING AFTER NUMEROUS REPORTS WERE RECEIVED OF TRIANGULAR-SHAPED LUMINOUS OBJECTS, AN AIR SEARCH WAS INSTITUTED. THE "OBJECT" REPORTEDLY APPEARED ABOUT 10 P.M. (MST) LAST NIGHT AND DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 5:30 AND 6:30 A.M. THIS MORNING. NUMEROUS RESIDENTS OF THE AREA REPORTED SEEING THE "OBJECT". DETAILS WERE WITHHELD FROM THE PUBLIC. AUTHORITIES SAID THEY HAD NOT MADE VISUAL CONTACT WITH THE LUMINOUS OBJECTS. SOME WEATHER OBSERVERS IN MONTANA SPECULATED THE OBJECT MIGHT BE EXPLAINED AS NORTHERN LIGHTS WHICH ARE PARTICULARLY BRILLIANT IN MONTANA."
