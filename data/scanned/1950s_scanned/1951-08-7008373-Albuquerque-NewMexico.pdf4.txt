SUBJECT: Unidentified Type of Aircraft Sighted by
me at Albuquerque, New Mexico, at 2155 Hours, 25 August 1951

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, United States Air Force
Washington 25, D. C.

1. SYNOPSIS: Handled by Security Guard. The witness, a Sergeant, sighted one and a half (1 1/2) mile long object brighter than 2-25 watt light observed by himself and wife. Approached from Southeast at 500 miles per hour, no sound. Object had glowing white lights on trailing edge of wings and black stripes. Object was seen at an elevation of 45 degrees from the horizontal at 2155 hours, 25 August 1951, disappeared at 1000 hours, 26 August 1951. Lt WILLIAM L. DAW, Officer of the Guard, Kirkland Air Force Base, New Mexico, notified the Office of Special Investigations, District Office, KAFB, of this incident.
2. DETAIL: As reported by the witness, an unidentified type of aircraft was sighted by him at Albuquerque, New Mexico, at 2155 hours, 25 August 1951. The witness, a Sergeant, while on duty as a Security Guard at Sandia Base, sighted the object at an elevation of 45 degrees from the horizontal. The object, which he described as being one and a half (1 1/2) miles long, was brighter than a 2-25 watt light. It approached from the Southeast at an estimated speed of 500 miles per hour, with no sound. The object had glowing white lights on the trailing edge of the wings and black stripes. The object was observed for approximately 5 minutes, then it disappeared. The reflection of these lights could be seen on the underside of the object. The witness also stated that the underside of the object appeared to be concave. This sighting was confirmed by his wife.
