UNCLASSIFIED

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

30th Air Division 	TR-GJ-516 	PAGE 2 OF 3 PAGES

In accordance with ADC letter 200-1, dated 11 April 1951, the following information is submitted on reported visual sightings of so-called unidentified flying objects by Contractor Guard at K-25 Plant, Oakridge, Tennessee:

a. Description of Object/Phenomena:
(1) Object sighted was square and approximately twenty feet wide. Color between a white and gray. No markings were observable.
(2) Always kept broadside to observer.
(3) Showed no unusual vapors or flames. Conventional aircraft. No sound, smoke or noise was audible.

b. Time Factors:
Sighted at 0615 EST, 7 December 1951, and was under observation about two minutes total and 30 to 45 seconds each time object came out of clouds.

c. Manner of Observations:
Visual only. No optical or electronic equipment was used for observation. Speed constant, altitude varying approximating 300 feet from top of clouds.

d. Location of Object/Occurrence:
Observer was outside Barrier Fence of K-25 Gaseous Diffusion Plant, in Tower #14, which is a part of the security system around the plant. Location of observer was within 1/2 to 3/4 mile of occurrence. 

e. Witness/Observer Data:
Witness and observer was Mr. C. Contractor Guard at K-25 Plant, Oakridge, Tennessee. Employed by the Union Carbide Corporation, employed 2 hours, 37 minutes when object was sighted. Good physical and mental condition, no illness, fatigue or nervousness. Fully informed of his responsibility by his superiors and he appears to be honest and conscientious.

f. Weather Conditions:
0622 EST - Ceiling 6,000 scattered. Visibility: 30 miles. Wind: 090 12KT - 06 17 MPH. Altimeter: 29.99.
0830 EST - Ceiling 6,000 scattered. Visibility: 30 miles. Wind: 090 15 MPH. Altimeter: 29.97.
Time area was under constant surveillance by weather observers at 3 hour intervals.
