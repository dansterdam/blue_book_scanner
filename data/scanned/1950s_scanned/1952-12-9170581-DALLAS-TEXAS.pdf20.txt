MARCH 8-
SUBJECT:REPORT OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT SIGHTING - DALLAS, TEXAS

1. The following report is submitted based on an interview with the observer, Mr. A. J. CROWDER, Dallas, Texas, on 7 March 1952:

2. On 9 December 1951, between 1930 and 2000 hours, Mr. CROWDER observed an unidentified object in the sky approximately 20 miles southwest of Dallas, Texas. The object was first observed by Mr. CROWDER while he was traveling west on U.S. Highway 80 between Dallas and Mesquite, Texas.

3. DESCRIPTION OF OBJECT: Mr. CROWDER reported that the object was disc-shaped, approximately 30 feet in diameter, and was completely silent in flight. He stated that the object appeared to be illuminated and emitted a yellowish-white glow. The object was estimated to be traveling at a speed of approximately 500 miles per hour.

4. FLIGHT OF OBJECT: Mr. CROWDER stated that the object was observed for approximately 2 to 3 minutes and disappeared in an easterly direction. Upon first sighting the object, Mr. CROWDER stopped his automobile and observed the object for about 1 minute. He then continued his trip toward Dallas for approximately 1 mile, stopped his automobile again, and observed the object for an additional 1 to 2 minutes.

5. WEATHER CONDITIONS: Clear sky, with visibility estimated at 20 miles. No wind.

6. CONCLUSIONS: The reported sighting of an unidentified flying object by Mr. A. J. CROWDER is considered reliable and authentic. The description of the object coincides with reports of similar unidentified flying objects observed in this area within the past 12 months.

ALFRED C. LOEDDING
TECHNICAL ASSISTANT
