AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
PLACE: Yancey County, North Carolina
DATE: 25 July 1952
SUBJECT: Sighting of unidentified flying objects
REPORT MADE BY: Major Bronne, G-2, Langley AFB Base, called this office to inform them of a report which had been telephoned to him from the 8th Naval District, Norfolk, Virginia.
Lt. Colonel F. L. Blocker, of the Amphibious Base, Little Creek, Virginia, was contacted by Mr. R. C. Shyrock who is the Vice President of a Hosiery Company located at Burnsville, North Carolina and reports that he and two assistants were on the beach at Craney Island, Virginia, on Saturday, 26 July 1952, about 2 p.m. EDT. They observed the objects.
Sergeant A. B. Gearner, North Carolina State Police, reports that he and two civilians were on the beach at Burnsville, North Carolina, on Saturday, 26 July 1952, about 3:40 p.m. EDT, and observed the objects. The lead object would draw away and the other two objects would then form a tight formation around it, then they would separate again.
Mr. C. D. Grimes stated that one of his friends was a man who had a considerable amount of experience in aviation physics. This man stated that he had "never seen anything like that before."
Lt. Colonel F. D. Amick is presently staying at the Waynesville, North Carolina Hotel and will be there for two weeks.

JOHN S. GRANT, Colonel, USA
Deputy Chief of Staff, Inteiligence
Department of the Air Force
