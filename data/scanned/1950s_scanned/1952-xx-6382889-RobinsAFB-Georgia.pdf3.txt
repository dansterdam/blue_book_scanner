AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
1. Unidentified Flying Objects:
a. Observer: TAMA, Robins Air Force Base, Georgia.
b. Location: Robins Air Force Base, Georgia.
c. Date and Time: 23 September 1952 and 2230 hours, EST.
d. Weather: (Partial report of Base Weather Station) Visibility 12 miles, 5,000 feet broken, Thin Cirrus, Winds - NW force 5, 3 degrees centigrade.
2. The observer watched the objects for three (3) or four (4) minutes and returned to the main base.
3. Upon continuing the investigation, the Main Gate Officer interviewed 1/Lt. Clifton F. King and 1/Lt W. L. Rowland, the Air Police, to accompany him to a spot below the Object location.
4. "Observer stated that the objects were silently an illuminated bright red color, about the size of a star. The objects were hovering and the distance as when first seen by was estimated as about five (5) miles and the altitude was estimated 12,000 feet or more."
5. "Observer stated that the objects were about the same size as the surrounding stars and just as clearly defined. He could not detect any sound, vibration, smoke, flame or other peculiarities other than the illuminated color."
