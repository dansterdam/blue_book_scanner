AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

Unidentified Aerial Object Sighting on 27 July 1950 in Germany

LOCATION: Wiesbaden, Germany
DATE: 27 July 1950
OBSERVER: Captain, USAF

SUMMARY: The Intelligence Officer, 602d Air Intelligence Wing, Wiesbaden Air Base, reports the following information on an unidentified aerial object sighted in the sky to the west of Wiesbaden Air Base.

DETAILS: 
1. "At 1850Z, while sitting on the porch of the MATS barracks, I observed an object in the northern sky at an altitude of 12,000 to 15,000 feet. The object was circular and resembled an automobile headlight tinted so that a portion of the under-elliptical axis. At approximately 1855Z, the object accelerated and leveled off at an altitude of approximately 6,000 feet, continuing on a straight course. I observed a simultaneous acceleration and disappearance of the object."

2. The Intelligence Officer states that the barracks are located on Wiesbaden Air Base.

3. The two control tower operators who were on duty at Wiesbaden Air Base at the time of the sighting were contacted. Neither of the airmen observed any means of propulsion and neither could state the direction of the object's disappearance.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.

APPROVED:
WILLIAM L. TRAVIS
Lt Colonel, USAF
Chief, Intelligence Division
