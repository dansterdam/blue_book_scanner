II. SYNOPSIS:
At approximately 1920 hours on 5 November 1957, while on routine patrol in the control tower at the Long Beach Airport, the observer sighted an unidentified object in the sky. The object was observed for a period of approximately five (5) minutes, during which time it was tracked on the airport's radar scope. The object engaged in various maneuvers and is considered to be unidentified.
III. DETAILS:
1. On 5 November 1957, at approximately 1920 hours P.S.T., the observer was on routine patrol in the control tower at the Long Beach Airport. The observer then spotted an unidentified object in the sky, approximately 15 miles southwest of the airport.
2. The object was observed to be round in shape, with a diameter estimated to be 75 to 100 feet. It was a bright white in color, and appeared to be luminous. The object was observed to make several sharp turns and abrupt changes in direction, traveling at a high rate of speed. At one point, the object stopped suddenly, hovered for a few seconds, and then accelerated rapidly, disappearing from sight.
3. The observers reported that the object was tracked on the airport's radar scope during the entire period of observation. The radar returns indicated that the object made several rapid changes in speed and direction.
4. After the object disappeared from sight, the observer immediately reported the incident to the 75th Air Police Squadron and to the Federal Aviation Agency. Both agencies expressed interest in the sighting, and requested that a detailed report be submitted.
5. It is the opinion of the observer that the object sighted was not a conventional aircraft, and that its performance characteristics were beyond the capabilities of any known aircraft.
