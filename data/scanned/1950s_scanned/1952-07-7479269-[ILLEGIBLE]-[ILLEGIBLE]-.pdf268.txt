METER stated that at approximately 0930 hours on 1 July 1952, the students under his supervision observed two unconventional aircraft, a number of which were jet propelled, when two unidentified objects were sighted at a higher altitude.
METER further stated that one of these objects appeared to suspend motion for a period of approximately 5 minutes after which it took off in a southeasterly direction. He stated that these objects displayed a speed in excess of the conventional aircraft presently used.
At approximately 0933 hours on 1 July 1952, Sheldon P. de BAAL, Agent, G-2, Fort Monmouth, transmitted via telephone the information received from METER to Capt. KILPATRICK, ACOFS, G-2, Headquarters, 1st Army, Governors Island, New York.

At approximately 0938 hours, Agent de BAAL recontacted METER and was assured by METER that the 12 men comprising his class of instruction had visually sighted these two objects emitting from the unconventional aircraft within the immediate vicinity was missing from the conventional aircraft.
