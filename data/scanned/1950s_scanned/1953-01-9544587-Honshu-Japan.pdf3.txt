AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

DIR OF INTEL HDQTS AAC 259 IRT L-33 APR 2 1953 

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects Observed Over Honshu, Japan 31 Oct 1952

9 Nov 1952 ATIC 25271-T:
1. The object reportedly observed over Honshu, Japan on 31 Oct 1952 has been the subject of a previous intelligence report (L-33). The information contained in this report is amplified and clarified as follows:

2. The object was observed by numerous Japanese citizens, airline and military pilots, and Korean security personnel on the night of 31 Oct 1952. Statements indicate that the object was of large size, approximately 50 to 100 feet in diameter, and emitting a bright light or glow.
The object was observed over Honshu for approximately 30 minutes before disappearing at high speed.
3. The object did not resemble conventional aircraft and no similar objects have been reported in the past by observers in this area.
Its peculiar characteristics and manner of flight indicate this object to be of unusual nature. Further investigation would be necessary to determine the actual nature of the object.

4:4 D. TIHIANDER, CAPT, USAF
