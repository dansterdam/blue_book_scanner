UNCLASSIFIED
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
12-53
IDENTIFICATION OF FLYING OBJECTS
27 January 1953, 1520Z, in the vicinity of P-13, Suwon, Korea, source observed two (2) unidentified flying objects for a period of three (3) to five (5) minutes.

(I) Course: These objects were round, approximately below (12) to fifteen (15) feet in diameter, gray or white in color, and hovering at an altitude of about 3,000 feet.

(II) Characteristics: The objects were motionless and exhibited no visible means of propulsion. No smoke, trail, or exhaust was observed. No sound was heard. The objects were moving considerably faster than F-80 type aircraft in the area at the time.

(Ill) Observation Conditions: The weather was clear, visibility was excellent. The objects were observed in the daylight.

At the time of sighting, one element of the (2) P-13 aircraft in the flight pattern to the right of the observer and directly underneath the objects, was on his left at low altitude. No unusual meteorological conditions prevailed at the time of sighting.

A. HOWARD, JR.
Capt, USAF
D/I, 51st FIW
