SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects
TO: Commanding Officer, 81st AAA Bn, South Park Mil Res, Broughton, Pennsylvania.
DATE: 15 March 1954
1. Description of incident: At approximately 7:45 AM on 15 March 1954 two unidentified flying objects were observed by the following personnel of this unit:
Capt Carroll L. Smith, Radar Officer
Cpl J. M. Berkheimer, Radar Operator
PFC Joseph C. Marchesni, Radar Operator
PFC Louis A. Bassili, Radar Operator
2. The objects were first observed at 0815 Roger time and observed for a total of 6 hours and 15 minutes.
3. All observations were made by electronic devices which are normally employed in the M-33-C FCS to engage targets.
4. This unit is located at approximately latitude 40°45' N, longitude 80°00' W. The elevation is approximately 1020 feet. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of 25,000 to 30,000 feet.
5. The observation was made by the following personnel assigned to this unit:
Capt Carroll L. Smith, Radar Officer, Qualified by on the job training
Cpl J. M. Berkheimer, Radar Operator, Qualified by on the job training
PFC Joseph C. Marchesni, Radar Operator, Qualified by on the job training
PFC Louis A. Bassili, Radar Operator, Qualified by on the job training
