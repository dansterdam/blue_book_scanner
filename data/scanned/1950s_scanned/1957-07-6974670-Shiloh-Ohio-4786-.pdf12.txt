CIRCUMSTANCES OF SIGHTING

On June 25, 1957, at approximately 9:30 p.m., the witness, M.J. LARRISON, observed a bright object in the sky, southeast of his residence in Shiloh, Ohio. The object was observed for approximately 30 to 40 minutes and was described as a brilliant white light that changed in intensity several times during the observation. The object was observed to be suspended in the sky and made no sound. The object disappeared from view gradually, fading into the background.

CONCLUSION
Based on the information gathered, this case is being filed as a Probable Astronomical Phenomenon. The object observed was most likely a very bright planet or star.
