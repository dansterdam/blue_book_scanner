AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
1/F SAIP, Stewart AFS, Newburgh, N-3-53
Page 2 of 3 pages
1. At approximately 0900 hours on 11 January 1953, Agent V. REID, Chief Agent, Counter Intelligence Corps, Fort Benning, Georgia, interviewed Lt. WILLIAM F. ADAMS, a student at the U.S. Army Finance School, who stated that on 10 January 1953 at approximately 1750 hours, while ADAMS was in vehicle 623 as a passenger on his way to ward 712, Post Hospital, Fort Monmouth, New Jersey, observed an Unidentified Flying Object in the sky, said object was oval in shape, orange in color and was traveling in a horizontal direction at a high rate of speed. ADAMS further stated that the object disappeared from view in approximately 3 seconds.

2. At approximately 0950 hours on 14 January 1953, Agent MAXINE BEETZ interviewed Miss JANE COLB, a student nurse at the Post Hospital, Fort Monmouth, New Jersey, who stated that at approximately 1750 hours on 13 January 1953, she was standing in front of ward 712 with several other student nurses when she observed an Unidentified Flying Object in the sky, said object was described as oval in shape, orange in color and was traveling at a high rate of speed in a horizontal direction. COLB further stated that the object disappeared from view in approximately 2 minutes after she first observed it.

3. It is stated that the object, in the opinion, was at an altitude of approximately 3,000 to 4,000 feet and was traveling at an estimated speed of 500 to 700 miles per hour. It was further stated that the object was reddish orange in color. Upon detection, the object appeared to be the size of a basketball and upon disappearance, the object was estimated to be approximately 1/2 of that size.

4. It is further stated that the object was observed for approximately 20 to 60 seconds, in the direction of New York City, passing over the Post Hospital in a horizontal direction.

5. It is stated that the object disappeared from view at approximately 1751 hours on 13 January 1953.

6. At approximately 1030 hours on 14 January 1953, Agent MAXINE BEETZ interviewed Sgt. JAMES J. MCMULLEN who stated that on 13 January 1953, while standing on the parapet in front of ward 712, he observed an Unidentified Flying Object in the sky which was circular in shape, orange in color and was traveling at an estimated speed of 1,700 to 2,000 miles per hour. MCMULLEN further stated that the object was observed for approximately 1 second before it disappeared from view.

7. It is stated that the only thing he noticed was that the object looked to him like a round ball, orange in color.

8. At approximately 1120 hours on 14 January 1953, Agent MAXINE BEETZ interviewed Cpl. CHARLES HEASLETT who stated that while on his way to his car, in front of Ward 712, he noticed an object in the sky which he described as oval in shape, orange in color, and approximately the size of a basketball. He further stated that the object was observed for approximately 2 minutes and then disappeared from view.

9. It is stated that the object disappeared from view at approximately 1750 hours on 13 January 1953.

10. It is stated that the object in his opinion was at an altitude of approximately 20,000 feet and traveling at a speed of approximately 700 to 1,000 miles per hour which is a speed of a conventional aircraft.
