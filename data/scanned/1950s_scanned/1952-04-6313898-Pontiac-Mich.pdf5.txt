SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL PHENOMENA
ON 29 APRIL 1952, AT 0830 HOURS THE FOLLOWING REPORT WAS SUBMITTED TO THE INTELLIGENCE OFFICER OF THE 4708TH DEFENSE WING CONCERNING SIGHTINGS OF UNCONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT OBSERVED ON 27 APRIL 1952:
A/2C HAROLD E. SKAGGS, OF THE AIR POLICE SQUADRON TOOK A CALL FROM A MR. ROBERT ZWICKLE OF PONTIAC, MICHIGAN, WHO REPORTED THAT HE AND SEVERAL OTHER PERSONS HAD OBSERVED A STRANGE OBJECT IN THE SKY AND HAD PHOTO-GRAPHED IT. THE OBJECT WAS DESCRIBED AS BRILLIANT WHITE IN COLOR AND WAS IN A BUSINESS ESTABLISHMENT WHERE THEY CONDUCTED SOME PHOTOGRAPHIC WORK. MR. ZWICKLE WAS DRIVING HIS CAR WHEN HE FIRST SIGHTED THE OBJECT WHICH WAS HOVERING OVER THE AREA. HE IMMEDIATELY STOPPED HIS CAR, CALLED SEVERAL OTHER PERSONS, AND THEY ALL OBSERVED THE OBJECT FOR SEVERAL MINUTES. IT WAS OBSERVED TO SUDDENLY DISAPPEAR AND THEN REAPPEAR AGAIN IN A DIFFERENT POSITION. PHOTOGRAPHS WERE TAKEN BUT HAVE NOT BEEN DEVELOPED. MR. ZWICKLE STATED HE WOULD FORWARD THE NEGATIVES TO THIS HEADQUARTERS AS SOON AS THEY WERE DEVELOPED.
                                    ANDREW J. CORNIA
                                    CAPTAIN, U.S.A.F.
                                    ASSISTANT INTELLIGENCE OFFICER
                                    4708TH DEFENSE WING
