DATE: 13 JUNE 1952
12. Extracted from FEAF "Interim" #717, dtd 0620/t, 13 June 1952:
Enemy balloons: Recently several reports have been received concerning the sighting of an unidentified object having a primary color of white changing to red, then one [one] is silver, and they are located at an altitude between 10,000 and 20,000 feet. On 15 May between the hours of 2102/I and 2206/I several were sighted and observed as being off-shaped or round, at an altitude estimated between 10,000 ft. and 16,000 feet, and in one case it was reported to have parked vertically for a distance. This movement was observed through a battery commander's scope and measured for 10 miles of travel. On the occasion when the objects were seen and observed, ground control was informed but the Air Technical Intelligence specialist and an ordnance Technical Intelligence specialist at OGII 55/1 June and saw a balloon being raised and lowered on a cable. Some of the previous sightings of unidentified objects have occurred in the same general area and the suspected for [for] a free balloon. The purpose of raising and lowering a balloon so near the front lines is not apparent, but could possibly be a method of communication.
(EVALUATION: B-3)
