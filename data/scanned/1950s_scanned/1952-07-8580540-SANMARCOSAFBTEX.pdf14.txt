Headquarters 29th AD.F.
Washington 25, D.C.

1. In accordance with paragraph 7a, AFR 200-2, 29 April 1952, the following report is submitted:

a. A single object in the shape of a perfect circle, giving off a brilliant light, was observed with a following trail or blue haze. It has been sighted in this vicinity. No sound was audible, and no trail was left.

b. The object was observed approaching seeming. The object was estimated to be traveling at a speed of approximately 700 to 1000 miles per hour. The object was observed to make a slight change of direction.

c. Object was sighted at 2240 hours CST, 21 July 1952 and was observed for approximately sixty seconds.

d. Object was observed visually without the aid of optical or electronic instruments.

e. Object was observed overhead by persons located at 29° 52' North, 97° 56' West, San Marcos gunnery range, on a southerly heading.

f. Competent observers were not rated per qualified observers and conflicting statements (attached) make analysis difficult.

g. Weather observation 2230 COT, 21 July 1952: 2300, scattered, visibility 10 miles, temperature 73°, dew point 67°.
