UNCLASSIFIED
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
DISTRIBUTION: TWX - 006
SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECT OBSERVED IN SAN ANTONIO, TEXAS AREA ON 6 NOVEMBER 1954
1. ON 6 NOVEMBER 1954, AT APPROXIMATELY 0745 HOURS, A STUDENT PILOT AND HIS INSTRUCTOR FLYING IN A T-33 AIRCRAFT NEAR SAN ANTONIO, TEXAS, OBSERVED AN UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECT.
2. THE OBJECT WAS FIRST SIGHTED IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT OF THE AIRCRAFT'S WINDSCREEN. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE OBLONG IN SHAPE, MANEUVERABLE, AND FLYING AT A VERY HIGH SPEED. THE OBJECT WAS ESTIMATED TO BE TRAVELING AT APPROXIMATELY 3 TO 4 TIMES THE SPEED OF THE T-33 WHICH WAS FLYING AT 400 KNOTS.
3. THE OBJECT MAINTAINED ITS COURSE FOR APPROXIMATELY 20 SECONDS, THEN MADE A VERY SHARP, ALMOST INSTANTANEOUS, TURN OF APPROXIMATELY 90 DEGREES TO THE LEFT.
4. AFTER THE TURN, THE OBJECT ACCELERATED RAPIDLY AND DISAPPEARED FROM SIGHT.
5. NEITHER THE STUDENT PILOT NOR THE INSTRUCTOR PILOT WAS ABLE TO OBTAIN A PHOTOGRAPH OF THE OBJECT.
6. THE SIGHTING WAS REPORTED IMMEDIATELY UPON LANDING.

UNCLASSIFIED
