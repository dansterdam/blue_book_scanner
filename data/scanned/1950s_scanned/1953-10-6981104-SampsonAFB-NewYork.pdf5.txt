AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
REPORT NO.: FTD-53-4/l REPORT SERIAL NO.: 6981104
DATE: 9 October 1953
ORGANIZATION: Sampson AFB, Geneva, N.Y.
LOCATION: Sampson AFB, Geneva, N.Y. 
DATE-TIME GROUP: 7 October 1953
WITNESS(ES): Capt. J.M. GILLIS, U.S.A.F.
SUMMARY (General Observations and Analysis): 
This report contains information of the observation of a bright silver object from Sampson AFB Hospital, Geneva, N.Y. at 1123 and 1143 hours, 7 October 1953.
The first witness, Capt. J.M. GILLIS, U.S.A.F., reported first by telephone that the object appeared to be traveling at a high rate of speed and was visible for approximately 20-25 seconds. Captain GILLIS could not estimate the altitude or size of the object, but described it as being bright silver in color and appearing to be cigar-shaped. He stated the object was moving in a level flight path and appeared to be traveling in a straight line. Captain GILLIS stated the object seemed to disappear rather than land or take off.
The second witness, a young girl, was reported to have observed the object and stated that it appeared to be much larger than a jet aircraft and had a spinning motion. No estimate of altitude or speed was given.
