AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

COUNTRY: U.S.A.
SUBJECT: Shaw AFB and Congaree AFB, South Carolina
DATE OF INFORMATION: 5 March 1953 
PREPARED BY: USAF, 3rd District Office of Special Investigations 

SUMMARY: A streak-shaped flying object or unidentifiable size was observed. The object appeared to descend and there was no sound observed.

1. The time of visual sighting was 1548 EST, 5 March 1953, for a period of approximately one hour.

2. The characteristics of the object were as follows: The object appeared to be streak-shaped, and was moving at a high rate of speed. It was observed by personnel of the 727th AC & W Squadron, located at Congaree AFB, S.C., and the 727th AC & W Squadron, located at Camden, S.C.

3. The object was observed visually by three officers and approximately fifteen airmen. The object appeared to descend and there was no sound observed. The weather at the time was clear and visibility was excellent.

4. The observer, Mr. J. Edward Jones, Assistant Director of Security, Savannah River Project, stated that the object appeared to be approximately 30,000 feet in altitude and the size was estimated to be approximately 20,000 square feet. The object appeared to be descending at a rapid rate.

5. The object was apparently a meteor, based on the triangulation and characteristics of the object observed. There was no evidence that the object was a powered device.

6. No other meteorological and other conditions reported which might account for the sighting.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS:
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
DOD DIR 5200.10

DIRECTOR OF INTELLIGENCE, HEADQUARTERS USAF, Washington 25, D.C.
