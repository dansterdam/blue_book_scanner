UNCLASSIFIED
UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
OFFICE OF THE DIRECTOR OF INTELLIGENCE 
REPORT OF INVESTIGATION

UNKNOWN SUBJECT: Unidentified Objects in Flight Over Lubbock, Texas

SPECIAL INQUIRY

SYNOPSIS: Spot Intelligence Report, DO #23, File 24-9, dated 7 September 1951.

Investigation prompted upon verbal request of Air Force Marshal and base intelligence officers, on 17 September 1951. Based upon report of numerous sightings of bright lights moving in formation over Lubbock, Texas, during first week of September 1951. Air Force Intelligence Command through Air Force District commander, advised detailed reports received concerning said events. Investigation concerning reportable unidentified objects sighted over Lubbock, Texas, during interval of 3 September 1951 to date, held DIC direction via JPPRS. Intelligence report, file 24-9, dated 7 September 1951, copies of which have been furnished Air Force District Commander.

DETAILS: On 21 August 1951, numerous citizens in Lubbock, Texas, observed a formation of 20 to 30 bright lights, approximately 2,000 feet in altitude, moving in a generally northerly direction at an estimated speed of 600 to 800 MPH. Object sighted for approximately 3 minutes. Visibility 15 miles, temperature 77 degrees, wind from the east at 5-10 MPH. Direction of flight was across the northern part of Lubbock, Texas, to a point approximately 5 miles north of Lubbock, Texas, where objects were lost from view. This incident was observed by several individuals including W.I. Odum, assistant professor of civil engineering, Texas Technological College, Lubbock, Texas; Mr. John W. MacKay, professor of biology, Texas Technological College, Lubbock, Texas; and Mr. W.L. Lewis, resident of Lubbock, Texas.

Further inquiries at Reese Air Force Base, Lubbock, Texas, indicate that no unusual aircraft activity was observed during evening of 21 August 1951. Residents in area north of Lubbock, Texas, interviewed, stated they observed a similar formation on the evening of 25 August 1951, at approximately 9:30 p.m. Visibility estimates, descriptions, and direction of flight same as on 21 August 1951.

Additional observations were made on 30 August 1951, at approximately 9:00 p.m. on objects similar to those seen on 21 and 25 August 1951. Objects were observed until approximately 9:30 p.m. Several photographs and motion pictures were obtained by residents of Lubbock, Texas, and forwarded to Air Force District Commander for evaluation. Negatives and photographs were obtained by AFOSI and forwarded to Air Technical Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio, for evaluation.
