SUBJECT: Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object at Philadelphia, Pa. Between 2110 hours and 2300 hours, Eastern Daylight Time.

1. DETAILS OF INVESTIGATION AND REPORTS INCLUDING PERTINENT EVENTS:

TO: Director, Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. SYNOPSIS: Interviewed on 30 April 1955 he had sighted an unidentified object at Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania, on 27 April 1955. The object was a bright light, varying in color and shape. Report from U.S. newspaper indicates object seen on 27 April 1955.

2. DETAILS: On 28 April 1955, was interviewed at his place of employment, the Public Corporation, 7700 Wissinghon Avenue, Philadelphia, Pa. He stated on the evening of 27 April 1955 that he had sighted an unidentified flying object. He first noticed the object around 2110 hours and observed it until approximately 2230 hours. He said it was a bright light, varying in color from white to orange to reddish-white. Sometimes it appeared as a single, stationary light, and at other times as two or more separate objects, sometimes appearing to move rapidly and at other times remaining stationary. According to the witness, the object was between the size of a basketball and a baseball and hovered in the sky. It appeared to flash and spread out like a distant star, with evidence of color change. The witness estimated the object was approximately 500 yards from his position and could notice no sound coming from it. He made a sketch of the object that he had observed. He said he had notified the local police station on the evening of 27 April 1955, and that the police had investigated but could not determine what the object was.
