SUPPLEMENT TO AF FORM 112
REPORT NO: DD-INTDZB-3-55 THREE PAGE 5 OF 12 PAGES

1. 30/25002 JUN 55: Investigator's statement: At 6:30 PM, on 30 June 1955, I was alerted at my office in New York City, N.Y. The sighting had been reported to the Filter Center by the Eastern Air Defense Command at Stewart AFB, Newburgh, N.Y.

I immediately went to the site of the sighting, arriving at 7:10 PM. The witnesses, two Air Force officers, stated that they had observed an unusual object in the sky. According to their statements, the object was first observed at approximately 6:30 PM, and was tracked by the radar at Stewart AFB from 6:30 to 7:00 PM. The object was described as circular in shape, metallic in appearance, and approximately the size of a B-29 aircraft. It was reported to have been moving at a very high rate of speed, and to have made abrupt changes in direction. The radar at Stewart AFB had obtained negative results for identification, but it did not indicate the presence of the object.

2. RADAR DATA: The 12th Weather Squadron of Stewart AFB, Newburgh, N.Y. furnished the following summary of weather conditions for 16/0002Z MAY 25: RADAR CONTACT LOST AT 25.000
FEET ALTITUDE DUE TO RAIN SHOWERS.

Interviewed in: New York City, N.Y.
Statement by: Capt. H.J. Anone, U.S.A.F. The witness stated that he and another officer were standing outside the control tower at Stewart AFB, Newburgh, N.Y. when they observed an unusual object in the sky. The object was circular in shape, metallic in appearance, and approximately the size of a B-29 aircraft. It was moving at a very high rate of speed and made abrupt changes in direction. The object was observed for approximately 30 minutes, from 6:30 to 7:00 PM on 30 June 1955.
