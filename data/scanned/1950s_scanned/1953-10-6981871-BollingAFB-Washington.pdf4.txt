AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
REPORT NO.   12-3-53 
OFFICE SYMBOL: AFOIN
PLACE: Bolling Air Force Base, Washington
DATE: 3 September 1953

1. Report is a description of an unidentified object submitted by 1/Lt. George
V/pp Zokaris, USAF. The object was a spherical, white illuminated object, approximately 30 feet in diameter, which was observed for approximately 20 to 25 minutes.

2. The location of the observers was from the yard of a grade school located at Bolling Air Force Base, Washington.

3. Identifying information of the observers is as follows:
1/Lt. George V. Zokaris, AF11779259, USAF Band member, Bolling Air Force Base.
1/Sgt. James E. Stark, AF11779274, USAF Band member, Bolling Air Force Base.
1/Sgt. Frank T. Keaney, AFS217177559, USAF Band member, Bolling Air Force Base.

4. Weather conditions at the time of the sighting were described by the observers as clear and calm.

5. No specific maneuvers or actions of the object were observed which might account for the sighting. The object appeared to hover and then move away at a high rate of speed.

6. It was found that the preparing officer, Lt. Col. A.J. Kulick, USAF, was absent from the office due to illness at the time the report was submitted. The final disposition of this report is unknown.
