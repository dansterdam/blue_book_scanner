FROM: T. WALLACE REPORT
SUBJECT: Unidentified Aircraft

TO: COMMANDING GENERAL
MILITARY AIR TRANSPORT SERVICE
WASHINGTON 25, D. C.

REFERENCE: Director of Special Investigations, Headquarters, United States Air Force,
Washington 25, D. C.

1. EXHIBIT: Ground sighting aboard U. S. ATTU of object at 19.57 hours, 21 February 1951, described as cylindrical in shape and about 40 to 50 feet in length. Object was observed to be lighted as if by internal illumination. This object was observed at a distance of approximately 7.5 miles.

2. DETAIL: Sighting was made by Lt. D. H. SILL, USN, from the bridge of the USS ATTU (ACV 33), 1st Naval District, under date of 21 February 1951. Object was sighted at 19:57 hours at an azimuth of approximately 15 degrees and an elevation of approximately 30 degrees. Course of object appeared to be from approximately northwest to southeast and at an estimated speed of 1500 to 2000 knots. Object was observed for a period of approximately 150 seconds, disappearing at an azimuth of approximately 85 degrees and an elevation of approximately 10 degrees. Weather was clear, visibility unlimited.

Interviewed by Lt. D.H. SILL, USN, at Pearl Harbor, T.H. on 24 February 1951, No formal statement submitted. This exhibit is classified CONFIDENTIAL.
