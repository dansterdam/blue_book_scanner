"CONFIDENTIAL

SUBJECT: Report of Unidentified Aerial Objects
TO: Commanding General

Dayton, Ohio

1. This is in accordance with AFCI 200-2 dated 14 April 1952, which requires the reporting of all unidentified aerial objects observed within the United States to tais headquarters.

2. The information below was gathered by the undersigned and observed first hand, rather than as a hearsay as in the reported.

3. As mentioned, tnis Airforce object was reported to 82/4 airway air traffic control point and several locations at the Minneapolis St. Paul International Airport (Wold-Chamberlain Field), very early on the morning of 22 April 1952. The control tower operator on duty and the base operations officer on duty at that time confirmed the object visually.

4. To satisfy his own curiosity, the undersigned proceeded to the outskirts of Fargo, North Dakota, about 4000 hours on 22 April 1952, equipped vith a pair of 7x50 binoculars.

5. As to the 21.5 hours several trails were observed, hovever, no object was seen visually. Also illuminated trails covering a distance of about 3 miles were observed, but no aircraft was spotted causing such illumination. This together with the strange illumination, thus causing the strange illumination.

6. Nothing characteriatic of the reported object could be seen with the binoculars. The only observations were the illumlnated trails as described.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS: PAUL J. SANDERS
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS DOD DIR 5200.10
Statistician, Commanding
