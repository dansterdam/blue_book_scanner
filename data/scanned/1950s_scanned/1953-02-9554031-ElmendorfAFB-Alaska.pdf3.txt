As approximately 13/0453, February 1953, an unidentified red light was sighted in the vicinity of Elmendorf AFB by the following members of the 23rd Bomb Sq, 21st Bomb Wg, 10th Air Div (Def), Elmendorf AFB, Alaska: MAJOR WILBUR L. MORRIS, CAPT LOUIS R. RASCHKE, 1st LT DONALD R. TAYLOR, 1st LT THOMAS W. STOLL, M/SGT GEORGE M. DESJARDINS, and S/SGT NORMAN C. MOATS.
The object is described as a large red light, estimated to be 1,000-1,500 yards in distance. The light remained
stationary for approximately five (5) minutes, then dimmed and disappeared in a northerly direction.
The object was described as oblong in shape, having an apparent size of 25-30 feet in length and 15-20 feet in width. The light was brilliant and fluctuated in intensity several times during the observation. The angular elevation of the object ranged from 10 to 15 degrees.
The duration of the sighting was five (5) to ten (10) minutes. No weather balloons were released during the period. All persons who could have knowledge of this sighting have been interrogated. The flight remains unidentified.
