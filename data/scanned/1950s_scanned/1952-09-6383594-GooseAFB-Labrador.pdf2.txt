AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
17 OCTOBER 1952, REIDSVILLE, NORTH CAROLINA
INCIDENT WITNESSED BY: J. P. BARKELL, MAJOR, USAF
SUMMARY:
- A radar signal was observed that resembled an aircraft and exhibited a clear, steady track. However, no aircraft was visually confirmed.
- The object was estimated to be at an altitude of 3,500 feet and traveling at a speed of 300 knots.
- Weather conditions at the time were scattered clouds at 4,500 feet.
- All aircraft in the area were accounted for.
The report is signed by FREDERICK W. CASTLE, Major, USAF, Chief, Evaluation Division.
