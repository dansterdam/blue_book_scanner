AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
1. On 17 April 1952, the Michigan State Highway Patrol received a report that a light was sighted at approximately 1600 hours on that date and at a point approximately 4 1/2 miles east of Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan, on U.S. Highway 28. The light was observed for approximately eight (8) minutes and was described as a bluish-green color and estimated to be from 75 to 100 feet in length. 
2. The light was observed by a number of people and investigation revealed that no other person or persons had seen the phenomenon. The temperature at the time of the sighting was fifty (50) degrees.
COMMENTS of Preparing Officer:
a. The Preparing Officer is of the opinion that the object sighted could be:
(1) Weather balloon
b. Kite
c. Friendly aircraft
d. Sun reflected from distant cloud
e. Sun reflected from Lake Superior
f. Sun reflected from what passed over St. Mary's River Falls at Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan.
