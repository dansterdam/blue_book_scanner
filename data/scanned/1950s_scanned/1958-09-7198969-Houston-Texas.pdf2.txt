1. On 29 September 1958, Ellington Air Force Base received a report from Doctor---, that he had sighted an unidentified object. Doctor--- stated that he had observed this object in accordance with paragraph 15, AFR 200-2.

a. Description of the object:
(1) Shape: round with a fan shaped tail. 
(2) Size: slightly smaller than a full moon but larger than any star or planet.
(3) Color: bright orange.
(4) Number: one (1).
(5) Sound: ------ stated that no sound emitted from the object.
(6) No other pertinent or unusual features were noted.

b. Description of course of object:
(1) Object stated that he had gone to his back yard to feed his dogs. He stated that the object was so large and unusual that he was distracted by its presence in the sky.
(2) He used a small compass to plot the object's azimuth and estimate its angle of elevation. The first sighting was at an azimuth of 330°. Angle of elevation 15 - 20 degrees at disappearance on an azimuth of 240°.
(3) Flight path was a continuous straight flight path from northeast to southwest. No maneuvers were observed. Object was visible continuously.
