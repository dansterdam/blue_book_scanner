THE FOLLOWING IS A SUMMARY OF THE DETAILS AND CONCLUSIONS REACHED IN THE CASE OF THE "UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT" REPORTED ON DECEMBER 6, 1954 AT GULFPORT, MISSISSIPPI, BY COLONEL GEORGE A. ELLIOTT, DEPUTY DISTRICT ENGINEER, GULFPORT, MISSISSIPPI

The report stated that on the evening of DECEMBER 6, 1954, COLONEL GEORGE A. ELLIOTT, DEPUTY DISTRICT ENGINEER, GULFPORT, MISSISSIPPI, OBSERVED AN UNIDENTIFIED OBJECT IN THE SKY SOUTHWEST OF GULFPORT, MISSISSIPPI. THE OBJECT WAS FIRST SIGHTED AT APPROXIMATELY 7:30 PM. IT APPEARED TO BE BRIGHT IN COLOR AND MOVING RAPIDLY ACROSS THE SKY. AFTER OBSERVING IT FOR APPROXIMATELY 10 MINUTES, IT DISAPPEARED FROM VIEW. COLONEL ELLIOTT STATED THAT HE WAS UNABLE TO DETERMINE THE ALTITUDE, SIZE OR EXACT NATURE OF THE OBJECT.

THE OBJECT WAS REPORTED TO HAVE BEEN OBSERVED BY VARIOUS OTHER PERSONS IN THE GULFPORT AREA, BUT NO OTHER EYEWITNESS STATEMENTS WERE OBTAINED.

BASED ON THE LIMITED INFORMATION AVAILABLE, IT WAS CONCLUDED THAT THE OBJECT OBSERVED BY COLONEL ELLIOTT WAS PROBABLY A METEOR OR SOME OTHER NATURAL PHENOMENA. NO EVIDENCE WAS FOUND TO INDICATE THAT THE OBJECT WAS OF EXTRATERRESTRIAL OR UNNATURAL ORIGIN.
