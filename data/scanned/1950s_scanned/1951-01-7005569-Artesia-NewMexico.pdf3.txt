51-B-129 OBJECT OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS

Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, U. S. Air Force
Washington 25, D. C.

On 11 January 1951 an unidentified aerial object was sighted by five (5) persons while driving in the Cottonwood, New Mexico area. At the time of the observation, the object appeared to be round, white, and luminous. It was stationary for approximately 20 minutes, at an altitude of 2000 to 4000 feet. Two cylinders which appeared to be smoke trails were observed trailing from the object. When the object began to move, it traveled at a high rate of speed and in a straight line. Five (5) civilian employees of the NMA Division, Holloman AFB, New Mexico confirmed this observation. At the time of the observation, no weather balloons were known to be afloat in the vicinity of the controlled area. It is believed that no weather balloons were aloft at the above time.

Telephone, the weather bulletin furnished by the U. S. Weather Bureau, Knoxville, Tennessee, indicates visibility on the day of observation was "excellent" at 1500 hours. Based on flight testimony, this object was observed for 25 to 30 minutes, whereas State Police witnessed the object for 15 to 20 minutes.

As further incidents of this type are reported, Additional information will be forwarded.

PATRICK W. HAYES
CAP CAPTAIN, USAF
District Commander
