AF FORM 112--PART II
APPROVED 1 JUNE 1948

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SUBJECT: Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object

IR-30-52

As 2255 hours 22 July 1952, an unidentified object was observed by military guard personnel at Ellsworth AFB, South Dakota. The observation lasted approximately 3 minutes.

The object appeared to be a long blue flash, four to five times as long as twice as thick, as 035 type aircraft. There was no definite outline and no audible sound.

The object was in sight approximately 3 seconds, traveling at a very high rate of speed, at an estimated altitude of 5000 to 6000 feet. 

The object was flying straight and level, from southeast to northwest.

In the weather at time of sighting was CAVU with scattered cumulus clouds.

The observer has had no previous experience in aircraft observation.

No interceptions were made and no photographs were taken.

A-33 type aircraft from this base were flying in the vicinity during several exercises. Additional aircraft from Ellsworth AFB and Rapid City Air Force Base were reported in this area at time of observation.

UNCLASSIFIED
