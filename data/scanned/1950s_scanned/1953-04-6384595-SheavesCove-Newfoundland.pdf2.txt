NORTHEAST AIR COMMAND
MITCHEL AIR FORCE BASE

1. Concerning the report of an unidentified flying object observed on the night of 21 March 1953 in the vicinity of Sheaves Cove, Newfoundland.

2. Reference is made to the report submitted by the Commanding Officer, Auxiliary Air Force Station, Torbay, Newfoundland, on 23 March 1953.

3. Investigation 3: Interviews were conducted with the observers and other witnesses in the area. Statements were obtained from the following individuals:

(Names and other identifying information have been redacted)

4. On the night of 21 March 1953 at approximately 2300 hours, the observers, who were at home, observed a brightly illuminated object moving in the sky from the southwest to the northeast at a high rate of speed. The object was described as being round in shape, emitting a bright white light, and appeared to be at an altitude of approximately 5,000 feet. The object was under observation for approximately 30 seconds before disappearing from sight.

5. Subsequent checks were made with the Royal Canadian Air Force, the Royal Canadian Navy, and the United States Air Force to determine if any aircraft were in the area at the time of the sighting. No aircraft were known to be in the vicinity.
