II. RELIABILITY: SOURCES were not personally interviewed by investigators and therefore their reliability is unknown. SOURCES' statements were submitted through 26th Air Division to EODF. The investigators obtained these statements from EADF.

III. SOURCES' DESCRIPTION OF SIGHTING:
1. SOURCES were pilot and copilot on Colonial Airlines Flight 2 on the night of 6 Sep 55. They were flying at an altitude of 6,000 degrees over Poughkeepsie, NY.
2. At 07/0025z Sep 55 SOURCES sighted an unidentified object ahead and slightly above their position. The object had a blue white exhaust flame and was moving at a speed of about 200 to 210 degrees in about a 15 to 20 degree angle.
3. The object was observed for about 3 seconds and disappeared off the airliner's left wing tip at which time it was emitting a lot of exhaust as well as a blue white flame and blinking flash.
4. SOURCES stated that two other airliners also observed the flash of light, one of them located over Albany, NY (UNKNOWN AIRLINES) and one approximately 10 miles north of Poughkeepsie.
5. SOURCES stated that the object was not a meteor and that its speed was in excess of 400 to 500 mph.
