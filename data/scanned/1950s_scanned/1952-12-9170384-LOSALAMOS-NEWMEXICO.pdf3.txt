AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
REPORT NO. Z-12-52
DATE: 9 December 1952
SUBJECT: Sighting of a large jet exhaust
LOCATION: Los Alamos, New Mexico
(1) Summary: At approximately 1345 hours, 9 December 1952, an Air Force observer sighted the exhaust of a large jet aircraft in the vicinity of Los Alamos, New Mexico. The exhaust was observed for approximately 10 minutes, then disappeared from view behind some mountains.
(2) Air Division (Materiel) concluded that the exhaust was probably caused by a high altitude jet aircraft.
(3) Other Factors:
(a) Exact time and location of observation: Approximately 1345 hours, 9 December 1952, 3-4 miles north of Los Alamos, New Mexico.
(b) Confidential Section of Los Alamos and Installation - About 3 miles north of Crossroads.
(c) Obscured visibility, considered excellent. "Observer is employed and trained as an observer."
(d) Clear weather. No mirage visibility. Wind: 14 knots at surface.
(7), (8), (9), and (10) all negative.
COMMENTS of Preparing Officer: The observer was a highly reliable Air Force officer stationed at Los Alamos. The object was observed visually and appeared to be the exhaust of a large jet aircraft at high altitude.
