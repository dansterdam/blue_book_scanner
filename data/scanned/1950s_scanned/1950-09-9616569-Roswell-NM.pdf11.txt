SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Reports of Unconventional Aircraft 
TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

SYNOPSIS: Recent reports of sightings of unconventional aircraft:

1. A simultaneous sighting by father and son, residents of New Orleans, on 10 October 1950, of a mysterious bright object at a great altitude.

2. Twelve (12) bright, round, diving and spinning objects seen from a window of New Orleans on 11 October 1950.

3. The Shreveport Times of 7 November 1950, in a special report from Jonesville, described hovering objects seen by members of a farmer's family on 6 November 1950.

4. A letter to the editor of The Times-Picayune, New Orleans, Louisiana, on 15 October 1950, described flying saucers observed by two (2) Miami Airlines, Inc. pilots on the preceding Sunday.

DETAILS:
1. On 15 October 1950, a Special Agent of the Office of Special Investigations, on 15 and 24 October 1950, respectively, and reported, a simultaneous sighting of an unconventional flying object. Details as submitted by the LARRIER's:
(1) Time 0716 hours, 10 October 1950. Object was under observation for five (5) to ten (10) minutes.
