SUBJECT: Report of Information on Unconventional Aircraft

TO:     Commanding General
        Air Materiel Command
        ATTN: MCIS
        Wright-Patterson Air Force Base

1. In accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 4 and 5, Air Defense Command Letter 200-3, dated 11 April 1951, subject as above, the following report of an unconventional aircraft or objects, which was sighted over this field, is forwarded for your information:

a. Description- The object was elliptical and flat in appearance, similar to a large silver disc or perhaps a thin aircraft wing. It was light silver in color. No noise nor visible exhaust were present. The object moved over the airfield from the northeast and crossed on a southwesterly heading at a greatly accelerated pace, hovered momentarily, then descended and landed on the field and then ascended rapidly out of sight at approximately a 60 degree angle.

b. The object was sighted at approximately 0918P, 29 May 1951. Duration of sight, approximately 4 minutes. It disappeared from sight in approximately 30 seconds after it started to ascend.

c. The object was sighted with the naked eye by observers on the ground. No aircraft was in the air over the field at the time, nor were radar observations attempted.

d. Source of information is reliable and authentic, 2 officers (pilots) and 2 airmen reported seeing the object.
