SUMMARY: (Enter concise summary of report. Give significant in first one-sentence paragraph. List enclosures if any. Single line of report on AF Form 112--Part II.)

The objects were sighted at Lubbock, Texas at 9:15 pm on 22 Jul 52. They were seen by several citizens, including a teacher and some students. The objects appeared as a group of lights numbering from 30 to 50, moving in a formation and disappearing for short periods. The objects were soundless and appeared to be more than normal. The exact nature and identity of the objects are unknown.

The objects were observed again at Lubbock, Texas at 9:45 pm on July 22, 1952. They appeared as before, disappearing and reappearing in the southern sky. Their movements were erratic and fairly high in the sky.

The objects were again observed at Lubbock, Texas at 11:20 pm on July 22, 1952. They behaved as on the previous two occasions.

The sightings were reported to the 4925th Air Intelligence Squadron at Carswell AFB, Texas, and the 8th Air Rescue Squadron at Carswell AFB, Texas. Photographs were taken but have not been developed.
