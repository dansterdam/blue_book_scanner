AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
REPORT NO. 7-25-53
PASADENA, TEXAS, 23 MARCH 1953
WHO: PATRICIA D. LIEBERT, A/C 17939020.
WHAT: OBSERVATION OF AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT, AS FOLLOWS:
ON THE EVENING OF 23 MARCH 1953, BETWEEN THE HOURS OF 20:00 AND 22:00 WHILE ON DUTY AS A PROJECT OPERATOR ON THE DATI NET ON THE SECOND FLOOR OF THE AIR INTELLIGENCE MISSILE CONTROL CENTER IN PASADENA, TEXAS, THE OBSERVER OBSERVED AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT IN THE SKY WHICH WAS ESTIMATED TO BE OVER 15 MILES AWAY FROM THE OBSERVER'S LOCATION. THE OBJECT WAS OBSERVED FOR A PERIOD OF APPROXIMATELY 3 MINUTES. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE A BRIGHT STEADY LIGHT WHICH CHANGED TO A BLINKING LIGHT AND THEN BACK TO A BRIGHT STEADY LIGHT.AT NO TIME DURING THE OBSERVATION WAS THERE ANY CHANGE IN THE OBJECT'S HEIGHT OR SPEED. THE OBSERVER STATES THAT THE OBJECT WAS UNLIKE ANYTHING SHE HAS EVER OBSERVED BEFORE AND SHE IS UNABLE TO IDENTIFY IT.
WHERE: PASADENA, TEXAS
WHEN: 23 MARCH 1953, 20:00-22:00 HOURS
INSTRUMENT: VISUAL OBSERVATION
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
For Leonard Durkess, A/2C, Air Police Section, 7726th
ON THE 23 MARCH 1953 BETWEEN THE HOURS OF 20:00 AND 22:00 WHILE ON DUTY AS A PROJECT OPERATOR ON THE DATI NET ON THE SECOND FLOOR OF THE AIR INTELLIGENCE MISSILE CONTROL CENTER IN PASADENA, TEXAS, AN OBJECT OVAL SHAPED, LARGER THAN A STAR AND BRIGHT BLUE GREEN IN COLOR WAS OBSERVED BY THE OBSERVER. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO CHANGE FROM A BLINKING LIGHT TO A STEADY LIGHT AND BACK TO A BLINKING LIGHT SEVERAL TIMES. THE OBJECT DID NOT APPEAR TO CHANGE IN ALTITUDE OR SPEED DURING THE ENTIRE OBSERVATION PERIOD OF APPROXIMATELY 3 MINUTES. THE OBSERVER IS UNABLE TO IDENTIFY THE OBJECT.
