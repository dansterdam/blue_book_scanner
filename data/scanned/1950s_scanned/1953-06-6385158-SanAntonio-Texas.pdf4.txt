AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT 

Commander, 3AANA 

On 29 April 1952, in accordance with AF letter 200-5, dated 29 April 1952, the following information is submitted:

(1) The object was saucer-shaped and smallin size. Only one object was observed. It was brownish-gray in color. The object moved back and forth erratically at a high rate of speed, sometimes pausing apparently stationary. It was silent and no trail or exhaust was observed. The object disappeared quickly, much in the same manner as it would a light-being extinguished. The elapsed time of observation was approximately 45 seconds.

(2) Object was observed 1945 hours, CST, 16 June 1953 for approximately five minutes. 

(3) A visual ground observation through 7x50 power binoculars was made.

(4) Observers were at their place of duty, the control tower at Kelly AFB, Texas. At the time of the sighting, the weather was clear and visibility was excellent.

(5) Observers were: 1/Lt Robert C. Sherwood, AF 1638301; A/2C Albert R. Kessel, AF 17107269; and A/1C William J. Paher, AF 15292492; all from the 1323rd AACS Unit stationed at Kelly AFB, Texas.

(6) Paet weather prevailed at the time of the sighting. Visibility was fifteen miles and temperature was approximately 75 degrees Fahrenheit. Six thousand feet reported.

(7) Compass sightings stated that due to the physical make up of the object that the sighting could be explained as the reflection of the sun on a high cirrus cloud.

(8) There is no physical evidence of the sighting.

(9) Local AC&W unit contacted with negative results. ATIC Center, San Antonio, Texas conducted VAS liaison with negative results.

(10) No air traffic reported in the general area at the time of the sighting.

DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
