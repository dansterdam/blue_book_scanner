AURORA BOREALIS
NORTH ATLANTIC
Mr. K. H. Weihlts, aboard the Panamanian SS "Patio, Capt. P. Thomkins, reports the following:
On 19 September 1953, in approximate position 45°23' N., 60°24' W., the radio operator observed northern lights were observed from 2230' to 0130' GMT. These lights were described as a slightly shimmering and straight wave or blue light moving from the horizon upwards. The radio operator reports no movement on short wave during this period.
Weather clear, wind about 10-15 knots, air temperature about 60-65 degrees Fahrenheit.
