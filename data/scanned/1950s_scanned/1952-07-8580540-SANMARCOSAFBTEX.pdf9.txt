AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT 
UNCLASSIFIED  

REPORT NO. TR-11-52  
DATE: 21 July 1952  
PAGES: 1  

1. In accordance with paragraph 7b, AFR 200-2, 29 April 1952, the following report is submitted:  

1. A single object in the shape of a flat disc, giving off a brilliant white light, was observed. The object was observed for approximately 10 to 15 seconds. It appeared to be moving at a tremendous speed, and no analysis was made of the propulsion system. The object was observed on 21 July 1952 and disappeared as though the flame or lights had been extinguished.  

2. Duration of sighting approximately thirty seconds.  

3. Object observed without the aid of optical or electronic instruments.  
Object was observed in azimuth at 097° 43' North and 097° 43' West. Object passed from observation on a southerly bearing.  

4. Statements made by observers are self-explanatory. 

5. Estimations of size, distance, temperature, and location: ILLEGIBLE  
Excellent clarity of observation.  

6. There was no known air traffic in the general area at the time of the sighting.  

2. Statements of observers are as follows:  

(1) Lt Col C.E. Moody, 0-402988, Personnel Officer, 322nd FTR Sq, 433rd FTR Wing, Shaw AFB, reported the following: On 21 July 1952 at about 2200 hours while in the control tower at Shaw AFB, SD, I observed a single disc-shaped white object. It appeared to be about 2000 to 3000 feet in elevation, flying at an extremely high rate of speed from north to south. The object was in view for approximately 10 to 15 seconds and then disappeared as though the flame or lights had been extinguished. At no time did I hear any sound. The weather was clear, the visibility excellent, and there were a few scattered clouds in the sky. The object was completely silent and showed no trail or exhaust of any kind.  

(2) Sgt Leon W. Thome, RA 14191501, Ground Control Approach Operator, 322nd FTR Sq, 433rd FTR Wing, Shaw AFB, stated: While in the control tower at Shaw AFB, SD, on 21 July 1952 at about 2200 hours, I observed a single white, flat, disc-shaped object. The object appeared to be at an elevation of approximately 2000 to 3000 feet and was travelling at an extremely high rate of speed from north to south. The object was in view for approximately 10 to 15 seconds and then disappeared as though the flame or lights had been extinguished. It was completely silent and left no trail or exhaust of any kind.
