SUPPLEMENT TO AF FORM 112
SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object
Charleston Air Force Base, S. C.
28 October 1958
On the evening of 28 October 1958, 2350 hours, an unidentified light was observed about 15 miles SE of Charleston Air Force Base. This object, falling quite rapidly from the sky, east of Charleston Air Force Base. These lights were different in that it showed no tail and no exhaust, as is normally associated with a meteor. The object descended to an altitude of approximately 800 to 1,000 feet, hovered over Runway 33, when they first observed what appeared to be a white ball of fire or a bright light of a few seconds duration. Then the object quickly accelerated and disappeared to the northeast at a tremendous rate of speed.

The object was observed on an approximate azimuth of 105 degrees and approximate angle of 30 degrees. No sound was heard and no trail or exhaust was observed. The duration of the sighting was approximately 2 to 3 minutes, no visual aids were used and the aircraft were observed in area at time of sighting.

A check with the following organizations revealed the following information: 
Operations: 1648th ATD Operations, Pope Operations, CAT Section, 363rd TW - Aircraft were airborne in local area, exact operations unknown. No air-to-air missiles fired, no air-to-air gun firing and no aircraft disappeared.

DEFENSE WARNING DIV, CHARLESTON AF STATION: No radar contacts or information regarding this sighting.
