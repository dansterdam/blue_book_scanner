DArE OF INFORMATION-JANK-6-.-1953
REPORT NO.—9544858
PLACE—SUWON. KOREA
SUBJECTS-UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT
1. DESCRIPTION OF OBJECT:
Object described as shining meteor or bright celestial object which increased in size as it approached the observer on the ground. No sound was heard. Object moved at high speed and disappeared from sight after a few seconds.
2. COURSE TAKEN BY OBJECT:
Object was first sighted to the northeast and moved in a westerly direction before disappearing from sight.
3. TIME OF OBSERVATION:
Object was observed sometime between 2000 and 2200 hours on 5 January, 1953.
4. METHOD OF OBSERVATION:
Observed by military personnel on the ground.
5. WEATHER CONDITIONS:
Weather was clear at the time of observation.
6. RELIABILITY OF WITNESS:
The observer is considered to be a reliable witness.
7. CONCLUSIONS:
The object was probably a meteor or some other natural celestial phenomenon. No further investigation seems warranted.
RANDOLPH AIR FORCE BASE, TEXAS 28 JAN 1953
