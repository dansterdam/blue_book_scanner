DEPARTMENT OF THE AIR FORCE
HEADQUARTERS UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
WASHINGTON

THE INSPECTOR GENERAL USAF
6TH DISTRICT OFFICE OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
ROBINS AIR FORCE BASE, GEORGIA

SUBJECT: Report of Unidentified Flying Object

TO: Commander
Dobbins Air Force Base
Marietta, Georgia

1. This is in confirmation of a conversation between the Base Commander, DOBBINS AFB and Mr. S. J. WALKER, Detachment Commander, Atlanta Detachment, Office of Special Investigations, concerning the sighting of three unidentified flying objects.

2. On 10 May 1954, Mr. BRANT, ASIG, Atlanta Field Office, Federal Bureau of Investigation, telephonically advised Mr. WALKER, that a "nuclear scientist, Georgia", had reported that three UFO's, estimated in his estimation, were not conventional type aircraft.

3. On 11 May 1954, Mr. WALKER was interviewed on this point, and he gave the following information which is set forth in the sequence outlined in AFR 200-2:

SHAPE: Round; flying in horizontal position in relation to earth.
SIZE: Estimated 20' diameter; thick in center, tapering to thin outer edges.
COLOR: Metallic luster.
FORMATION: Three objects, flying in a V formation.
AERODYNAMIC FEATURES: None recognized.
VAPOR TRAIL OR EXHAUST TRAIL: None.
MANEUVERING CHARACTERISTICS: Unknown.
ENGINE NOISE: None.
DURATION: None; straight flight.

UNCLASSIFIED
