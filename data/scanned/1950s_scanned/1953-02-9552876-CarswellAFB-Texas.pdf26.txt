7TH AFP WG, CARSWELL AFB, TEXAS
13 FEBRUARY 1953
2ND LT CHARLES F. SPRINGER
AF LTR 200-59 19TH AIRDIVISION MESSAGE 7A2 SUB, DTG 132332Z FEB 53
1. AT 0235 ON THE MORNING OF 13 FEB 1953, MR. [REDACTED] VISUALLY OBSERVED 3 VERY BRIGHT LIGHTS TRAVELING ACROSS THE SKY OVER FORT WORTH, TEXAS.
2. THE LIGHTS WERE FIRST OBSERVED APPROXIMATELY 500 FEET APART AND APPEARED STACKED IN VERTICAL ECHELON.
3. THE BRIGHT LIGHTS WERE OBSERVED IN A STEEP CLIMB AND ATTAINED AN ALTITUDE OF 20,000 FEET VERY RAPIDLY.
4. THE LIGHTS PICKED UP BLIPS ON THE SCOPE OF AN APN-14 RADAR ON B-36 AIRCRAFT 310, STATIONED AT 47TH BOMBARDMENT WING, CARSWELL AFB.
5. ONLY TWO OF THE 3 ORIGINAL LIGHTS ON THE APN-14 RADAR WERE A CONSIDERABLE DISTANCE FROM THE OTHER SHIP, WHICH WAS DISTINGUISHED EASILY.
6. BESIDES MR. [REDACTED], 3 OTHER MEMBERS OF DEPARTMENT 76 AT CONVAIR WITNESSED THIS INCIDENT.
