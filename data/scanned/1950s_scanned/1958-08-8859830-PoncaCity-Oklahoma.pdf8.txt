SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object Report

TO: Commander
Air Technical Intelligence Center
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton, Ohio

1. The following information concerning an unidentified flying object in this filter center area is submitted in accordance with instructions contained in AFR 200-2, dated 3 February 1958:

a. Description of the Object:
(1) Shape - Round. 
(2) Size compared to known object - One tenth the size of a dime, held in the hand at about arm's length. The observer also compared the object's size to a ball shot from a large German candle.
(3) Color - Bright, fiery orange.
(4) Number - One (1).
(5) Formation - Not applicable.
(6) Any discernible features or details - The observer said the object looked like a ball of fire, or bright, fiery light, orange in color and was travelling at a high rate of speed. He commented that the object appeared to be far in the distance, maybe 30 miles away. The observer further stated that he did not know if the object might be a satellite, a meteor or something else. He did not believe it was a meteor that he observed. However, he admitted never having actually seen a meteor or fireball background in astronomy or meteorology.
(7) Tail, Trail or Exhaust - The observer stated the object appeared to have a tail to it and, assuming it was 4 inch in diameter, the tail would be one (1) inch in length.
(8) Sound - None.

b. Description of Course of Object:
(1) What first called the attention of the observer - The observer had just closed his garage door, facing east, turned to the left and then noticed the fiery, bright object in the northeast traveling at terrific speed.
