SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Uncoventional Aircraft

THRU: Commander
Military Air Transport Service
Andrews Air Force Base
Washington 25, D. C.
ATTENTION: Inspector General (CGI)

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, United States Air Force
Washington 25, D. C.

SYNOPSIS: Unidentified aircraft object observed in sky over Hickam AFB, T.H. at 2232 hours on 24 December 1950.

DETAILS: At 2230 hours 24 December 1950, a bright object, traveling at a high rate of speed from the direction of Lanikul, Oahu, disappeared in low altitude mountains. The former section of this object appeared to be of the white bright-elongated light in shape, and was traveling at a height of approximately 6000 feet and at a very high rate of speed. Observers described that the object had an orange flame-type tail, about as long as the object itself, tapering to a point. An overall description gives was "like that of a guided missile through water".
