5D 24-21-29 9 December 1953
SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT REPORTEDLY SIGHTED NEAR FOOD OUTLET AIRPORT, OHIO
SPECIAL INQUIRY
TO: Commander
Air Technical Intelligence Center
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
ATTN: ATIAA-2C
1. The following is forwarded for your information:
On 20 November 1953, Mr. (redacted), employed at [Cutler State Engineering Company], Marietta, Ohio, Telephone: (redacted), advised that on 20 November 1953, at approximately 0330 hours, he and six other persons, Mr. (redacted), Parkersburg, West Virginia, Telephone: (redacted), Mr. (redacted), Marietta, Ohio, Telephone: (redacted), and three unknown persons, were driving west on US Route 20, near Marietta, Ohio, when they sighted an unidentified object in the sky. The object was described as circular in shape, approximately 40 to 50 feet in diameter, metallic in color, and emitting a reddish-orange glow. The object was traveling at an estimated speed of 1,000 miles per hour and was at an estimated altitude of 1,500 feet. The sighting lasted approximately a third of a minute. All were of the opinion that it was not a conventional aircraft.
(Redacted) stated he had reported the incident to a Mr. [Redacted], a Federal Bureau of Investigation assistant agent at Marietta, Ohio, who advised that the United States Air Force was collecting information on "flying saucers" and recommended that he contact the Office of Special Investigations at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base.
