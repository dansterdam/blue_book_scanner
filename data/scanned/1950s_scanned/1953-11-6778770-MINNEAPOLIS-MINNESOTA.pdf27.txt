FLT. 2-A, 4921 AISS
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
BASE - SIGHTING OF UNUSUAL AERIAL OBJECTS BY CREW MEMBERS OF "C-ETHER" AIRPLANE: On 28 October 1953 at approximately 1615 Hours, crew members of C-ether airplane noticed 5 or 6 unusual airborne objects while flying at an altitude of approximately 6,000 feet. The objects appeared to be round, luminous, and to have a mirror-like finish. They were observed for a period of approximately 15 minutes. The objects were at an estimated altitude of 10,000 to 15,000 feet, and were traveling at a speed of approximately 1,000 to 1,200 m.p.h. The objects climbed and descended rapidly, and executed sharp turns. The objects were also observed by ground observers. Photographs were taken, but they are not available at this base. The sighting was reported to the 38th Air Division (Defense) ICD and the proper reporting channels.
