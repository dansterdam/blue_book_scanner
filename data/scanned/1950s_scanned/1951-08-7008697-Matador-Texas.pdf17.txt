INVESTIGATION INDICATED THAT VERBAL REQUEST FROM AIR PROVOST MARSHAL, GREENLING AIR FORCE BASE, TEXAS, 17 SEPTEMBER 1951, BASED UPON RECEIPT OF CLASSIFIED P-2 INFORMATION CONCERNING GENERAL ACTIVITY IN SOUTHWESTERN U.S. TEXAS, REQUESTING THE TECHNICAL DEVELOPMENT GROUP, WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE, TO INVESTIGATE AN UNIDENTIFIED OBJECT SIGHTED OVER LUBBOCK, TEXAS.
2. UNDER THE CARE AND SUPERVISION OF COLONEL C.H. HARDIN, COMMANDING OFFICER, AIR TECHNICAL INTELLIGENCE CENTER, WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE, AN INVESTIGATION WAS INSTITUTED TO DETERMINE THE NATURE OF THE UNIDENTIFIED OBJECTS AS DESCRIBED IN AIR REGULATION 200-15, PERTAINING TO THE REPORTING AND INVESTIGATION OF UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL PHENOMENA.
AT HONDO AND TEXAS
3. ON 17 SEPTEMBER 1951, THE WRITER CONFERRED WITH TAYLOR, WEST WITH THE EDITOR OF THE LUBBOCK, TEXAS, NEWSPAPER OF LUBBOCK, TEXAS. MR. CHARLES GUY, WHO WITHOUT ANY QUESTIONS, ASSISTED IN OBTAINING AVAILABLE COPIES OF THE NEWSPAPER INFORMATION AS REQUESTED TO THOROUGHLY DISCUSS ARTICLE WITH THE SCOPE OF THE REQUIRED INVESTIGATION. THESE COPIES OF THE NEWSPAPER WERE PHOTOGRAPHED IN PRIVATE.
AT HONDO, TEXAS
4. DUE TO THE CLASSIFICATION OF SECRET IMPOSED BY THE COMMANDING GENERAL, AIR TECHNICAL INTELLIGENCE CENTER, AT THE TIME OF THE CONCLUSION OF THE PHYSICAL INVESTIGATION, COPIES OF THE PHOTOGRAPHS WERE NOT ALLOWED TO BE DEVELOPED OR PRINTED IN THEIR ENTIRETY FOR FURTHER REPRODUCTION. THESE PHOTOGRAPHS, HOWEVER, HAVE BEEN MADE A PART OF THIS REPORT IN DATED ORDER OF PUBLISHING, AS PRESCRIBED AS PART OF THIS REPORT.
AT MATADOR, TEXAS
5. THE SIGHTING OF AN UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECT OVER U.S. HIGHWAY 83 NORTH OF MATADOR, TEXAS WAS REPORTED TO THE LUBBOCK, TEXAS LEADER-MATADOR NEWSPAPER ON 31 AUGUST 1951.
