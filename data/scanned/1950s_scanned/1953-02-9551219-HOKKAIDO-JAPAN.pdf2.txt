SUBJECT: REPORT OF OBSERVATION OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS MADE ON 7 FEB 53 NEAR HAKODATE, HOKKAIDO, JAPAN

On 7 February 1953 at approximately 1900 hours, three unidentified flying objects were observed by approximately 15 persons, including Japanese police, at Hakodate, Hokkaido, Japan. The objects were observed to be round, fluorescent white in color, and about 20-30 feet in diameter. They were observed to fly in a circular pattern at an estimated altitude of 2000-3000 feet for about 15 minutes. The objects emitted no sound and showed no means of propulsion. Their movement was described as being erratic, hovering, and at times abrupt changes of direction. After about 15 minutes the objects flew out of sight in a northerly direction at a high rate of speed.
