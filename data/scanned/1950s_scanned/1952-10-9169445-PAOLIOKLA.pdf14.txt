Terrin Air Force Base, Tex.
(1) The one object sighted was round, bright-bluish in color; it was traveling from the southwest to the northeast at an estimated speed of 3,000 to 3,500 George V. Wafts, to be 600 miles per hour; it passed by one of the sources, George V. Wafts, to the east. There was no distinguishable exhaust but a bright glow. The object burst into fragments and simultaneously disappeared.
(2) Object was sighted at approximately 0035 hours, 29 October 1952. Length of time observed was about ten seconds.
(3) Observation was made visually from a C-47 type aircraft, altitude 5,500 feet.
(4) Location of observers was 35° 37'N, 97° 19'W, over the Canadian River, 5 1/2 miles east of the "Drumum Cave" airfield. The object was at 9,000 feet altitude and traveling east to west, straight line along, one of the crew members of the C-47 aircraft.
(5) All these observers have not previously experienced the sighting of unidentified flying objects. The two officers have responsible positions in a flying training group, Perrin Air Force Base.
(6) Weather at the time and place of sighting was fair. Visibility was good without any wind from the northeast.
(7) There was no known condition, meteorological or otherwise, which could have accounted for the sighting.
(8) There is no known existence of evidence such as fragments, photographs, etc., of the sighting.
(9) This case was reported to Oklahoma City radio station KOMA of the incident and also reported it to Range Control Oklahoma City. This base then notified Carswell Air Force Base, Texas, which did not recognize the C-47 aircraft.
(10) No air traffic was observed in the area at the time of the sighting.
