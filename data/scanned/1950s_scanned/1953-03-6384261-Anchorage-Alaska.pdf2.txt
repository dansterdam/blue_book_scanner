ATIC DOCUMENT 1207414 (A), ANCHORAGE, ALASKA
AS REPORTED BY SPECIAL INVESTIGATOR 07302 ON 30 MARCH 1953 AND AGAIN AT 07302 5 APRIL 1953, A CIVILIAN OBSERVED A BRIGHT LIGHT IN THE NORTHWEST. THE OBJECT WAS DESCRIBED AS BEING OF APPROXIMATELY 25 MILES LONG AND WAS DESCRIBED AS ABOUT THE SAME SIZE AS A FOOTBALL AND APPROXIMATELY 35 FEET ABOVE THE HORIZON. IT WAS DESCRIBED TO BE 70 MILES AWAY. ANOTHER OBJECT WAS SIGHTED ON 20 MAY 1953 OF 20 MILES VISIBILITY. ON 1 APR THERE WERE SCATTERED CLOUDS AT 8000 FEET. VISIBILITY ON BOTH OCCASIONS WAS REGARDED BY TWO OTHER PERSONS.

ATIC COMMENT: THE PREPARING OFFICER STATES THE FOLLOWING: INVESTIGATION OF DATA HAS NOT DEFINITELY ESTABLISHED THE CAUSE OR SOURCE OF THE LIGHT. POSSIBILITY EXISTS THAT THE SOURCE OBSERVED PLANET JUPITER WHICH APPEARS VERY BRIGHT IN THE APPROXIMATE POSITION OF OBSERVED LIGHT.
