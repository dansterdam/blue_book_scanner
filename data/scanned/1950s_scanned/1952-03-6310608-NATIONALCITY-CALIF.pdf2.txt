SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Findings of Unconventional Airborne Objects, San Diego, California, during March 1952.

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. REPORT: Source of information, an experienced air observer, reports that during March 1952 in the San Diego, California, area, several unidentified aerial objects were sighted. Other members of a private club in San Diego have also reported seeing similar objects on various occasions. The objects are described as flying at extremely high speeds and altitudes, and performing maneuvers not characteristic of conventional aircraft. The objects are reported to be metallic in appearance, varying in size from approximately 30 to 50 feet in length. There is no discernible method of propulsion visible, and the objects leave no vapor trail. It is reported that the objects were observed by several reliable and competent observers over a period of several days.

2. DATA: Mr. _____ a civilian, residing at 2005 E. 16th Street, National City, California, advises that he is a member of a private aircraft owners' club in San Diego, California, and he has observed the objects described above on a number of occasions. The members of this club live in various points in and around San Diego, and they have reported to Mr. _____ that they have also observed similar objects on other occasions. Mr. _____ has submitted to this office reports along with various photographs, clippings, and other related data concerning his observations.

Included with the above are reports submitted by two other members of the same club, Messrs. _____ and _____, also residents of the National City, California, area, who corroborate the sightings made by Mr. _____ and other members of the club.
