Case No: 10,320 Date: 9 May 52
Subject: Unidentified Flying Object 
1. This report is being submitted at the request of the undersigned who personally investigated the case on the night of 9 May 1952.
2. At approximately 1950 hours on 9 May 1952, a round, luminous object was observed in the sky over Greenville, S. C. by several persons, most of whom were at the time stationed at a local military installation.
3. The object was observed to be about 40' in diameter, moving at a very rapid rate of speed from South to North, and at an altitude estimated to be between 5,000 and 10,000 feet. The object appeared to be self-luminous, emitting a bright, white light, and did not leave any trail or exhaust. The object was observed for approximately 30 seconds before disappearing from view in a Northerly direction.
4. Extensive interviews were conducted with all available witnesses. The accounts of the various observers were in substantial agreement as to the nature, speed, altitude and general direction of movement of the object.
5. Based upon the information gathered, it is considered that the object observed was an Unidentified Flying Object. No further investigation is contemplated unless additional pertinent data is received.
