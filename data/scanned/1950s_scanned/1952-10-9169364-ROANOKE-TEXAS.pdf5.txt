HEADQUARTERS
THE ARTILLERY CENTER
FORT SILL, OKLAHOMA
Office of the AC of S, G-2

APO 361
3 November 1952

Reported Sighting of Unidentified Aerial Object

TO: Commanding General
Fourth Army
Fort Sam Houston, Texas
ATTN: AC of S, G-2

1. Reference letter your Headquarters, AG350.09 AKADL-I, dated 2 February 1952, Subject: Intelligence Activities.

2. In compliance with par 6a(2), above referenced letter, the following report concerning sighting of unidentified aerial object is forwarded for your information:

a. Date and Time Observed: 1618 hours, 26 October 1952.
b. Location: 2 miles south of Roanoke, Texas on Highway 77.
c. Observers: Driver's wife pointed out object and asked what it was.
d. Description: When first observed, object appeared cigar shaped and about 2/3 the size of an airliner. A brilliant blue reflection was initially sighted about 300° above horizon. Object was observed 90° from north, object appeared to be 5 minutes from observer. Object travelled straight up then went southerly losing some altitude than moving south at a much slower rate. Object appeared to cover a 60° angle in the South East.

3. Action taken: Telephone check was made with Post Field Address, and records were checked from Carswell Flight Service, Fort Worth, Texas with negative results.

H. F. CLARKE JR.
Lt Col
AC of S, G-2
