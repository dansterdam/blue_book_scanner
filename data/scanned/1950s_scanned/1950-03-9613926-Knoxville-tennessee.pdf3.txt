UNCLASSIFIED

HEADQUARTERS, UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
OFFICE OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
WASHINGTON 25, D.C.

6 March 1950

MEMORANDUM

SUBJECT: Incident in Knoxville, Tennessee, Indicative of Unidentified Objects in VICINITY of KNOXVILLE. 

The following information was received from Lt. Garland G. Thomas, Fort McPherson, Georgia: On 23 February 1950, an unidentified object was observed by a number of persons in the vicinity of Knoxville, Tennessee. The object was described as circular in shape, with a bright, metallic-like surface, and approximately 50-60 feet in diameter. It was reported to have maneuvered at a high rate of speed, making a number of abrupt turns and changes in direction, and at one time hovered motionless for a period of approximately 20 minutes. No sound was reported. This object was observed by several persons, including police officials and others, over a period of approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes, after which it disappeared from view. The object was not determined to be a conventional aircraft or any other known or identified object.

Air Defense Division Chief of Air STAFF, Knoxville, Tennessee, advised this information and stated that no aircraft or weather balloons were in the vicinity at the time; and further indicated the object's actions were not consistent with any known or identified object. No further information was furnished.
