Headquarters USAF
Washington 25, D. C. 

Wing Intel: SYNOPSIS: 1st Lt ARTHUR S. PALMER, 375th Troop Carrier Squadron APS, received information from the Officer of the Day, 13 May 1952, that unidentified objects had been sighted by members of the Greenville, South Carolina, police force. There were approximately 2235 hours, 13 May 1952.

DETAILS: (1) Mr. JOHN MARTIN, Greenville, South Carolina, was interviewed by Lt. PALMER and made the following statement: "On the night of May 13, 1952, at approximately 2235 EST, four (4) objects, described as modified cylinder or obloid in shape, flying at an unknown altitude on a heading of about 305 degrees, were brownish orange in color and rather dull glowing in appearance." 

"These objects were in a diamond formation, almost directly overhead, going on a straight course, oscillating slightly, first one side would slip up then the other. They were in sight for about five (5) seconds."
