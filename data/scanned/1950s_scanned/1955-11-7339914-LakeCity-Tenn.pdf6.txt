SUPPLEMENTARY INVESTIGATIVE EFFORTS:

1. A check with the McGhee-Tyson Weather Station was made and the following information was received: At the time of the sighting, sky was clear with no clouds, winds were surface SW 6, 3000 ft 330 der 35 kt, 6000 ft 320 deg 45 kt, 12,000 ft 310 der 50 kt, 20,000 ft 320 deg 50 kt.
2. A check with the McGhee-Tyson flight service revealed negative observations by commercial airlines.
3. The Air Weather Service has no regular reporting channels for information and therefore had no information.
4. Knoxville Filter Center reported negative reports from observers.
5. The 663rd AC&W sq did not track objects on radar.
6. After visual observation 663rd AC&W sq direction center scrambled two (2) F-86H aircraft after the objects. No completed intercept accomplished.
7. Base intelligence officer reported negative.
