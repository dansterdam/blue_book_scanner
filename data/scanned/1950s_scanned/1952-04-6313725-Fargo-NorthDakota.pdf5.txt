APPENDIX I

Fargo, North Dakota - 25 April 1952

I. DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT
Between 2100 and 2145 hours on 25 April 1952, eight observers reported sighting unidentified flying discs which appeared in a wide sky formation. There were five discs per flight and a total of five flights at eight minute intervals. The direction of flight was constantly south to north.
The formation was described as a wide "V", irregular or more specifically, sloping. All eight discs piloted, if such is the case, appeared to have trouble maintaining constant speed and altitude.

II. STATUS OF THE INVESTIGATION
The Commanding Officer, District #20, 462nd Ground Observer Squad, Fargo Filter Center, Fargo, North Dakota, followed up the report personally. He proceeded to the top (66th floor) of the Black Building in Fargo, North Dakota, at about 2030 hours on 28 April 1952 equipped with binoculars, highspeed cameras, telescopes, etc., and noted the mysterious flights. A number of local citizens shared his vigil with him.

III. CONCLUSIONS
The unidentified discs seen traveling very high and fast, and of bright red and orange color were identified by the above observers (II) as being migrating mallard and teal ducks.
