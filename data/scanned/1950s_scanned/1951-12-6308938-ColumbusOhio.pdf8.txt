166TH FIGHTER-INTERCEPTOR SQUADRON
144D FIGHTER-INTERCEPTOR GROUP
LOCKBOURNE AIR FORCE BASE
COLUMBUS, OHIO
27 December 1951

SUBJECT: Information on Unconventional Aircraft  

TO: Commanding General
Air Material Command
ATTN: MGIS
Wright-Patterson AF Base
Dayton, Ohio

1. This compliance with Air Defense Command Letter 200-1 dated 11 April 1951 and in lieu of AF Form 112 the following information is submitted:

a. The object appeared to be an airplane of unknown size without a tail section and was silver in color. No trail or exhaust was seen and the propulsion system was unknown. The observer was flying in the opposite direction and the object appeared to be traveling at a greater speed than the observer's 200 miles per hour. No sound was heard and the object continued to tumble or roll until it was lost in the sun.

b. Object was sighted at approximately 13:30ZS on 22 December 1951 and was observed for approximately one (1) minute.

c. The object was observed visually from the air. The observer was flying an F-84D type aircraft at approximately 15,000 feet with an indicated air speed of 225.

d. The object was approximately 17+ (5) miles east of Columbus, Ohio heading west at an altitude of 15,000 feet when the object was first sighted. The object was at 200 objects high when the observer, heading in an easterly direction at an altitude of at least 20,000 feet.

The high degree of reliability of the observer is shown in the fact that he is a jet fighter pilot with approximately 1700 flying hours, having had combat experience as a fighter pilot during World War II.

f. The sky was clear at time of sighting.
g. None.
h. None.

i. Observer started to turn to give chase but object was lost in the sun.
