UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
(AIR INTELLIGENCE DIVISION)
OFFICE OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
REPORT OF INVESTIGATION

TITLE: UNKNOWN SUBJECT; Unidentified Objects 
in Flight Over Lubbock, Texas

CHARACTER: SPECIAL INQUIRY

REFERENCE: Spot Intelligence Report, DO 923, File 29-0, dated 7 September 1951. 

Investigation conducted upon verbal request of Air Provost Marshal and 2nd Air Force on 18, 20, 21 September 1951. Based upon classified information in Spot Intelligence Report, DO 923, File 29-0, dated 7 September 1951, copies of which were made available by 2nd Air Force. This report concerns reports of unidentified objects sighted over Lubbock, Texas, during August and September 1951. The investigation was conducted by 2nd OSI District, Investigator.

On 16 August 1951, several citizens in Lubbock, Texas, reported seeing a formation of 40 to 50 unidentified objects flying at an estimated speed of 1,000 miles per hour at an altitude of about 500 feet. The unidentified objects were reported again on 31 August 1951. Mr. Carl Hart, Jr., a former B-29 gunner, reported seeing about 30 of the objects and estimated their size as approximately 1 1/2 times the size of a B-29 bomber. The objects were reported to be luminous, and to travel in a diagonal or triangular formation. They were reported to have been seen on several occasions. Mr. W. I. Investor, a former Air Force pilot, also reported seeing the objects on 31 August 1951. He stated that the objects appeared to be about 30 feet in diameter and to have a metallic surface. They were reported to travel in a diagonal formation and then to scatter, each object traveling at a high rate of speed. 

Unauthorized disclosure of information is prohibited and will be considered a violation of AFR 205-1.

APPROVED

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS; 
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
DOD DIE 5200.10
