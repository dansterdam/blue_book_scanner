DETAILS:

1. This investigation opened at the direction of the District Commander, Air Defense Division, Los Angeles Field Office, Atomic Energy Commission, Los Alamos, New Mexico. Concerning a light phenomenon observed near Los Alamos, New Mexico.

2. On 3 September 1952, Mr. and Mrs. W. were in the Atomic Energy Protective Force, Los Alamos, New Mexico. While driving west on State Highway 4, in the vicinity of Los Alamos, they sighted a light traveling in a near Southward direction at an estimated altitude of about two hundred (200) to three hundred (300) miles per hour. The light was described as being round in shape, or odor given off by light not known. No exhaust trail was noted. The light disappeared to the South, reappearing at intervals of approximately 15 minutes for a period of about two hours.

September 1952 - During the period 215 and 225 hours, two different lights, one bluish-white in color, the other amber in color, were observed by the Atomic Energy Protective Force, Los Alamos, New Mexico. These lights disappeared toward the South, the bluish-white light after about three (3) minutes, the amber light after about two (2) minutes, then proceeding Southward by a similar light occurred.
