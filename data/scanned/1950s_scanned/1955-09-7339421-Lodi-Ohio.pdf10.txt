SUPPLEMENTARY INVESTIGATIVE EFFORTS:

1. A check with the Akron Municipal Airport at Akron, Ohio revealed the following information:

   a. That revolving beacon was operating between the hours of dusk and dawn on 22-23 Sept 1955. This beacon operates during these hours throughout the year. Revolutions per minute of the beacon is six (6).

   b. Two (2) searchlights, sixty (60) inch, arc, were operating during the evening of 22 Sept 1955. One, located approximately eight hundred (800) feet from the boundary of the airport to the west (see Incl. # 1). These were being used for advertising purposes of a Gas Filling Station and a County Fair respectively. Revolutions of these searchlights very slow.

   c. It was also learned that the Columbia Chemical Works in Barberton, Ohio (see Incl. # 1)?, spread Lodi and Akron clouds chemicals into "settling beds" which look like the Barberton area. The source of out of these includes known locally as "fog", to an altitude of 300,000 feet. It definitely influences the visibility of the Akron Airport at times. These cloud formations have been responsible for many air night accidents in the past; causing reflections on the clouds from the beds and other light sources.
