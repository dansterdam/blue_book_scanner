In accordance with APL 200-2, dated 29 April 1952, Subject: "Unidentified Flying Objects Reporting (Short Title: UFO)", the following report of an unidentified flying object is submitted.

On 13 February 1953, between 1728 and 1820, PST four observers sighting a GQG POT-1 object in San Francisco Oakland, witnessed an unusual object described as round with what appeared to be a small dome on top. The speed of this object was not estimated but it appeared considerably slower than that of a jet aircraft. The object was observed from the ground and was seen to be traveling at an altitude estimated at 10,000 feet. The object was described as round with what appeared to be a small dome on top. The speed of this object was not estimated but it appeared considerably slower than that of a jet aircraft. The object disappeared over the city of San Francisco.

Enclosed herewith is a copy of the story pertaining to these objects as printed in the San Francisco Examiner edition dated 14 February 1953, according to the complainant. This is the only report on similar objects to the one described in this report during the early morning hours and early evening of 13 February 1953.
