1. SUBJECT: Reference is made to AAF 12 T 2 137, dated 21 January 1951, concerning an unidentified aerial object sighted over Sioux City, Iowa, on 22 January 1951, by an assistant controller and a controller at the Ground Control Approach (GCA) station of the Sioux City Municipal Airport.

The report states that the object appeared to be a white light, approximately 30 feet in diameter, moving in an erratic course at a high rate of speed. It was observed for about 30 seconds and then disappeared. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of 5,000 to 7,000 feet. No sound was heard.

2. DETAILS:
This inquiry was made upon instructions received from Colonel WILLIAM F. LITTLE, Chief, Air Technical Intelligence Division, A-2, Headquarters, United States Air Force.

The report states that on 22 January 1951, at approximately 1910 hours, an unidentified aerial object was observed over Sioux City, Iowa, by the assistant controller and the controller at the GCA station of the Sioux City Municipal Airport. According to the report, the object appeared to be a white light, approximately 30 feet in diameter, moving in an erratic course at a high rate of speed. It was observed for about 30 seconds and then disappeared. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of 5,000 to 7,000 feet. No sound was heard.

The report further states that the object was under surveillance for the entire period of observation and that there was no malfunction of the GCA equipment. The report concludes that "the object is believed to have been a large meteor or bolide."
