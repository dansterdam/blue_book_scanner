UNCLASSIFIED
FEB 1 1953 

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object, Honshu, Japan.

DATE AND TIME: 22 January 1953

LOCATION: Honshu, Japan. 

SOURCE: Major Paul C. Taylor, Captain, USAF, Personnel

The following is a report of an unidentified flying object observed from Honshu, Japan:

On 22 January 1953, at approximately 0600 hours, a silver, circular object was observed in the sky over Honshu, Japan. The object was estimated to be about 30 feet in diameter and was traveling at a high rate of speed, estimated at over 1,000 miles per hour. The object was observed for approximately 15 seconds before disappearing from sight. No vapor trail or exhaust was visible. Although the object was very bright, no sound was heard by the observer.

The observer, Major Paul C. Taylor, USAF, stated that he was unable to identify the object, and that it did not resemble any known aircraft. He further stated that the object moved with a smooth, gliding motion and followed no discernible pattern or course.

THOMAS W. FINN
Colonel, USAF
Director of Intelligence
