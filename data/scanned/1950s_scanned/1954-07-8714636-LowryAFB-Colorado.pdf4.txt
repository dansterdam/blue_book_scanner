14TH DISTRICT OFFICE SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
LOWRY AIR FORCE BASE, COLORADO

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects

TO: Director, Special Investigations
Headquarters, United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. At 1230 hours on 9 July 1954 this office received a telephonic communication from Captain Lowry Air Force Base, Colorado. Operation- Officer, who stated that at approximately 1200 hours on that date his office had received a call from a civilian who reported sighting 6 or 7 objects at a very high altitude and a very fast rate of speed and that he did not believe the objects were conventional aircraft.

2. Special Agents EDWARD L. RUSH and M. E. ECKER conducted interviews with the civilian witness (description 3) who stated that the objects were flying at an altitude of approximately 20,000 feet and that they were traveling at a speed of approximately 1,000 miles per hour and were maneuvering erratically. The witness also stated that the objects appeared to be metallic in color and were round or saucer-shaped.

3. Special Agents KEANE and RUSH interviewed Captain _________ and determined the following information: At approximately 1300 hours on 9 July 1954 Captain ________ dispatched a T-33 aircraft to investigate the sighting. The pilot of the T-33 aircraft reported that he had been unable to locate the objects. Captain _________ also stated that he had received several calls from civilians reporting the sighting of similar objects in the vicinity of Lowry Air Force Base. Captain __________ further stated that the objects were seen to be moving in a northeasterly direction above a cirrus haze.
