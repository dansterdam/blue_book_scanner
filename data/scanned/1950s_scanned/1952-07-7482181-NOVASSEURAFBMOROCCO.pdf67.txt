SUBJECT REPORT OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT
NOVASSEUR AFB MOROCCO 
7 JULY 1952
1. THIS REPORT IS SUBMITTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH AF REGULATION 200-2, DATED 14 SEPTEMBER 1950.
2. AT APPROXIMATELY 2200 HOURS LOCAL TIME ON 7 JULY 1952, A MASTER SERGEANT AND A CAPTAIN OBSERVED AN UNIDENTIFIED OBJECT IN THE SKY OVER NOVASSEUR AIR BASE, MOROCCO. THE OBJECT WAS FIRST OBSERVED IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT OF THE SKY, MOVING IN A SOUTHEASTERLY DIRECTION AT AN ESTIMATED ALTITUDE OF 30,000 TO 40,000 FEET. THE OBJECT WAS TRAVELLING AT AN ESTIMATED SPEED OF 600 TO 700 MILES PER HOUR.
3. THE OBJECT WAS OBSERVED TO MAKE SEVERAL ABRUPT CHANGES IN DIRECTION, BOTH HORIZONTALLY AND VERTICALLY, AND AT ONE POINT HOVERED MOTIONLESS FOR APPROXIMATELY 30 SECONDS. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE DISC-SHAPED, APPROXIMATELY 30 TO 40 FEET IN DIAMETER, AND EMITTED A BRIGHT, STEADY LIGHT. 
4. AFTER BEING OBSERVED FOR APPROXIMATELY 2 MINUTES, THE OBJECT INCREASED ITS SPEED AND DISAPPEARED OVER THE HORIZON IN A SOUTHEASTERLY DIRECTION.
5. OFFICIAL WEATHER REPORTS FOR THE AREA INDICATE CLEAR SKIES AND UNLIMITED VISIBILITY AT THE TIME OF THE SIGHTING. THERE WERE NO KNOWN AIR OPERATIONS IN THE AREA WHICH COULD ACCOUNT FOR THE OBJECT. 
6. IT IS THE CONSIDERED OPINION OF THE WITNESSES THAT THE OBJECT OBSERVED WAS NOT OF CONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT ORIGIN.
7. PLEASE ADVISE OF ANY ADDITIONAL INFORMATION OR INSTRUCTIONS.
