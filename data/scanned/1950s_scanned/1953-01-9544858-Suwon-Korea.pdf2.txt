UNCLASSIFIED

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object, Suwon, Korea, 9 January 1953.

1. On 9 January 1953, at approximately 2215 hours local time, an unidentified flying object was observed by four military personnel stationed at Suwon Air Base, Korea.

2. The object was observed for approximately 10 minutes. It was described as round, silver in color, and approximately 30-40 feet in diameter. The object appeared to be at an altitude of approximately 1,500 to 2,000 feet and was traveling in a northerly direction at an estimated speed of 400-500 knots.

3. No sound was heard during the sighting. The object exhibited a steady glow and maintained a constant altitude and speed throughout the observation period.

4. Attempts were made to track the object with searchlights and to establish radio contact, but all efforts were unsuccessful. No other aircraft were observed in the vicinity at the time.

5. The observers were Warrant Officer John E. DOE, Sgt. Richard T. ROE, Airman 1st Class James P. SMITH, and Airman 2nd Class William H. JONES, all assigned to the 35th Fighter-Interceptor Squadron, 5th Air Force, United States Air Force.
