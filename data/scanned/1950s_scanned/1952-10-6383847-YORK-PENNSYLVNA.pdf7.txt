REPORT INTERPSTING ICE REPORT

SUBJECT: (Unclassified) UNIDENTIFIED SUBJECT(S) APPEARING OF UNCON-VENTIONAL AIRCRAFT (UFOB) REPORTED BY U.S. COAST GUARD STITION AT GLOUCESTER, MASS., AT 2030 TO 2130 HOURS, 14 OCTOBER 1952.

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, USAF
Washington 25, D. C.

SYNOPSIS:

On 14 October 1952, Captain ROBERT E. METCALF, U. S. Coast Guard Station in Boston, Massachusetts, reported a sighting of one (1) unidentified airborne object, and that Gloucester Life Boat Station in Gloucester, Massachusetts, at approximately 2013 hours, 14 October 1952, reported a sighting of one (1) unidentified airborne object, and that the Coast Guard Cutter "Yankton," from a fishing boat off Gloucester, and from a yacht off Marblehead, also reported phenomena were interviewed. No activity or condition developed that encounters for sighting.

DETAILS:

1. At 2130 hours, 14 October 1952, Captain ROBERT E. METCALF, U. S. Coast Guard Station in Boston, Massachusetts, had received reports of the sighting of unidentified airborne objects from the Gloucester Life Boat Station in Gloucester, Massachusetts. The report stated that the unidentified object was about the size of a fishing boat off Gloucester, and which was en route from Portland, Maine, to Provincetown, Massachusetts. According to METCALF, the first report was received at 2013 hours and the additional reports were received by the Coast Guard within the hour. On 15 October 1952, Mr. R. C. SEAMANS, Coast Guard, was interviewed.
