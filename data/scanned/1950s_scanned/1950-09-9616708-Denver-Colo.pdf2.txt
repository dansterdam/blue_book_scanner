AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
Sighting of Unidentified Flying Objects, Colorado, USA
Denver, Colorado, USA
29 September 1950
From: M. R. Britton, Captain, USAF
To: Commanding Officer, Lowry Flight Service Center, Lowry AF Base, Denver, Colorado

The Commanding Officer, Lowry Flight Service Center, Lowry AF Base, Denver, Colorado, reports the sighting of unidentified flying objects in the vicinity of Arapaho and Jefferson, Colorado, on the night of 28 September 1950 between 1900 and 2000 hours. The objects were described as being circular in shape and approximately 45-50 feet in diameter. The objects maneuvered at high speed and made turns at a sharp angle. The objects were observed for approximately 30 minutes, during which time they remained stationary at times.

Enclosed is a signed statement concerning the sighting, made by USAF officers at the time of the observation. There is additional information in relation to the unidentified flying object sighting. Apparent confirmation of the sighting is furnished by the included reports of fourteen persons also having observed flying objects.

APPROVED:
ROBERT M. SUTTON
Captain, USAF

WILLIAM L. TRAVIS
Lt. Colonel, USAF
Chief, Intelligence Division
