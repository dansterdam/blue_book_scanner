SUBJECT: Air Intelligence Report on Multiple Unidentified Flying Objects observed in the sky near Hamilton APB, Calif., on March 15, 1952.

The following is a report on unidentified objects that were observed in the sky in the vicinity of Hamilton APB, Calif., on March 15, 1952:

At 1219 hours: A pilot of a P-51 aircraft on a routine training mission reported visually sighting 4 objects at an estimated altitude of 25,000 feet. The objects were traveling in a straight line at an estimated speed of 500 mph and disappeared after approximately 10 seconds. The objects appeared to be round or oval in shape and were a silver or aluminum color. No exhaust or vapor trails were observed.

At 1225 hours: A second pilot of a P-51 aircraft reported visually sighting 10 objects at an estimated altitude of 25,000 feet. The objects were traveling in a straight line at an estimated speed of 600 mph and disappeared after approximately 15 seconds. The objects appeared to be round or oval in shape and were a silver or aluminum color. No exhaust or vapor trails were observed.

The observers were located at their homes on Hamilton APB, Calif. They were both experienced pilots stationed at Hamilton APB, Calif. 

The weather officer reports the skies were clear during the time of the sightings. Winds were 30-40 mph at 25,000 feet.

Meteorological activity, physical evidence of the sightings, identification of sighting sources and security classification of the report are discussed as investigative.

1. Concurs with preparing officer: The information available to the preparing officer concerning the unidentified objects sighted was as reported.
