UNCLASSIFIED

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

Place and Date of Intelligence: NOVASSEUR AFB, MOROCCO, 7 JULY 1952

Subject: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT

1. At 1830 hours on 7 July 1952 from Novasseur Air Force Base, Morocco, an object was sighted in the sky to the East by the sparras wing provost marshal. The object was bright and round and appeared to be about 12-15 feet in diameter at a distance of about 50 yards from the observer. The object was at an altitude of about 1,000 feet, and was observed for approximately 2-3 minutes. 

2. The object suddenly checked out and disappeared in the direction of the north. No deviation in course. 

3. Spanish national, USAF ground crew hired employee, sighted the object and stated it was an aircraft of some kind but of a very different and unconventional design, the wings were very short and object moved at a very rapid speed. 

4. The following reports were given to the Preparing Officer at Novasseur Air Force Base:

a. Capt. Ira Fairchild, AO 518332, Purchasing and Contracting Officer, 5th Air Depot Wing, stated that the object resembled a meteor, however, visibility was good - Object resembled a meteor, however, it was hovering and oscillating and then shot away at a very high rate of speed. 

b. Capt. Gerald M. Carleen, AO 519301, Deputy Director of Maintenance, 5th Air Depot Wing, stated that the object resembled a large ball of flame and was hovering and oscillating and then shot away at a very high rate of speed.

UNCLASSIFIED
