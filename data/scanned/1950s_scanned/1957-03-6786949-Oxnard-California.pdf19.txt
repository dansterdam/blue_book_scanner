GALPIRAN, CALIFORNIA. On the evening of April 5, 1957, at approximately 2030 hours, Captain Walter G. Winter and Traffic Officer James R. Wilson of the California Highway Patrol, VENTURA, CALIFORNIA, sighted an unidentified flying object in the Oxnard area. As the officers were patrolling along Highway 101, approximately 15 miles south of Oxnard, they observed a bright light in the sky which appeared to be traveling at a high rate of speed. The officers stopped their vehicle to observe the object more closely. They stated that the object was diamond-shaped, about 30 to 40 feet in diameter, and was surrounded by a bright white light. The object appeared to them to be approximately 500 to 1000 feet in altitude and was traveling in a northeasterly direction. After observing the object for about 5 to 10 minutes, it suddenly increased its speed and disappeared from view.
