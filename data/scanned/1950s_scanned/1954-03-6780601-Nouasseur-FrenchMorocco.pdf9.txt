REPORT OF INVESTIGATION
Unidentified Flying Object, Nouasseur, Morocco, 5 March 1954
SPECIAL INQUIRY
Spot Report, Nouasseur Dt, MAD, 5-3-1, dtd 031254
Investigation initiated by the District Commander, North Africa District. According to THEODORE A. RORST, Maj., 316th Air Division (Defense), USAF, the object was sighted by a crew of three on 5 March 1954 at approximately 2300 hours, local time, while on a routine night flight from Nouasseur Air Base, French Morocco, to Wheelus Air Force Base, Tripoli, Libya. The object was observed at an altitude of approximately 10,000 feet and at a distance of approximately 10 miles ahead of the aircraft. It appeared as a bright, luminous object moving at a high rate of speed, estimated to be in excess of 1,000 mph (ground speed). The object was observed for approximately 60-90 seconds before disappearing from sight. Visual observation was made by all three crew members who stated that the object exhibited neither wing nor tail structure, emitted no trail or exhaust, and made no perceptible noise. According to preliminary estimates, the size of the object was approximately 30-40 feet in diameter. No evasive action was taken by the aircraft, and the object was not tracked on airborne or ground radar. Additional details regarding this incident will be forwarded upon completion of further investigation.
