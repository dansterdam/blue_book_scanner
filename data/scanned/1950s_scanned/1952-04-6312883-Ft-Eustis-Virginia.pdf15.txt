SUBJECT: Unusual Aerial Phenomena, Vicinity of Ft Eustis, Va., 17 April 1952
1. Reference is made to DO 31 INR, CAI 262, dated 15 April 1952.
2. While report pertaining to the observation of an unusual aerial phenomenon by five (5) persons at Fort Eustis, Virginia, at approximately 2135 hours on 17 April 1952.
3. On 19 April 1952 Lt Colonel ******, Fort Eustis, Virginia, reported to this office that at approximately 2135 hours on 17 April 1952, five (5) persons in the area of the Maintenance Shop, Post Engineers, Ft Eustis, observed an unidentified aerial object passing from southeast to northwest in a straight line, and that this object appeared to be under some intelligent control.
a. On 18 April 1952 **/Sgt **** ****, was interviewed and according to the subject, the unidentified object was first observed by Mr. **, a civil service employee at Fort Eustis, and Sgt **** who were working in the Park for approximately two (2) hours and his wife were leaving the hill park, and overtook Sgt **** and his wife. They continued the observation until the object disappeared in a northeastern direction at an altitude of approximately 3000-4000 feet.
