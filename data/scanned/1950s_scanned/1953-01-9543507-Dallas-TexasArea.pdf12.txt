CASE NO. (Record - page 2)
An unidentifiable object was observed in the sky, around 6:30 P.M. on 1 January 1953, in the Dallas, Texas area. The object was first sighted at Carswell Air Force Base, Fort Worth, Texas, and was last seen by observers in Dallas, Texas.

According to the document, a civilian at Carswell Air Force Base reported seeing a mysterious object in the sky over Paris, Texas. He claimed to have observed the object for about 30 minutes. The object was described as being circular in shape and metallic in appearance. A radar unit at Tinker AFB, Oklahoma, also reported tracking a target by radar in the Paris, Texas area. The radar tracked the object from a point 3 miles southwest of Paris to a point 5 miles northeast of Paris, covering a distance of 8 miles in about 15 minutes. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of 600 knots and at an altitude of 7,500 feet. The time of the initial radar pickup was 6:32. Eleven minutes later, at 6:43, the object disappeared. No other details about the object or its possible identification are provided.
