UNCLASSIFIED
SUBJECT AIR INTELLIGENCE REPORT
Major Field
Spokane, Washington
22 August 1951

SUBJECT: Report of Information on Unconventional Aircraft

TO: Commanding General
Air Materiel Command
ATTN: MCINT
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton, Ohio

1. In accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 4 and 5, Air Defense Command Letter 200-1, 11 April 1951, the following information, which was obtained as the result of an observation of an unconventional aircraft in this area, is forwarded for your information.

2. Description: The object was elliptical in shape, estimated to be approximately 30-60 feet in diameter. It appeared to be silver or aluminum in color. It was observed for approximately 2-3 minutes while it was in view. The object did not make any noise, and no trail or exhaust was observed. If the object was not seen initially, it appeared suddenly, and as it passed over the observers, it appeared to be stationary in the air. The ascent and descent of the object was very slow, approximately 1/2 mile per minute. Observers, when the object was sighted at approximately 40 degrees, estimated the speed of the object to be 500-600 miles per hour. The object started a 80-90 degree climb. The movement of the object was very smooth and steady, and it appeared to be under positive control. The object continued in a steady 80-90 degree climb until it disappeared from view.

3. The object sighted approximately 1100, 20 August 1951. Duration of observation: approximately 2-3 minutes.

4. The object was sighted with the naked eye from the ground. Observer's distance from the object was approximately 2-3 miles. Observers stated no aircraft were seen in the vicinity of the object during the observation.

DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
