AF FORM 112—PART II
FLIGHT CLASSIFIED     AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

WAT* 9 JUNE 1952
PLACE: 25 MILES WEST OF ALBUQUERQUE. N.M.

AT 3 POSITION: 23 MILES WEST OF ALBUQUERQUE, N.M. AT 1130
HOURS. 9 JUNE 1952, FLIGHT AND GROUND CREWS OF AN F-86D
AIRCRAFT OBSERVED AN UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT.

AS DESCRIBED BELOW, WAS OBSERVED SIMULTANEOUSLY BY PILOTS OF A FLIGHT OF 2
F-86D AIRCRAFT FROM A POINT AT APPROXIMATELY 3:30 O'CLOCK RELATIVE TO THE AIRCRAFT NOSE.
THE FLIGHT LEADER FIRST OBSERVED THE OBJECT AS IT CLIMBED FROM VISUAL CONTACT.

THE TRAJECTORY OF THE OBJECT WAS CONSIDERED TO BE FLAT OR ONLY SLIGHTLY UP —CLIMBING, AND WHEN OBSERVED APPEARED TO BE NOT MORE THAN 230-300 FT. FROM THE AIRCRAFT. ITS ALTITUDE WAS ESTIMATED AS
11,000 FT.

THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE RECTANGULAR IN SHAPE WITH DIMENSIONS POSSIBLY SLIGHTLY LONGER THAN WIDE, AND WAS WHITISH OR SILVER IN COLOR.

THE ABOVE WAS REPORTED TO KIRTLAND AIRWAYS AT ALBUQUERQUE WHO STATED THAT NEITHER BALLOONS NOR AIRCRAFT (GAC SINCE APPROX. 0800 THAT MORNING) KIRTLAND WERE IN THE AREA AT THAT TIME. A ROUTINE REPORT WOULD BE FORWARDED.

A TRUE EXTRACT:

ROBERT E. KRUSE
1ST LT., USAF
WING INTELLIGENCE OFFICER
