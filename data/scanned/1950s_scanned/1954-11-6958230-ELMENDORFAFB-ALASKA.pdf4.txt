AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

A. SUBJECT OF REPORT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS OR FLYING SAUCERS OBSERVED IN VICINITY OF ELMENDORF AIR FORCE BASE, ALASKA.

B. DATE OBSERVED: 6 NOVEMBER 1954

C. LOCATION: ELMENDORF AIR FORCE BASE, ALASKA

D. DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT:
AT APPROXIMATELY 1730 HOURS, 6 NOVEMBER 1954, RADAR INSTALLATION AT ELMENDORF AIR FORCE BASE, ALASKA, DETECTED FOUR UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS IN THE VICINITY OF THE BASE. THESE OBJECTS WERE TRACKED BY RADAR FOR APPROXIMATELY FIFTEEN MINUTES. DURING THIS TIME, THE OBJECTS PERFORMED MANEUVERS WHICH INCLUDED SUDDEN 90 DEGREE TURNS, ACCELERATIONS FROM A HOVERING POSITION TO AN ESTIMATED SPEED OF 600 MPH, AND ALTITUDINAL CHANGES IN EXCESS OF 20,000 FEET IN 8 TO 10 SECONDS. THE OBJECTS WERE DESCRIBED AS ELL IPTICAL IN SHAPE, FROM 30 TO 40 FEET IN DIAMETER, AND APPEARED METALLIC IN COLOR. GROUND OBSERVATION OF THE OBJECTS WAS HAMPERED BY OVERCAST WEATHER CONDITIONS. NO KNOWN AIRCRAFT WERE IN THE AREA AT THE TIME.

E. ACTION TAKEN: REPORTED TO INTELLIGENCE OFFICER ELMENDORF AIR FORCE BASE.

F. AGENCY AND OBSERVER IDENTIFICATION: AIR FORCE RADAR SITE ELMENDORF AIR FORCE BASE, ALASKA.
