In compliance with AFR 200-2, dated 26 August 1953, the following report is submitted:

(1) (a) Object was round.
(b) Size estimated to be approximately equal to that of an orange held in the hand at arm's length.
(c) Object was orange colored.
(d) One object was observed.
(e) No discernible features or details noted.
(f) No tail, trail, or exhaust noted.
(g) No sound was heard.
(h) Observer did not notice object until it was almost directly overhead and at first thought the object to be a shooting star but immediately noted that it was too low and much too large to be a star. Observer further noted that the object was orange colored instead of white as previously observed shooting stars.

(2) (a) Observer was in the front yard of her residence watering shrubs and flowers by the light of a kerosene lantern. Observer's back was to lantern when she noticed a large flash. Observer recalled that lantern had exploded and had turned to face the lantern when she noticed the light in the sky.
(b) Angle of elevation of the object when first observed was approximately 90 degrees.
(c) Angle of elevation of the object upon disappearance was about 45 degrees.
(d) Object flew a straight and level flight path when observed. First observed almost directly overhead and flew approximately northward until it disappeared.
(e) Object disappeared by passing out of view (Observer's home obstructed line of vision).
(f) Object was observed between two and four seconds.

(3) (a) A visual ground observation was made.
(b) No optical or electronic aids used.
(c) N/A

(4) (a) Object sighted 220500Z July 1954.
(b) Light conditions: Night.

(5) Observation made from observer's residence, 109 East Castle Hills Land, San Antonio, Texas.

Occupation: Landlady. Reliability unknown.
