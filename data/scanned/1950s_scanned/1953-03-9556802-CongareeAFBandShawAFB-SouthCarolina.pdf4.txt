Air Intelligence Information Report

T-2 30 March 1953

PAGE 1 OF 1

DOWNGRADED AFTER 12 YEARS
DECLASSIFIED USAF 280-10

In compliance with paragraph 7a, Air Force Letter 200-5, the following information is submitted:

1. The object was sighted at 1825 hours, 26 March 1953, approximately 30 miles north of Congaree AFB on a heading of 300 degrees.

2. No other aircraft or ground installation was observed in the vicinity at the time.

3. The object was described as a light circular shape with variable color, initially bright white but changing to orange and then red during the observation. The object was estimated to be approximately 50 to 60 feet in diameter. It was observed for a period of approximately 30 seconds.

4. No sound or trail was observed emanating from the object.

5. The sighting was made by two pilots who were on duty. These airmen are rated excellent in regard to reliability, experience, and powers of observation. They were on a routine Operational Readiness flight at the time of the sighting.

6. The weather conditions were as follows:
(1) Charlotte, N.C. - Thin broken clouds at 25,000', 15 K. Vis.
(2) Columbia, S.C. - Thin broken clouds at 18,000', 15 K. Vis.
(3) Augusta, Ga. - Thin broken clouds at 20,000', 15 K. Vis.

The radar operator was scanning the area at the time of the sighting but no target indication was observed.

This report is being submitted to higher headquarters for further analysis and inter-action without concurrence.
