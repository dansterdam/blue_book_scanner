Air Intelligence Information Report
1. DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT: 
On 8 July 1952, at approximately 5:30 P.M. local time, a pilot, en route from Washington, D.C. to Cleveland, Ohio, observed a formation of unidentified objects at an altitude of approximately 15,000 feet. The pilot described the objects as being circular in shape, glowing white in color, and traveling at high speed in an easterly direction. The pilot estimated the speed of the objects to be in excess of 600 knots. After observing the objects for approximately 2-3 minutes, they disappeared from view.

2. RESULTS OF INVESTIGATION:
The sighting was reported to the Air Defense Command and investigated by Headquarters, 20th Air Division. According to the report, the pilot was considered to be a reliable observer. The report further stated that the sighting could not be explained in terms of any known aircraft or natural phenomena. The object observed did not conform to the characteristics of any type of aircraft known to be operating in the area.

3. CONCLUSIONS:
The investigating officer concluded that the objects observed could not be satisfactorily identified as any known type of aircraft or natural phenomenon. The report recommended that the incident be classified as "unidentified."
