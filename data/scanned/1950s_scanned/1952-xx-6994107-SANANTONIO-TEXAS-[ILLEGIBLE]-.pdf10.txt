AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SAAMA

In compliance with AF Letter 205-5, dated 29 April 1952, the following information is submitted:

(1) Object was singular, appeared round, size undetermined, and emitted a white light. Object appeared to be circular with exact shape undetermined in the immediate area of object. Object gave appearance of rapid ascension when approached by C-97.
(2) Time of sighting approximately 0052 hours, C.S.T., 28 July 1952.
(3) A visual observation from a C-97 aircraft was made.
(4) Observer was in C-97 aircraft, flying in the vicinity of San Antonio, Texas, whose altitude and longitude unknown, at time of the sighting.
(5) Object observed by Captain Samuel W. Tucker, Pilot, and Capt Donald E. Anselmo, co-pilot, of C-97 aircraft no 44-80256. Both officers attempted to intercept the unidentified object. However, the C-97 closed from the rear but failed to overtake it. Both are completely reliable.
(6) Weather and wind aloft at the time of the sighting unknown.
(7) Object was identified as the Planet Jupiter.
(8) No physical evidence of the sighting.
(9) C-97 was fully air-to-air, Texas flew interceptive mission unidentified object. 
(10) Local air traffic in the immediate vicinity unknown.
