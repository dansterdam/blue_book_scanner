SUBJECT: Uncorroborated Type of Aircraft Sighted by Individuals at Albuquerque, New Mexico, on 25 August 1951.
TO: Commanding Office, Air Technical Intelligence Center, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Dayton, Ohio. ATTN: Chief, Technical Analysis Division
Reference is made to your letter, dated 30 October 1951, subject as above, file ATIC/ATIAA-2c.
This District was not familiar with the reported sighting over Albuquerque, on 25 August 1951, as indicated in the Report Intelligence Report enclosed with referenced letter.
On 10 November 1951, Mr. and Mrs. were shown the photographs enclosed with referenced letter, and stated the formation of lights appeared similar to the lights they had observed on the trailing edge of the unidentified object they saw at 2155 hours, 25 August 1951.
In addition to the outline sketched on the photograph, Mrs. assisted by her husband, drew a sketch of the object sighted, which is attached as inclosure.
On 13 November 1951, Mr. D. M. BUSSEY, Chief, Aircraft Communications of the Air Route Traffic Control Center, Albuquerque, New Mexico, was contacted in regard to any unusual incidents that may have been reported on the night of 25 August 1951. Mr. BUSSEY revealed that there were no reports of any unusual sightings on 25 August 1951.
