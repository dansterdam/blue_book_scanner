SUBJECT: Green Fire-Balls Reported over Phila.
29 January 1952
SPECIAL INQUIRY

TO: District Commander
5th District OSI (10) USAF
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton, Ohio

1. Reference is made to telephone message received from Major JOHN F. O'CONNELL, your office, at 0930 hours on 29 February 1952, requesting the furnishing of information concerning a report that green fire-balls had been seen flying over Philadelphia on 29 January 1952.

2. In an interview on 27 February 1952, Dr. CHARLES FEDERER OLIVIER, Director, Astronomical Observations, University of Pennsylvania, furnished the following information:

At approximately 1817 hours on 29 January 1952 a FIRE-BALL was observed in the sky over Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. This meteor was blue-green in color with a short trail. He stated that the brilliancy averaged to be that of a quarter-moon. Its visibility lasted approximately 10 to 12 seconds at which time it disappeared, not less than 50 nor more than 100 miles distant from the observer. This general phenomenon was of a powerful nature and the fragments to have fallen as meteorite.

Dr. OLIVIER stated that he had received approximately 150 reports from individuals regarding this incident. These reports have been in the form of phone calls, letters, etc., as a result of which requests have been received for additional information of the phenomenon.

Dr. OLIVIER stated that he did not personally observe this meteor nor was it observed by anyone from the University of Pennsylvania. Dr. OLIVIER stated that there is no doubt that the phenomenon occurred as described.
