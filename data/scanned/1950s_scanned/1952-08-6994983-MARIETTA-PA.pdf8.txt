UNIDENTIFIED STATION
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
UNCLASSIFIED) Unidentified Flying Objects Reporting (Short Title: FLYOBJ)
Middletown Air Material Area
CONTROL NO. 6 AUGUST 1952
NAME OF OBSERVER: John L. Seeley
AIR FORCE LETTER NO. 200-5 - 29 APR 52
CITY AND STATE
Marietta, Pa., reported observing an unidentified flying object on 6 August 1952.
DESCRIPTION OF OBJECT(S): The object was a disc-shaped object, about the size of a small airplane, flying at approximately 1000 feet altitude and at a speed of approximately 400-500 knots, traveling parallel to the ground in a south-easterly direction. The object was described as being silvery-white in color. No smoke, trail or exhaust was observed.
OBSERVATIONAL DATA: The object was physically described as a brilliant white light with a relative size of approximately half that of the moon. It had a grayish glow around the outside that was brighter near the center. The object was described as traveling "very fast" and as being at a "low altitude." Two or three second duration.
MRS. MARIETTA'S OBSERVATION: Mrs Marietta's observation was collaborated by Mr. S. who confirmed observing an object at approximately one mile south-west of Marietta (crossroads - 4000'). The object was stated to be round with a grayish color and was moving at a high rate of speed parallel to the ground in a southeasterly direction. NO PHOTO OF OBJECT
The weather at the time of the observation was clear, with 10-mile visibility. This office can offer no definite explanation for the sighting.
