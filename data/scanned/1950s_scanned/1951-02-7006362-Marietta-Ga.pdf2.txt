SUBJECT: Sighting of Unidentified Flying Object or Light

TO: Commanding General
Fourteenth Air Force
Robins Air Force Base, Georgia

1. The following information is submitted regarding the sighting of an unidentified object or light by a number of persons at Dobbin Air Force Base between 2300 and 2320 hours, 2 February 1951.

a. Number of objects sighted: one.

b. Location, altitude and time of sighting: Observers at Dobbin Air Force Base reported seeing an object hovering at about 300 or 165 degrees altitude position estimated between 1000 and 2000 feet above ground level.

c. Flight path and speed of object: Confirming to witnesses as to the object moving slowly at first and then quickly disappearing at a high rate of speed. To estimate available on apparent speed.

d. Weather at time of sighting: The sighting took place at Dobbin Air Force Base between 2300 and 2320 hours, 2 February 1951 with twelve (12) miles visibility. Some weather reported at 2330, 2 February 1951.

e. Description of object or light as seen by observers: Object loved to the ground and vanished leaving a green, blue and white light. The object or light was first observed at 2320 hours on 2 February 1951 at the Dobbin Air Force Base Operations.
