AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects (report)
DATE OF INFORMATION: 25 July 1952
FROM: HEADQUARTERS, AIR MATERIEL COMMAND, WRIGHT-PATTERSON AIR FORCE BASE, OHIO
TO: DIRECTOR OF INTELLIGENCE, HEADQUARTERS UNITED STATES AIR FORCE, WASHINGTON 25, D.C.
SUMMARY: On the evening of 25 July 1952, several Air Force personnel observed a group of unidentified objects maneuvering in the sky over Presque Isle Air Force Base, Maine. The objects were described as bright, circular, and capable of high-speed, erratic maneuvers. The sighting lasted approximately 40 minutes.
BASE: Presque Isle Air Force Base, Maine
DATE: 25 July 1952
TIME: 2230-2310 hours
OBSERVATION: The unidentified objects were first observed at approximately 2230 hours on 25 July 1952 by Airman 1/c John E. Dzierzanowski, 36th Air Rescue Squadron, Presque Isle Air Force Base, Maine. The objects were observed maneuvering at high speeds, making erratic turns and dives, and occasionally splitting into smaller groups. The objects were described as bright, circular, and capable of maintaining a constant speed, altitude, and direction.
SUMMARY: The sighting lasted approximately 40 minutes, and was observed by several Air Force personnel, including 1/Lt Arthur J. Stahl, 36th Air Rescue Squadron, and Airman 1/c John E. Dzierzanowski, 36th Air Rescue Squadron. The objects were tracked on radar by the tower controller and the GCI radar station. The tower controller reported that the objects moved at speeds in excess of 500 knots and made abrupt course changes.
The objects were tracked on radar for the duration of the sighting. It was estimated that there were from 10 to 15 objects in the formation. When the sighting ended, the objects disappeared from both visual and radar observation. 
There were no aircraft from Presque Isle Air Force Base or any other known source in the area at the time of the sighting. The weather conditions were clear, with good visibility.

1. Narrative statement of 1/Lt Stahl
