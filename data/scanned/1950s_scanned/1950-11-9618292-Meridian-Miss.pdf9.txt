25 November 1950
SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT
SUBJECT: Reports of Unconventional Aircraft
TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D. C.
a. SYNOPSIS: Recent reports of sightings of unconventional
aircraft in the Mississippi, Arkansas area are as follows:
b. Faraging Pilot of the Meridian Star, who is a major in
the Mississippi National Guards, sighted an unconventional aircraft
while flying an F-84J aircraft and flew in pursuit of the object.
c. Three (3) Mississippi National Guards assisted members
of the unconventional aircraft which was forced down from the vicinity
of McComb, Mississippi.
d. Two (2) weather forecasters at Key Field, Mississippi,
gave reported weather conditions for period that unconventional aircraft was sighted.
e. Two (2) Control Tower employees at Key Field, Mississippi,
stated an unidentified aerial object that could have been a conventional aircraft.
f. MCATP Radio reported source of object.
g. Force Radio reported an object.
h. The Clarion Ledger carried an account of folks in Brandon having seeing another "flying saucer" flying across town.
i. The Vicksburg Evening Post carried an account of Major Stephens sighting an unidentified object over Mississippi recording the edge of a bright tilted plate.
j. The Newton Daily News carried an account of a "mysterious
streak of light" in the sky being sighted by local citizens.
