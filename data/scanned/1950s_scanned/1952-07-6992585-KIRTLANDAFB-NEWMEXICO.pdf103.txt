Orlando, Florida                           REPORT NO.             12-22-52                 PAGE 2      OF      2      PAGES

At 0900 hours 30 July 1952 an unidentified object was observed over
Orlando, Florida, by the undersigned at the above address, facing west, at time
of sighting.

The object was egg-shaped, aluminum colored, with no audible sound,
and no vapor trail, exhaust or protuberances visible.

The object came from northwest, flying very high at a high rate of
speed, and made a gentle turn to the right. The object was in sight approximately 10 seconds. About two minutes later observer saw an object flying
from the southeast, at the same altitude and speed as the first object.
The second sighting was approximately five seconds in duration. About ten
minutes later an object was observed on the same course and altitude, again
from the northwest. Observer stated all three sightings could have been
the same object.

Weather at time of sighting was CAVU with scattered cumulus clouds.

No interceptions were made and no photographs were taken.

The observer has had no previous experience in aircraft observation.

Aircraft from this base were flying in the vicinity during period of
observation. No aircraft from MacDill Air Force Base, Patrick Air Force
Base, or Homestead Air Station were reported in this area at time of
observation.
