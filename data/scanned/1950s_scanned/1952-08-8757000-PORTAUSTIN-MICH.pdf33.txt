UNCLASSIFIED
12 AUG 1952

SUBJECT: (Unclassified) UFO REPORT(S)

IMPORTANT: Declassification on Inconvenience subject, 1952 AUGUST 12. RESTRICTED.

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, USAF
Washington 25, D.C.

SUBJECT:

a "Flying saucer" was observed on 6 August 1952, approximately 0935 EDT, at Port Austin, Michigan, lasting 8 seconds. The object was slightly longer than a standard-size cigarette and emitted a yellow-green glow and was at an estimated altitude of 1,000 feet. The size and glow of the object were in marked contrast with the surrounding darkness.
WITNESS: C.J.W., 39, OCCUPATION: airman and photographer, who provided with his family on a regular vacation in Port Austin, Michigan. 
On 6 August 1952, the witness was standing on the front porch of his home facing in a southwesterly direction. A relative passed by and called his attention to an unusual light in the sky. The witness states that he saw a luminous object, approximately 1,000 feet in altitude, which came from the northeast and crossed his line of sight in a southwesterly direction in about 8 seconds. The object appeared to be slightly longer than a standard-size cigarette and emitted a yellow-green glow. The size and glow of the object were in marked contrast with the surrounding darkness. The witness reports that the object disappeared on a straight-line course. The witness was greatly impressed by the speed and the manner in which the object vanished from sight.
