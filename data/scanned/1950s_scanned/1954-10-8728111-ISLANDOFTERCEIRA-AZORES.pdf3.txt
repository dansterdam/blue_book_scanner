AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

REPORT OF INVESTIGATION - ISLAND OF TERCEIRA, AZORES.

1. AT APPROXIMATELY 1820 HOURS ON 8 OCTOBER 1954, TWO OFFICERS, BOTH MEMBERS OF THE 65TH AIR DIVISION MAINTENANCE GROUP, OBSERVED TWO UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS OVER THE ISLAND OF TERCEIRA, AZORES.

2. THE OBJECTS WERE FIRST OBSERVED AT AN ESTIMATED ALTITUDE OF 3000 TO 4000 FEET. THEY WERE DESCRIBED AS BEING CIRCULAR IN SHAPE AND BRIGHT ORANGE IN COLOR. THE OBJECTS APPEARED TO GLIDE AT A STEADY SPEED OF APPROXIMATELY 700 MPH FROM EAST TO WEST ACROSS THE ISLAND.

3. THE OBJECTS WERE UNDER OBSERVATION FOR APPROXIMATELY 10 MINUTES. WHEN LAST OBSERVED, THEY CONTINUED THEIR WESTERLY COURSE OUT TO SEA.

4. DETAILED VISUAL OBSERVATIONS AND DESCRIPTIONS OF THE OBJECTS WERE OBTAINED FROM BOTH OBSERVERS. NO SOUNDS WERE HEARD DURING THE SIGHTING.

5. THIS REPORT IS SUBMITTED FOR YOUR INFORMATION AND APPROPRIATE ACTION.
