AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT 
UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECTS
Fort Eustis, Virginia

1. INCIDENT:
22 April 52
Unidentified aerial objects observed.

2. DETAILS: 
The objects were first observed at approximately 1230 hours on 22 April 52 by Tech Sgt Sahlstrom, Headquarters Battery, 5th AAA Automatic Weapons Battalion, stationed at Fort Eustis, Virginia. Tech Sgt Sahlstrom was outside the Headquarters Battery area when he observed two (2) objects in the sky approximately 30 degrees above the horizon in the northwest quadrant. The objects were moving in a southerly direction at a high rate of speed and were observed for approximately 10-15 seconds. 

3. OBJECT DESCRIPTION:
The objects were substantially smaller than an aircraft, circular in shape, and were observed to change direction at acute angles. No smoke or exhaust was visible and no sound was heard.

4. WEATHER:
The weather was clear, visibility was good, and the wind was from the west at 10-15 knots.

5. INVESTIGATION:
To get accurate or aircraft equipped with better-than-average performance was negative. The investigation at Fort Eustis indicated that there were no experimental or test flights in progress at the time of the sightings.
