CONFIDENTIAL
DEPARTMENT OF THE AIR FORCE
HEADQUARTERS UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
WASHINGTON
UNCLASSIFIED
25 D 21
27 May 1952
SUBJECT: Sighting of Two Unusual Objects over Artesia, New Mexico, on 16 January 1952.
SPECIAL INQUIRY - UNCONVENTIONAL AIRCRAFT
TO:
Chief
Air Technical Intelligence Center
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Ohio
ATTN: ATIC
1. Reference is made to letter, this office, dated 15 April 1952, subject: "Sighting of Two Unusual Objects over Artesia, New Mexico on 16 January 1952".
2. Forwarded herewith are two copies of the report of investigation by Special Agent JACK H. LANKBR, AFOSI District #13, dated 30 April 1952.
3. As reported, the sighting of two unusual objects while watching an astronomical research balloon on 16 January 1952. These objects appeared and disappeared several times in connection with the balloon flight, and when first sighted were approximately five degrees above the balloon which was at an approximate altitude of one hundred thousand feet. The objects appeared to be the same size as the balloon and proved in a straight line through thirty degrees of azimuth in approximately three seconds and then disappeared.
4. In the event further investigative reports are received in this regard, they will be forwarded to the above captioned office from your headquarters.
5. Your attention is invited to paragraph 7, AFR 205-1, which prohibits the disclosure of classified information to unauthorized personnel.
B. E. MARTIN
Lt Colonel, USAF
District Commander
