The following is a report from Capt. Donald K. Clayton, AC-677 597, of the 133rd AC&W Squadron, Wold-Chamberlain Field.
This report concerns a three (3) to four (4) minute observation of an unidentified object on 12 December 1951, at 7:50 p.m.
Visibility on this day was exceptionally good. Observation was at 15,000 feet. The object was observed for a total of 3 to 4 minutes and was very bright, approximately the size of a bright star, but much brighter. No sound was heard and no trail was observed. The estimated distance of the aircraft was at 10,000 feet, and no estimate of distance was obtained.
