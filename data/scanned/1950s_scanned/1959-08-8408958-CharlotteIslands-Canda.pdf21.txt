HEADQUARTERS
920 BOMBARDMEMT WING
UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
FAIRCHILD AIR FORCE BASE, WASHINGTON

REPLY TO
ATTN OF: DCOI

SUBJECT: (U) Unidentified Flying Object

TO: ATIC, Wright Patterson AFB, Ohio

1. In answer to your unclassified message AGIN 4E 9-1350E, dated 11 September 1959, the following is submitted:
a. Aircraft were flying a call of three aircraft in trail formation. About 1 mile separated each plane. (U)
b. The distance between the object and the aircraft varied from one to five miles. (U)
c. The rotating beacon was red. According to the gunner who witnessed the beacon visually, it was on an aircraft that had neither navigation lights nor anti-collision lights and in this gunner's opinion there was no other aircraft that could be seen. (U)
d. It was determined that the beacon was in the same general location as the radar targets. (U)
e. The beacon appeared to stay about the same brightness and size while it was observed. (U)
f. The beacon was viewed continuously (visually) without a break for about 10 or 12 minutes. (U)
g. The heading reported in the original report was not a magnetic heading. The magnetic heading was 162°. There was 100 knot drift and the variation was -25°. (U)
h. Aircraft did not vary course at any time except at regularly scheduled turning points. (U)
i. The ceiling and visibility reported was for the area of the sighting. (U)
j. The radar targets did close on the aircraft. The distance varied from 17.5 miles to about one mile. When the distance was within one mile the targets left the scope. (U)

UNCLASSIFIED
