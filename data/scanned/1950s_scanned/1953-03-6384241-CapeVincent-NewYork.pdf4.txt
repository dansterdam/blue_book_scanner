On 29 MARCH 1953, the following information was received:
At approximately 0525 EST on 29 MARCH 1953, the following incident was observed:
6:00 P.M. at Cape Vincent, New York, station # 10 H.D. notified this station that a U.S. Air Force Officer, Capt. ALEXANDER, phone 3991, observed an unidentified flying object over Cape Vincent for approximately 15-20 minutes. The object was described as a low flying blue-white disc, moving at a low altitude. Capt. ALEXANDER reported the object remained in sight approximately 15-30 minutes each time, and could be seen through the clouds.
The authenticity of this report seems reliable to a fair degree since four persons observed this object.
Weather in that vicinity was 8/10 overcast with winds southwest at 11.
