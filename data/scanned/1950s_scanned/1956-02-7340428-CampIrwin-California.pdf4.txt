27 February 1956

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects (U) 

1. During the dates 3 February - 21 February 1956, sightings of unidentified flying objects were observed visually and in three instances by radar oscilloscope, operated by the 6702 AAA-AW BATTALION, at CAMP IRWIN, CALIFORNIA.

2. On 6 February 1956 between the hours 1300 - 1600, PFC RICHARD L. WALKER, Co "D", 4th BN, 81st Armd. Regt., observed an unidentified flying object moving from north to south at an altitude of over 15,000 feet.

PFC RICHARD L. WALKER also from Co "D" 4th BN, 81st Armd. Regt., observed an unidentified flying object moving from north to south at an altitude of over 15,000 feet. The object was described as oval in shape, brilliant luminosity, and no trail. It moved with a tremendous burst of speed from grid square 114 to grid square 115 in about four (4) minutes.

Officer(s) from the Air Force Intelligence personnel visited this installation for investigation and questioned witnesses, but could make no official determination as to the nature of the observations.

Results of radar data cannot be provided.
