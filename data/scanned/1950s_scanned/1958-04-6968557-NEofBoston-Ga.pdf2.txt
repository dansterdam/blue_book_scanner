19 AUG 1959

SUBJECT: ATTN: Major L. J. Tacker

1. In response to a request by Mr. the following information is furnished:

On 9 April 1959, 0342 hours - the observer reported a fiery object, estimated at 2,500 feet altitude, traveling in a northwesterly direction at a high rate of speed. The object was observed for approximately one minute, during which time it changed color from orange to white. It then increased in speed and disappeared. The object was observed by two additional witnesses as well.

On 16 September 1958, 0815 hours - at an observer reported a round, bright object at a 45 degree elevation, traveling in a northerly direction at a high rate of speed. The object was observed for approximately 30 seconds before it disappeared.

On 21 December 1958, observers, Mr. reported a large, bright object, round in shape, which was observed for approximately 5 minutes as it traveled in a northeasterly direction. The object was estimated to be at an altitude of approximately 10,000 feet.

2. In accordance with current instructions, the reports of these sightings have been included in the files of this headquarters. No further action is contemplated at this time.

FOR THE DIRECTOR:

GEORGE K. HAMPTON, Major, USAF
Acting Chief, Technical Analysis Division
