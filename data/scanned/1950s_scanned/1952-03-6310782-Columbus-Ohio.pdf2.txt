AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

AREA WHERE OBJECT WAS OBSERVED: Columbus, Ohio
DATE/TIME GROUP: 12 March 1952 

SUMMARY: On 12 March 1952 a report was received from Lockbourne Air Force Base, Ohio, concerning a bright light flying at a high rate of speed from NW to SE, observed by several air force personnel. The object was described as being a long, shiny and wingless object, tapering from a circular north to a point in the south. It was traveling at an extremely high rate of speed.

At about 1:15 p.m. several Lockbourne AFB personnel observed the object through binoculars. A pilot and his instructor on a T-33 training mission also observed the object. The object was visible for 2 to 3 minutes. Several airmen volunteered their names as observers.
