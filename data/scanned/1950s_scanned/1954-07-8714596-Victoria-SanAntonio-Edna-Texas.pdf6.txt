Foster AFB, Victoria, Tex

1. Description of the object:

   a. Shape - Only saw a bluish burst of light, traveling north eastward and slightly diving.

   b. Size compared to a known object (use one of the following terms: head of a pin, bead, dime, nickel, quarter, half dollar, silver dollar, baseball, grapefruit, volleyball) held in the hand at about arm's length: At least a basketball, only much larger.

   c. Color - Blue.

   d. Number - One.

   e. Any discernible features or details. Left streak of fire behind, similar to a comet.

   f. Tail, trail, or exhaust, including size of same compared to size of object. Left streak of flame, whole flame similar to shape of wing gas tanks.

   g. Sound. If heard, describe sound. None.

   h. Was just a big ball of blue flame.

2. Description of course of object: Was north of Edna, Tex.

   a. What first called the attention of observer to the object: By bright flash.

   b. Angle of elevation and azimuth of the object when first observed: Passed to right, First saw it approximately 4,000', was slightly diving and passed below and to right of us, heading in north east direction.

   c. Angle of elevation and azimuth of object upon disappearance. Approximately 1500' heading in slight diving attitude in north east direction.

   d. Description of flight path and maneuvers of object: Was slightly diving during our observance of object.

   e. Manner of disappearance of object: Flame vanished after few seconds.

   f. Length of time in sight: 10 or 15 seconds.

3. Manner of observation:

   a. Use one or any combination of the following items: Ground-visual, air-visual, ground-electronic, air-electronic. (If electronic, specify type or radar). Air-visual.

   b. Statement as to optical aids: None.

   c. If the sighting is made while airborne, give type aircraft, identification number, altitude, heading, speed and home station: DC3 aircraft, do not
