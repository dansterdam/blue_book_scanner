1. The operational intelligence division, headquarters air force, air materiel command, 23 January 1953, has the following information:
This sighting was made by observers of the 1007th AACS squadron, March Air Force Base, while on a routine flight from March Air Force Base to San Francisco, California, on 23 January 1953. The sighting took place approximately 12 miles southwest of Corona, California, between 2000 and 2015 hours, 23 January 1953, and the sighting continued approximately 12 minutes.
Eleven crew members aboard the aircraft reported observing 4 bright, round objects, approximately 30 to 40 feet in diameter. These objects were first observed in a horizontal line, flying at an estimated altitude of 10,000 to 15,000 feet, and a speed of approximately 400 to 500 mph. The objects appeared to be metallic in color, showed no lights, made no sound, and executed quick, darting maneuvers. The objects were observed to split up, reform, and disappear and reappear several times during the 12 minute sighting. There was no physical evidence to substantiate this sighting.
