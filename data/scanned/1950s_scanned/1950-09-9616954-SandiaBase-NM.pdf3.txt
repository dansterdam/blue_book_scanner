SPOT INTELLIGENCE REPORT

SUBJECT: Unidentified Aerial Phenomena
TO:    Director of Special Investigations
       Headquarters, USAF
       Washington 25, D. C.

The following information was received from Headquarters, 4th
Army, Fort Sam Houston, Texas:

1. Synopsis: On 29 September 1950, a civilian observed an unidentified aerial object. Observation was made at 0645 hours, at which time a jeep was driving a Jeep on the Isleta road area of Sandia AFB.

2. Details of Observation - 29 September 1950:
a. Time of observation - 0645 hours (local).
b. Exact time of observation - 0645 hours (local).
c. Place of observation - Isleta Area, Sandia AFB.
d. Position of observer - Iglob area in Jeep -- south road along Coyote Springs Jeep Road.
e. What attracted attention to object - Movement or flight.
f. Number of objects observed - 1 (one).
g. Approx. angular size of object - Appears to be one-third the size of the full moon.
h. Color of object - Bluish-yellow flame, such as exhaust.
i. Shape - Appears cylindrically elongated with a cut-off rear from
which flame and sparks were coming.
j. Altitude of object (distance above horizon - 0° at horizon, 90° overhead) - approx. 50 degrees from zenith.
k. Duration of observation - 3 seconds.
l. Direction of flight of object - (Angle of flight) - Northeast to southwest (toward north) - approximately 50 degrees from north.
m. Distance from observer (distance to town, bldg, etc.) - over 3 mile.
n. Direction of flight or object - Northeast to southeast.
o. Time of flight - Approximately 3-4 seconds.
p. Speed - Approximately 350 to 400 MPH.
q. Trail and odor - None.
r. Trail - Bluish with yellow fringes. Length: 4-5 feet. DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS; 
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS
