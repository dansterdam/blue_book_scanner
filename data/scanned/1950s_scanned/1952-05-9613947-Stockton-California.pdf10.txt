AF FORM 112 - PART I

In accordance with AFR 200-2, Unidentified Flying Objects Reporting, the following report is forwarded:
AT 1940 PST, 22 MAY 52, AN UNIDENTIFIED AERIAL OBJECT WAS SIGHTED IN THE SKY OVER THE STOCKTON NAVAL SUPPLY STATION, STOCKTON, CALIFORNIA. 
There was only one (1) object observed. (2) People observed the object and reported it to the Intelligence Division, 159th Infantry Division, California National Guard. 
A civilian, a 1st Lt. in the Army Reserve, who took a reading by using an established sextant, estimated the object to be at an altitude of 30,000 feet.
The object was visible for a period of 25 minutes, then faded away. 
During observation, the object appeared to stop at 10,000 feet, then to ascend at an angle to 20,000, 30,000, 36,000 feet.
The Stockton Municipal Airport Station, Stockton, California was notified and an Air Weather Service Section and 4620th Air Base Wing notified, but no aircraft were at the reported location when they arrived. 
No further information could be obtained on the 22 May 1952, Results were negative.
The information was obtained on two radar tracks at approximately 1200 hours, but tracks faded before detailed information could be obtained.

RED E. MCINTOSH
Major, USAF
Chief Intelligence
