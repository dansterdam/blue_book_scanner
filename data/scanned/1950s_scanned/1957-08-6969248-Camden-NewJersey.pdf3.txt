1. SYNOPSIS: Mr. HALLORAN (ONI) BOYCE, interviewed 30 August 1957, stated that while en route to work in Camden, New Jersey, he observed a glowing bell-shaped object, the size of a grapefruit, climb and then dip out of sight. He could give no estimate of speed or unusual weather or celestial activity noted on that date.

2. DETAILS: 
AT PHILADELPHIA, PENNSYLVANIA
a. On 30 August 1957, Mr. HALLORAN, age 45, was interviewed at U. S. Naval Supply Depot, 700 Robbins Avenue, by Special Agent DOUGLAS T. REYNOLDS.
b. HALLORAN advised that at 0700 hours, 23 August 1957, while proceeding to work from his home, he observed a bell-shaped silver, glowing object at arms' length above the ground, which suddenly disappeared. He further advised that while the object was performing the maneuver it was also following a straight and steady course. He stated that he saw no trail or vapor, nor did he hear any sound. He estimated the object's size as approximately 4-5" when he first sighted it and at approximately 2-3" when it disappeared. Total duration of time was approximately one (1) minute.
c. HALLORAN further stated that he saw no land or air traffic in the area and that the object was not picked up by any local radar installations.
