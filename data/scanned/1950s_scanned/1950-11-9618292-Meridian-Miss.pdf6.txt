In compliance with United States Air Force letter, dated 8 September 1950, subject: "Reporting of Information on Unconventional Aircraft," the following information is submitted:

a. Object sighted was long and slender having the appearance of a parachute with no shrouds. It gave off a green-white glow. It flew in a wing-low attitude and gradually righted itself, finally disappearing due to darkness: in COLOR, white or silver-white with no shine.

b. Object was sighted 13 November 1950 at 1715 B and remained visible until darkness. Length of time observed was approximately one hour.

c. Sighted visually from a C-47 type aircraft which was flying at approximately 10,000 feet.

d. Observers first sighted the object at approximately 25 mi. south of Meridian, Miss. Object appeared to be at an altitude of 29 degrees. Object was on a heading of 295 degrees, 10 to 15 degrees above the horizon, and was finally lost at Vicksburg, Miss. due to darkness.

e. Witnesses: Capt. [Redacted] / Lt. [Redacted] / M/Sgt. [Redacted]

This report is classified as a C-2.

f. Weather was CAVOK and the wind 5 to 10 knots at 210 degrees;
sky was a red haze with the sunset.

g. No unusual weather or meteorological condition known.
