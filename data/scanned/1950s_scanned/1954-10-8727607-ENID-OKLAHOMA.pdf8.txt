AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

DATE: 8 October 1954
SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT REPORTED NEAR ENID, OKLAHOMA
LOCATION: ENID, OKLAHOMA
DATE/TIME GROUP: 10/0030Z

1. On 10 October 1954, at approximately 0030Z, a pilot of the 97th Bomb Wing, Altus AFB, Oklahoma, reported sighting an unidentified flying object near Enid, Oklahoma.

2. The pilot stated that while flying at 8,000 feet, he observed a bright white light at approximately 6,000 feet and about 3 miles northeast of his position. The light appeared to be stationary and was estimated to be 10-15 feet in diameter. After approximately 2 minutes, the light suddenly accelerated and disappeared from sight.

3. The pilot was flying a B-47 type aircraft and was on a routine night navigation training mission. Weather conditions were clear with unlimited visibility.

4. No other aircraft were in the area at the time of the incident.

5. This report has been forwarded to the Air Technical Intelligence Center (ATIC), Wright-Patterson AFB, Ohio, for further analysis and evaluation.
