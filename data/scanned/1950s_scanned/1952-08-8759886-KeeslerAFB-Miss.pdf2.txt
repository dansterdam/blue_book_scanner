INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
SUBJECT: Reporting of Information on Unidentified Flying Objects.
LOCATION: Keesler AFB, Mississippi
DATE OF INFORMATION: 19 AUGUST 1952
SOURCE: WITNESS: T/SGT. W. T. FOWLER, 608TH, USAF, BOMBWR WING, KEESLER AFB, MISSISSIPPI.
SUMMARY: (Unclassified) Reporting of Information on Unidentified Flying Objects.
AS REQUESTED, THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION IS SUBMITTED:
ON 19 AUGUST 1952, AT APPROXIMATELY 0810 HOURS, SGT. FOWLER OBSERVED SEVERAL UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS IN THE SKY OVER KEESLER AFB, MISSISSIPPI. HE ESTIMATED THERE WERE EIGHT (8) TO TEN (10) OBJECTS WHICH APPEARED TO BE METALLIC IN COLOR AND SAUCER-SHAPED. THEY WERE OBSERVED AT AN APPROXIMATE ALTITUDE OF 8,000 TO 10,000 FEET AND WERE TRAVELING AT A VERY HIGH RATE OF SPEED. THE OBJECTS WERE FLYING IN A CIRCULAR PATTERN AND WOULD ACCELERATE AND DECELERATE RAPIDLY. THEY SEEMED TO EXHIBIT A PULSATING MOTION AND WOULD CHANGE COLOR FROM RED TO GREEN TO WHITE.

THE OBJECTS WERE OBSERVED FOR APPROXIMATELY 8-10 MINUTES. SGT. FOWLER ESTIMATED THEY WERE TRAVELING AT A SPEED OF APPROXIMATELY 500-600 KNOTS. NO SOUND WAS HEARD AND NO EXHAUST TRAILS WERE OBSERVED.

IT WAS ESTIMATED THAT THE OBSERVATION WAS ESTIMATED AT BEING FIVE (5) TO TEN (10) SECONDS. HOWEVER, SGT. FOWLER STATED THAT DURING THIS OBSERVATION, TIME SEEMED TO BE DISTORTED AND HIS PERCEPTION OF THE LENGTH OF TIME WAS UNCERTAIN.

APPROVED:
LT COL, US AIR FORCE
INTELLIGENCE OFFICER
