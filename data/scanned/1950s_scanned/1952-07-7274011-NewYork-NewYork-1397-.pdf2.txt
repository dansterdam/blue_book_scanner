DEPARTMENT OF THE AIR FORCE
OFFICE OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
NEW YORK, NEW YORK

SUBJECT: REPORT OF UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS

1. On 7 July 1952, Special Agent SAC ALVIN B. SCHARTZ of the Cleveland, Ohio Office of Special Investigations, received a telephone call from SAC JOSEPH J. DOLEZAL of the New York, New York Office of Special Investigations. SAC DOLEZAL advised that he had received a call from JULIUS GLISH, an employee of the New York Office of Air Defense Division, Headquarters, U.S. Air Force.

2. GLISH advised SAC DOLEZAL that he had observed several unidentified flying objects over the New York City area on the evening of 6 July 1952. He stated that the objects were traveling in a scattered formation at a high rate of speed and at an estimated altitude of 1,000 to 2,000 feet. GLISH estimated that the objects were between 15 to 20 feet in diameter. GLISH advised that he had reported his observations to the New York Air Defense Filter Center.

3. SAC DOLEZAL advised SAC SCHARTZ that he would keep him informed of any further developments in this matter.
