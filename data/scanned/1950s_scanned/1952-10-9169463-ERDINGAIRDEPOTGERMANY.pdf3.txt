SUMMARY: MCD 5224, dated 30 October 1952.

The following information pertains to the sighting of an unidentified flying object in the vicinity of the Erding Air Depot, Germany, on 29 October 1952.

At approximately 0750 hours local time (Central European) on Wednesday, 29 October 1952, a soldier on guard duty at the operations building, Erding Air Depot, observed an unidentified flying object in the sky to the southwest of the depot. It was first sighted approaching from a distance and then passed overhead, continuing in a northeasterly direction.

The object appeared to be circular in shape, metallic in color, and was estimated to be approximately 30 to 40 feet in diameter. It was silently traveling at a speed estimated to be between 400 and 500 miles per hour at an altitude estimated to be between 6,000 and 7,500 feet.

The object was observed for a period of approximately 30 seconds. During the time it was under observation, no sound or exhaust was detected, and there was no evidence of any trail or vapor stream.

A check by the Operations Officer on duty at the radar site at the time of observation met with negative results.
