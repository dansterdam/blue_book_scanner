HEADQUARTERS UNITED STATES AIR FORCE
WASHINGTON

13TH DISTRICT OFFICE OF SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS
Offutt Air Force Base, Omaha, Nebraska

21 July 1952

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Object Reportedly Sighted over Omaha, 29 July 1952

TO: Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, USAF
Washington 25, D.C.

1. GV-12P3192 Three (3) individuals reported the sighting of an unidentified flying object at approximately 2355 hours, 29 July 1952 at Omaha, Nebraska. 

2. Employees of United Air Lines (name(s) deleted) reported to this office that on 29 July 1952 at approximately 2355 hours, they observed a reddish-orange colored object, about one foot in diameter, traveling in a circular motion at a high rate of speed at an estimated altitude of 500 to 1000 feet. The object was observed for approximately 10 to 15 minutes and then disappeared in a southeasterly direction. The employees stated that the object was definitely not an aircraft or astronomical phenomena and was not blinking or flashing. It was described as a steady, reddish-orange glow, traveling in a circular path.

3. It was added that the object remained in sight for about ten minutes, and that it is presumed the object had landed as it disappeared and did not reappear. The informants stated that her husband had also witnessed the object.

4. She stated that her husband had also been a witness to the same information as her information given.

5. Also reported to this office that he had sighted a round, disc-shaped object which disappeared in a southeasterly direction at approximately 2140 hours, 29 July 1952. He further stated that he had previously reported this, and that he was a former employee of the Omaha Air Force Base.

It was opined that the unidentified object may have been the spotlight, but that this was the only information he had been able to obtain.

DEGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS
