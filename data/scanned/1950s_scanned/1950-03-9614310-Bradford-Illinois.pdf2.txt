Report contains information on seven alleged sightings of unidentified flying objects observed by various USAF flight service centers during March 1950. The objects were described as flying at high speed, unusual course, and sudden stops. 
APPROVED:
C. J. WELLIAN
Colonel, USAF
Chief, Intelligence Division
As of 07:30, 15 March 1950, an unidentified object was observed over Bradford, Illinois, by the pilot of a commercial airliner flight from Chicago to Keokuk, Iowa. The pilot reported the sighting through the Burlington (Iowa) radio station to the 4602d Air Intelligence Service Center. No photographs or records of the object were received by the Wright-Patterson Flight Service Center, but the pilot declared the object to be shaped like a disc, about one mile per hour and it was headed southeast. The pilot stated that the object appeared to be at an altitude of about 12,000 feet and was observed for approximately 5 minutes.
The weather at Bradford, Illinois, as of 07:30, 15 March 1950, was estimated 25,000 feet broken.
