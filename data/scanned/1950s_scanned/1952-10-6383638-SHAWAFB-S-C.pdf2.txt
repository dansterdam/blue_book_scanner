HEADQUARTERS 
118TH TACTICAL RECONNAISSANCE GROUP 
Shaw Air Force Base 
South Carolina

ROC/T 360
SUBJECT: Report of Sighting of Unconventional Aircraft

TO: Commanding General
Air Material Command
ATTN: ECTS
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton, Ohio

1. In accordance with AF Ltr. AFOTC-CC-1, dated 8 September 1950, the following report of Sightings of Unconventional Aircraft is submitted:

a. Description: A brilliant white light, three times as intense as sunlight, steady in brightness center, diminishing toward edges, with no halo or radiation; circular in shape, and comparative size approximately 20 to 30 feet in diameter. No discernible aerodynamic features, trail or exhaust, propulsion system or sound. Sped under tremendous speed, but considered to be silent, impossible maneuvers performed, following maneuvers: Horizontal flight, vertical flight, still flight, sudden turn, extreme speed without approaching aircraft, lost contact with object.

b. Object was sighted at 1537 hours, Eastern Standard time, 1 October 1952, and remained in visual contact until approximately 1920 hours.

c. Object was observed from one RB-29 aircraft traveling at 270 MPH indicated at 22,000 feet. Last visual contact was at approximately 1920 hours, as object faded from view.

d. Location of sighting aircraft: 34° 06'N - 80° 34'W on a heading of 045° true, for approximately 10 minutes. Object appeared to left of aircraft's nose. An unknown distance, passed in front of aircraft's nose from right to left, to a position, came to what appeared to be a complete stop, resumed flight, going to a stop at 10 o'clock to the sighting aircraft. Object remained slightly below altitude of sighting aircraft.
