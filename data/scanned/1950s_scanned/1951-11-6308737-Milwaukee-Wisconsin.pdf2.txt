SOURCE: 20th Air Division (Defs), 3rd, Ind, Cth Div, 26 July 1951, AIR-2c.

An airline pilot of Capitol Airlines, while enroute from Des Moines, Iowa, to Chicago, Illinois, observed at 26/10232 a dark colored object which passed in front of his plane at a very high rate of speed. This object was approximately 25 miles due east of Capital Airlines' flight, and left a blue exhaust trail behind it.
2. The next day, 27/10232, the same Capitol Airlines pilot on a DC-3 trip, 24 apparently observed the same object as on the previous day, as it passed in the same easterly direction although he did not remember any orange color.
3. University of Michigan Observatory at Ann Arbor reports that no unusual activity to the skies was observed.
4. Weather at Milwaukee and Chicago was overcast at 1,000 feet, visibility 10 to 10 miles.
5. 20th Division radar station with pertinent area coverage indicates no record of the sightings.
6. Check of ADDC and MPS records indicate no known jet aircraft activity in the area.
7. Observers listed are both experienced pilots and appear to be stable, sincere persons.
