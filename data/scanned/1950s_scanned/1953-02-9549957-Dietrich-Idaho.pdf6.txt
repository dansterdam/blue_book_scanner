AF FORM 112 -- PART II
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

Document #3
477th Ground Observer Squadron
Boise, Idaho
15-2-53

The unidentified flying object was described as follows: silver in color. There was no indication as to type of propulsion and no sound was audible. Objects 15 inches to 3 feet in diameter on trailing wire. Size unknown, however observers reported there were two (2) or three (3) objects. The single object was observed on the definition of the Boise Airport control tower. The objects went in a zig zag motion at the time the planes were overhead. Reports indicate the objects were treetop to aircraft level.

1. Time of first observation the object appeared to be at a low altitude disappearance.

2. The object was first sighted about 2215 Z, 3 February 1953 and was observed for several minutes.

3. Observations were made from ground level. Object was first sighted by naked eye and then observed for about thirty (30) minutes through sight (3) power binoculars.

4. The object was sighted overhead and slightly South of a position 11° 15' East 142° 33' North, over Dietrich, Idaho. Altitude of first sighting about seven-thousand (7000) feet disappearing to the West at an unknown altitude.

5. Names of witnesses: Chief Observer at Dietrich, Idaho. Age 39, Supervisor at Dietrich, Idaho. Age 55, Farmer, Age 30, Farmer, Age 60.

All of the observers should be fairly accurate. Mr.__ and Mrs.__ have submitted reports on Air Force training exercises, aircraft, etc. All observers reside at Dietrich, Idaho.

6. Estimated speed over 500 mph. Estimated altitude over 25,000 feet, developed 30,000 feet and 60,000 feet according to the observers.

There was no known activities or conditions that could account for the sighting.
