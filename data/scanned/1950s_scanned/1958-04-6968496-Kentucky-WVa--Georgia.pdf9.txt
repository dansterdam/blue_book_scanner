LETTER DATED APRIL 21, --(UP)--THE NAVY BEGAN AN INVESTIGATION, MAY 7. WITH THE CRASH OF A NAVY JET FIGHTER PLANE SAID TO HAVE CRASHED NEAR GEORGIA AS A RESULT OF PILOT BEING BLACKED OUT. 
THE NAVY SAID MARIE CORP. CAPT.---OF COLUMBUS, OHIO, WAS EJECTED FROM RESEARCH PLANE, AS THE COLLISION OCCURRED. PILOT WAS TAKEN TO THE GENERAL HOSPITAL.
MEMBERS OF THE NAVY AIRCRAFT ACCIDENT BOARD WERE SCHEDULED TO ARRIVE HERE TO INVESTIGATE THE CRASH.
MR. AND MRS. GEORGE UNIT, WHO LIVE NEAR WHERE THE PLANE CRASHED, SAID THEY SAW AN ORANGE-COLORED, OVAL-SHAPED GLOW EXPLOSION AND THEN SMOKE.
THE PLANE CRASHED NEAR THE INTERSECTION OF TWO MAIN EXPRESSWAYS, ABOUT A MILE AND A HALF FROM SHERROD'S CORNER AIR FIELD.
THE UNIDENTIFIED AIRMAN ENROUTE TO HOSPITAL BY JEFFERSON COUNTY POLICE.
