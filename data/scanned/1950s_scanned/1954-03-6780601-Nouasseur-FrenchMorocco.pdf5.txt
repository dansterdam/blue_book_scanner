SUPPLEMENT TO AF FORM 112

WASHINGTON, D.C. 20333 
4 March 1954

The following information on reported unidentified flying objects is furnished by the Assistant Chief of Staff, Intelligence, Headquarters USAF.

"As approximately 2147-2154Z, the pilot of F-94, 5-3577 reported looking southeast and observing a strange light at an altitude of 30,000 feet and at a distance of 35 miles. The light appeared to be an intense white light and seemed to suddenly increase in size and brightness, and then diminish in size and to finally disappear. The pilot stated the light seemed to increase and decrease in size in a pulsing manner.

"The radar observer in the F-94 also observed the light which appeared on his radar scope at a range of 35 miles. He stated the light appeared on his scope for approximately 1 minute and then disappeared. The radar observer also observed a "second target" moving in the vicinity of the light."

Air Intell, 3/5/54 - The following information on the 1400 z - 2400 GMT, 7 Feb 54 incident was received from the USAF air attache, Rabat, French Morocco:

"As approximately 2230 GMT, the pilot of an F-84, FR-300, sighting an unidentified luminous object maneuvering at high speed above the clouds. The pilot attempted to close in on the object but was unable to maintain visual contact and also lost radar contact with it.

"The object appeared to be luminous and of a round shape. It moved at a high rate of speed and made abrupt turns and changes in altitude. The pilot stated that the object was several times brighter than the stars and the moon. The pilot was unable to approach within closer than approximately one mile of the object."
