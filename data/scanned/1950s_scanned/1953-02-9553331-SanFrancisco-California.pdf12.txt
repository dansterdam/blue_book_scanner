AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT UNCLASSIFIED
FROM: Dir. of Intel., 25 AD
Hamilton AFB, California

An unidentified flying object was observed by four members of a GGC Post located on top of Mt. Sutro in San Francisco, California during the evening of 13 February 1953.

The description of object (one ground visual appearance) which appeared to have metallic characteristics and be larger than a conventional aircraft. No means of propulsion were observed. A throbbing sound was heard apparently coming from the object as it passed overhead where the observers were located. The unidentified flying object disappeared from view over the western horizon. The object was estimated to be moving at a speed estimated to be slower than that of a conventional aircraft.

Time of sighting was between 1755 and 1820 PST, 13 February 1953. No other observations with this object were reported.

Location of observers during sighting was on top of Mount Sutro, San Francisco, California. The unidentified object was estimated by the observers to be approximately 10,000 feet altitude.

The observers of this object were: San Francisco, Calif., San Bernardino, Calif.

The weather was clear with a few scattered clouds. Visibility was reported as unlimited.

According to a newspaper article concerning similar sightings of unidentified flying objects which appeared in the San Francisco Examiner dated 16 February 1953, members of the University of California Astronomy Department stated that the sightings could have been caused by a temperature inversion on 13 February 1953.
