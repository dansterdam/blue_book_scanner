AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
Radar Observation of Unidentified Objects near Wiesbaden, Germany, 7 July 1950.
INTELLIGENCE DIVISION, DEPT. USAF
Andrews Air Base, Washington 25, D.C.
6 September 1950
16 July 1950
USAF airman
The Security Officer of the 1947th MACS Squadron, Wiesbaden, Germany, reports
the following information: On the night of 7 July 1950, while directing the landing of a US aircraft at Wiesbaden, he observed through the CCA (GCA) radar scope an unidentified target.
The sources stated that after observing the new target for several minutes,
it appeared the speed of the unidentified objects surpassed the speed of the aircraft under control.
The Wiesbaden control tower operator was contacted after the observation, he
stated that no jet aircraft were known to be in the area at that time.
HQ MATS COMMENT:
An airman of the 1947th MACS Squadron, occupying quarters in the town of
Wiesbaden reported to the organization's Security Officer that he had sounds similar
to those made by the fighter type aircraft at about 2200 on 7 July 1950. The airman
reported no visual sightings were made.
