UNCLASSIFIED

SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT SIGHTED BY WARREN SIMOOND ON 15 MAY 1955
FILE NO: 24-1933

DETAILS:
1. This investigation was directed by the Commander, 2d District OSI, based upon information received in this office that the object observed was a "flying saucer".
2. The further was assisted in this investigation by Special Agents WILLIAM G. MacMILLAN, and FRANK AULD.
AT NEW YORK, NEW YORK
3. On 24 May 1955, Warren SIMOND, New York City, was interviewed by S/A MacMANN and S/A AULD, who described the unidentified flying object which he photographed as follows:
a. Description of Object:
1. Showed the object appeared oblong at first but as the angle of sight changed with the movement of the observer, the object appeared more circular.
2. Size compared to a known object held in the hand, was about one-half the size of a quarter.
3. Object exhibited none of the usual characteristics of known objects (such as reflection, shadow, trail or exhaust) except that as the object moved, its appearance changed from a gray-white to a gray-black color.
4. Number: One only.
5. Formation if more than one: Not applicable.
6. Tail, trail or exhaust: None.
7. Sound: None.
b. Description of Course of Object:
1. That first called his attention was when it came into view below the tree line. The sighting was as brief as the object went behind the woods and out of sight.
2. Angle of elevation and azimuth of object when first observed were 10-45 degrees.
3. Angle of elevation and azimuth of object upon disappearance were 15-60 degrees.
4. Duration of the sighting was about 2-3 seconds, as described the maneuvers of the object, which to his knowledge moved back and forth in a straight line and maneuvered to that extent while he was able to observe it.
5. Manner of disappearance of object: The object moved out of sight at a high speed. It disappeared at a distance.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS,
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
