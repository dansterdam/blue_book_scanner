Submitted in accordance with AF LTR No. 200-5, dated 20 April 1952, Subject: Unidentified Flying Cbjects Reporting.
Between the hours 1930 and 1945 MST, three (3) to five (5) unidentified objects were observed maneuvering at an estimated altitude of 10,000 to 20,000 feet. The objects were circular in shape, about the size of a basketball, and appeared to be reflecting the sun's rays. They traveled at a speed estimated to be in excess of 1,000 mph. Apparently returning to vicinity of first observations when finally lost from sight.
No sounds were emitted or exhaust reflections observed. Weather clear, visibility 65 miles, surface wind calm.
