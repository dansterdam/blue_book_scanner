1. Reference is made to DO 291 w/x, Cir 292, dated 13 April 1952.

2. Based on a report concerning an unusual aerial phenomenon by five (5) persons at Fort Eustis, Virginia, on 17 April 1952.

3. On 18 April 1952 Lt. Colonel ________ and Captain ________ interviewed the five (5) persons named in the above-mentioned report. These persons stated that at approximately 2155 hours, 17 April 1952, five (5) persons, including ________ and __________who were on guard duty, observed an object approximately 30 feet in diameter, traveling in a straight line from northeast to southwest at an estimated altitude of 500 to 800 feet for a period of approximately 10 to 15 seconds. The object did not change direction or speed during the observation. Neither sound nor exhaust was noticed. The object then appeared to go into the clouds and was not seen again.

4. On 18 April 1952 S/Sgt. ________ and Sgt. ________ were interviewed. The following information was obtained:
They had been on duty at the Fort Eustis Main Gate from approximately 2130 to 2230 hours, 17 April 1952. During this period they observed an object which appeared to be approximately 30 feet in diameter, traveling in a straight line at an estimated altitude of 500 to 800 feet from northeast to southwest. The object was observed for a period of approximately 10 to 15 seconds, during which time it did not change direction or speed. No sound or exhaust was noticed. The object then appeared to go into the clouds and was not seen again.
