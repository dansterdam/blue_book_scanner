AF FORM 112 - PART I
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects Observed Near March Air Force Base, Southern California
DATE OF INFORMATION: 18 September 1952

1. This is a report of alleged "flying saucers" in the vicinity of March Air Force Base, Southern California, on 18 September 1952.

2. A summary of the description follows:
a. The object was oval shaped; it left no vapor trail; no sound was noted and no propulsion system was visible. The object was estimated to be 25,000 to 30,000 feet in altitude. The object was observed for approximately 20 minutes. It was visible only while in the sun's rays. It was estimated to be travelling at a speed of 250 to 300 miles per hour.
b. The occurrence was at 1515 hours. The length of time observed was approximately 20 minutes.
c. The sighting was made by 2 Air Force airmen while they were watching the late afternoon sky over the March Air Force Base area. No other observers were in the immediate vicinity of one another.
d. No calibrating instruments were available. The 2 observers were in the immediate vicinity of one another.
e. No estimate of reliability can be attempted by the reporter.
f. Conditions were clear and unlimited. No report on wind conditions is available.
g. A rough sketch is enclosed.

1. (Lt Col) A/20 Darrell Clarksun
2. SAC 3/52 George F. Miller
