APPENDIX VI
George AFB, Calif. - 1, 2, 13, 14, 20 May 1952

DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT
The sighting on 1 May 1952 at George AFB, Calif., was the first of a series of line sightings there in a three-week period. These sightings were made by military personnel.

a. 1 May 1952, 1315 PDST - Disc-shaped objects, flat white in color which gave no glare or reflection were sighted. They were in formation with three in front and two in the rear. The action two darted around in a circular zig-zag manner.

b. 9 May 1952, 1030 PDST - An unidentified round, silver object was sighted at the height diminishing until it dropped out of sight. 

c. 9 May 1952, 1230 PDST - Two unidentified objects moving with the speed of a fast jet. They appeared to be a silver metallic color with a dark spot in the center and at certain angles to the sun gave off a bright glare.

d. 9 May 1952, 1720 PDST - Object was of a dull color like a thundercloud, changing shape but had no sound or dynamic features.

e. 11 May 1952, 1220 PDST - Object looked like a white paper plate, flipping end over end with an initial speed comparable to a jet although later it reduced its pace.

f. 13 May 1952, 1425 PDST - A single object, appeared round, shiny, metallic which closed or diffused white or silver was observed for thirty minutes.

g. 14 May 1952, 1405 to 1430 PDST - Same description as object sighted on 13 May and by same sources.

h. 20 May 1952, 1425 PDST - A silver-colored, bright, round object was observed for several minutes. Initially the object was stationary but eventually moved over the horizon.

STATUS OF INVESTIGATION
Only balloons released from Edwards AFB can be tracked accurately over George AFB, consequently, for the most part they are the only balloons with which we are concerned. The balloons cannot account for the strange air objects sighted in cases which are probably balloons.
