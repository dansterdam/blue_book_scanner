SUBJECT: Sighting of Unidentified Flying Objects in San Diego, California, on 17 December 1952, by U.S. Navy employees
TO:
Director of Special Investigations
Headquarters, USAF
Washington 25, D.C.
1. SYNOPSIS: Between 1715 and 1825 hours, 17 December 1952, four employees of the U.S. Navy Electronics Laboratory, San Diego, California, sighted several high-speed, disc-shaped objects which they concluded were definitely not conventional flying aircraft.
2. The District Intelligence Officer, Eleventh Naval District, San Diego, California, obtained statements from the employees of the U.S. Navy Electronics Laboratory, which has no inclination the statements unsatisfactory.
3. The sightings were made between 1715 and 1825 hours, 17 December 1952.
4. H. W. YOEMAN, Electronic Scientist, NACA, AMES AERONAUTICAL LABORATORY, and A. J. BOTHNER, Electronic Scientist, identified the objects.
5. The speed of the objects was described as being from the speed of present day jet aircraft to at least five times that speed or approximately 2000 miles per hour.
6. The shape of the objects was described as cigar-shaped or disc-like. The objects were observed to change their shape as they traveled, described as being round shapes as well defined at the edges.
7. While the characteristics of the objects appeared quite unusual, the employees did not state the objects emanated bright lights, but not dazzling.
