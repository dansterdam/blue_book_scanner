CASE HISTORY OF REPORTED UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS INCIDENT, TRUK ATOLL, PACIFIC OCEAN, 7 FEBRUARY 1953

1. ON 7 FEBRUARY 1953, A JAPANESE FISHING BOAT, APPROXIMATELY 50 FEET IN LENGTH, WAS FISHING ABOUT 15 MILES SOUTHEAST OF TRUK ATOLL IN THE PACIFIC OCEAN. THE WEATHER WAS CLEAR AT THE TIME.

2. AT APPROXIMATELY 1700 HOURS TRUK LOCAL TIME, THE FISHING CREW SIGHTED A BRIGHT LIGHT IN THE SKY APPROACHING THEM FROM THE NORTHEAST. THE LIGHT APPEARED TO BE CIRCULAR IN SHAPE AND EMITTING A BRIGHT REDDISH-ORANGE GLOW. IT MOVED RAPIDLY ACROSS THE SKY AND DISAPPEARED FROM VIEW AFTER APPROXIMATELY 30 SECONDS.

3. THERE WERE NO UNUSUAL SOUNDS OR OTHER SENSATIONS ASSOCIATED WITH THE SIGHTING.

4. DURING THE INTERVIEW WITH THE FISHERMEN, THEY STATED THAT THEY WERE VERY FRIGHTENED BY THE OBJECT AND HURRIEDLY BEGAN RETRIEVING THEIR FISHING GEAR AND RETURNING TO THEIR HOME PORT.

5. A DETAILED REPORT OF THIS INCIDENT WAS FORWARDED TO HEADQUARTERS, UNITED STATES AIR FORCE, WASHINGTON, D.C., FOR EVALUATION AND ANALYSIS.
