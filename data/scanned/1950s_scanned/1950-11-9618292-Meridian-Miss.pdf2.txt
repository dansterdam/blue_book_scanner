The CLARION-LEDGER, (Jackson, Mississippi), of 18 November 1950, carried the following account of a "flying saucer" report:
"POINTS OVER BROOKHAVEN SEEN FLYING OBJECT"
"Brookhaven - Several persons have reported to the Leader-Times of seeing another "flying saucer" moving across Brookhaven shortly after 6 p.m. Monday afternoon. Does not reveal if pilots or air crew."
Other persons speculated that it was merely a vapor trail made visible by the late afternoon skies or left by a trail of smoke from a passing plane.
Tom Lofton drove out to U.S. Highway 51 and watched the object for about 15 minutes. "Sample said he thought it was a blimp or an airplane."  
J. A. Smith reported seeing what he described as a "flying saucer" at about 5:15 p.m. Monday on North Jackson Street. He said several persons observed the strange object moving northwesterly direction. He said it left a slight pink glow.
The Vicksburg EVENING POST (Mississippi) carried the following account of a mystery object sighted in sky:
"MYSTERY OBJECT SIGHTED IN SKY"
Indianola, Miss.- Nov 14. An unidentified object was sighted over Mississippi at an altitude of about 55,000 feet according to Maj. D.D. Stephens, National Guard pilot who said he chased it for 35 miles.
