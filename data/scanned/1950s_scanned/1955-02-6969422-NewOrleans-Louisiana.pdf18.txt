SUBJECT: 24 MAR 55 OBSERVER REPORT
1. CAPT S.R. WHO IS A PILOT AT MOISANT INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT, NEW ORLEANS, LA., REPORTS THE FOLLOWING:
(A) ON THE NIGHT OF 24 MAR 55 AT APPROXIMATELY 1905 HOURS, CAPT S.R. AND SEVERAL MEMBERS OF THE AIRPLANE MAINTENANCE CREW OBSERVED A STRANGE LUMINOUS OBJECT IN THE SKY FROM THE AIRPORT.
(B) THE OBJECT WAS FIRST OBSERVED OVER LAKE PONTCHARTRAIN TRAVELING IN A SOUTHWESTERLY DIRECTION AT AN ESTIMATED ALTITUDE OF 1500 TO 2000 FEET.
(C) THE OBJECT APPEARED AS A BRIGHT LUMINOUS BODY APPROXIMATELY 5 TO 10 FEET IN DIAMETER AND GAVE OFF A REDDISH-ORANGE GLOW. NO SOUND WAS HEARD.
(D) THE OBJECT MAINTAINED A STEADY COURSE AND SPEED FOR APPROXIMATELY 5 TO 7 MINUTES AND THEN MADE A SHARP RIGHT TURN AND ACCELERATED RAPIDLY OUT OF SIGHT.
(E) DURING THE OBSERVATION, CAPT S.R. AND HIS CREW WERE ABLE TO OBSERVE THE OBJECT THROUGH BINOCULARS.
(F) CAPT S.R. STATED THAT SEVERAL OTHER PILOTS AND AIRPORT PERSONNEL ALSO OBSERVED THE OBJECT.
2. NO FURTHER INFORMATION IS AVAILABLE AT THIS TIME.
