UNITED STATES
REPORT NO. TR-35-52-67 LEAVES BLANK

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECTS (REPORT)

1. DATE OF OBSERVATION: 6 August 1952
2. TIME OF OBSERVATION: 2300 PST
3. PLACE OF OBSERVATION: Richmond, California
4. WITNESS: Janes V. Westbrook, ASST. MR. of Emba Esso, Richmond, Calif.
5. DURATION: Approx. 12 MIN.

DESCRIPTION OF OBJECTS:

a. No. of Objects: 4
b. Description: The objects appeared to be circular in shape and luminous. They were approximately 200-300 feet in the air and were moving in a north-easterly direction at a very rapid pace, approximately 700-900 mph. The objects were seen to suddenly stop, hover, and then accelerate again to a very high speed.
c. Color: Luminous, glowing white in color.
d. Sound: No sound was reported.

SUMMARY OF INFORMATION:

The witness reported the observation of four luminous objects which exhibited very rapid and erratic flight characteristics. The objects were seen to stop in mid-air, hover, and then accelerate again to a very high speed. No sound was observed. The objects were seen from approximately 2300 PST to 2312 PST. They were observed by the witness to be circular in shape and luminous in color. The objects were estimated to be at an altitude of approximately 200-300 feet and were moving in a north-easterly direction at an estimated speed of 700-900 mph.

COMMENTS OF REPORTING OFFICER:

It is the opinion of the reporting officer that the objects observed were definitely of an unconventional nature, and could not be identified as any known type of aircraft.

COMMENT OF COMMANDING OFFICER:
Concur with reporting officer.

OLIVER R. STONE
Captain, USAF
Asst. Dir. of Intelligence
