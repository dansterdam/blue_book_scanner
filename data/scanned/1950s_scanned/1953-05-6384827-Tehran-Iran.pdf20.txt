AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

Tehran, Iran
11-52-53

1. Le Colonel Dayton C. Gaste, 75th AD-42619, USAF, advisor to the IIAF, reports the sighting (in unidentified flying object) over Tehran on 11 May 1952 at 0020 local time. He observed a light near Tehran and called it to the attention of the control tower at Mehrabad Airport. Other observers quickly saw the light, and it increased in intensity and appeared to approach the city. Some saw it change shape, but when he arrived only one was visible.

2. The following information on relative to this sighting is submitted, in accordance with AFR 200-2:

Shape of object not determined, size comparable to P-47, aluminium in color. Three or four red lights on one side, apparently rotating, while a green light was visible on the other side, similar to coordination exercises, object disappeared from sight in approximately 2-3 minutes.

3. Time of sighting 1500 local time (2130Z)

4. Observation made from the ground only, no optical equipment used.

5. Observer was located at approximately 35°43'N, 51°27'E with object immediately overhead. Estimated altitude of object 20-25,000 feet, object was flying in a straight line.

6. Weather: Air weather prevailed on the day of the sighting, visibility was good, a full moon was present.

7. No meteorological activity or condition could offer an explanation. 
8. No interception action taken.
9. No tracks of the object have been found in the area at the time.

Inclosures: 1. World Aeronautical Chart, Mt. Kasruch, 1:253,000 shows the approximate location of the event.
2. Attached is the signed statement of Le Colonel Gaste and Mrs. Gaste, who are submitted, unedited.

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
DOD DIR 5200.10

WILLIAM K. SMITH
