TIFFIN, OHIO 17 November 1957 HOAX

A science teacher from Tiffin, Ohio observed a flash of light while driving home from school the following day he went to the area where he say the flash. He found an object six to eight feet in diameter on the ground. The teacher contacted the Toledo, Ohio, Police and the item was removed. This information from the Pentagon because of press inquiries regarding the incident.

On 1 December, a representative from Project Blue Book examined the witness and obtained the object. This object was a copper colored metallic sphere, approximately eight inches in diameter having a fitting or apparatus on one side. It was not damaged and held together by a bolt. Heat and acid applied to the radio to search and blacken the slot indicated to the contents of the sphere. Another officer found the object to be an American Standard thread bolt and the radio tubes were American made.

The incident is regarded as an attempt at a deliberate hoax.
