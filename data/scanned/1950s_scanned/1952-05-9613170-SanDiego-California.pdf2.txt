SUBJECT: Findings of Unconventional Airborne Objects, San Diego, California, March to June 1952.

TO: Director of Special Investigating
Headquarters United States Air Force
Washington 25, D.C.

1. SYNOPSIS: Source of information, an aero-nautical engineer, member of the "Flying Saucer Sighting Club" in the San Diego area, have formed an unidentified flying saucer sighting club. Other members were alerted for further identification, tracking, and triangulation. Source has submitted various sightings and photographic conditions concerning sightings made during four month period, March to June 1952.

2. DETAILS: A National City, California, employed as an engineer with the Consolidated-Vultee Aircraft Corporation, San Diego, California, adviser that he along with several other engineers has formed a "voluntary flying saucer sighting club." The members of this club live in and around San Diego and its suburbs. According to C-2 data, when one member sights a flying saucer, the others assemble and attempt to keep the object under observation as long as possible by triangulation for the purpose of identification. More detailed physical characteristics and additional sightings can be made.

The mathematical computations and reports submitted to this office reports along with various photographs taken 9 March 1952, 9 May 1952, 13 June 1952, and 29 June 1952, were evaluated. These reports concern night and daytime objects.

Included with the above are reports submitted by two of Mr. C's associates, Captain C. E. Taylor, USAF, California, and M. B.G., A Sergeant who reside 5915 Barnard St. and 632 Catalina Blvd., San Diego, California. These reports concern a green fire ball incident on 28 March and a similar sighting on 9 May 1952 by Mr. C at the University of California, San Diego campus.
