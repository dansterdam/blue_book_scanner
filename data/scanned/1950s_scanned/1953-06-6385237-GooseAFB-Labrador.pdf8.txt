UNCLASSIFIED
AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT
HEADQUARTERS, GOOSE AIR FORCE BASE, GOOSE BAY, LABRADOR
13 FEB 1953
SUBJECT: UNIDENTIFIED FLYING OBJECT
1. On 5 December 1952, at about 1230 hours, there was reported sighted in the vicinity of the Goose Air Force Base, Goose Bay, Labrador, an unidentified flying object. The object was described as being large, bright, metallic in appearance, and traveling at a high rate of speed.
2. The object was under observation for approximately five (5) minutes and was tracked on radar for about 2.5 minutes. This object traveled across the northern sky at an estimated altitude of 5,000 to 8,000 feet. It was moving from the northwest to the southeast at an estimated ground speed of 600 to 700 knots.
3. The object was observed by several persons on the ground and tracked on radar by the Ground Control Intercept Equipment at Goose Air Force Base. There were no friendly aircraft or missiles in the area at the time. The object disappeared from both visual and radar contact.

Fredlund Richard
Squadron Leader, RCAF
Commanding Officer, 57th Air Defence Squadron

DOWNGRADED AT 3 YEAR INTERVALS;
DECLASSIFIED AFTER 12 YEARS.
