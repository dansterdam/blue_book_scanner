AF FORM 112 - PART II  
APPROVED 1 JUNE 1949

AIR INTELLIGENCE INFORMATION REPORT

FROM: 5253RD ACWE (OP4), APO 902
TO: AC/AS-2, WASHINGTON, D.C.

SUBJECT: SIGHTING

ON 26 FEBRUARY 1953 AT APPROXIMATELY 2110 HOURS, VICTIMS FROM FOX CALLED IN A STRANGE OBJECT WHICH THEY CLAIMED TO HAVE OBSERVED AT APPROXIMATELY FIFTEEN (15) MILES WEST OF THIS STATION.

OFFICER, WHO IS ALSO A CIVILIAN, WAS OUTSIDE AND TOOK A LOOK AT IT. THE OBJECT APPEARED TO BE CIRCULAR IN SHAPE AND BE APPROXIMATELY 30 FEET IN DIAMETER. IT WAS OBSERVED FOR ABOUT THREE (3) MINUTES AND CHANGED COLOR FROM RED TO GREEN TO WHITE DURING THE OBSERVATION. IT APPEARED TO BE A STRANGE OBJECT WHICH LOOKED LIKE A FIRE BALL. IT APPEARED TO BE HOVERING OVER THE AREA FOR A FEW MINUTES AND THEN DISAPPEARED.

APPROXIMATELY 2100 HOURS, I SPOTTED A SAME LIGHT AT APPROXIMATELY TWENTY MILES SOUTHWEST OF THIS STATION. IT APPEARED TO BE MOVING EAST AND CLIMBING SLOWLY. IT WAS OBSERVED FOR APPROXIMATELY TEN (10) MINUTES. IT APPEARED TO BE LOOKING FOR SOMETHING. AS IT REACHED APPROXIMATELY TEN (10) MILES FROM OUR STATION, IT SUDDENLY MADE A DIVE AND DISAPPEARED VERY RAPIDLY. THIS OBJECT DID NOT APPEAR TO BE CONNECTED TO THE OBJECT OBSERVED EARLIER INCIDENT TO LT. PARKER AND WENT BACK INTO THE OPERATIONS BUILDING AT APPROXIMATELY 2110 HOURS.

OUR STATION ENLISTED AND OFFICER PERSONNEL WHO CALLED AND WENT DOWNRANGE OF THEIR STATION, CHECKED OUR WEAPON AND NOTICED LARGE BIRDS (HAWKS) FLYING OVERHEAD. ALL EFFORTS TO CONFIRM OR IDENTIFY THIS STATION. SIGHTINGS HAVE SO FAR FAILED.

HARRY KEIM
V/23 AF 13113323
RADAR OPERATION, DET. # 20
