SYNOPSIS
Mrs. [Redacted] Box #207, Gold Beach, Oregon, advised that her attention was called to this object while on a fishing party on the Rogue River near Gold Beach on 24 May 1949. She described it as being round in shape, silver in color, and about the size of a C-47 aircraft. It made no noise, left no exhaust trail, and made no maneuvers. When first called to her attention, the object appeared to be three or four miles away. It was coming from the east, but later turned to the southwest. The position of the observer is as follows: Lat. '42° 25' North Latitude and 124° 24' West Longitude. Personnel of the CAA Weather Station, Medford Airport, Medford, Oregon, advised that there are no reports available exclusively for Gold Beach evidencing however, charts for the coastal area indicate the coast was open and clear on 24 May 1949.
