PART II - REQUIREMENTS

General

1. Date of sighting:  Latter part of April 1946.

2. Time of sighting:  Day time.

3. Place sighted (Observer's position): In open, rocky, treeless terrain
area, 25 miles N89o, about six miles South Southwest Delta, Colorado on
a ranch.  Observers was on horseback.  Sport was South of Grand Mesa Mountain,
about 20 miles and about 20 miles Southeast of Uncompahgre National Forest.
Coordinates approximately 38o 40' 30" North, 103o 10' 30" West.

4. Number of objects: Three broken pieces of aluminum ballast ring from
Japanese incendiary balloon.

5. Observable celestial phenomena: None

6. Distance of object from observer: Examined by observer on the ground.
Objects had been there for some time.

7. Time in sight:  Objects not seen while airborne.

8. Appearance of object, aluminum trump alloy in the original form of a ring,
part of Japanese balloon used during World War II, outside periphery diam-
eter 24", certain form with 72 holes in the periphery containing explosive
plug inserts.

9. Direction of Flight:  None.  From general knowledge of past history, pre-
sumption of flight would be eastward.

10 thru 20: Unknown
