HEADQUARTERS:
AIR WEATHER SERVICE
Andrews Air Force Base
Washington 25, D. C.

11 May 1949

In Reply 
Refer To: ATSW DBS

SUBJECT: Unidentified Flying Objects

TO Commanding General
Air Material Command
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base
Dayton. Ohio
ATTN: TDEMX

1. Reference is made to letter from your headquarters, MCIAAX,
dated 9 March 1949, subject "Unidentified Flying Objects," and first
indorsement thereto by this headquarters, dated 31 March 1949.

2. The Incident Summaries 173 through 235 have been checked
against routine weather-balloon ascents made by the Air Force, Navy
and Weather Bureau. Comments based on this review are inclosed.

3. It is recommended that the "Guide to Investigation of Un-
identified Aerial Objects" be changed as follows: Item 12, "Direction
of Flight of Object," should be changed to show that the plan of
control centers be distinguished where possible, and that motion la
apparent motion in direction. Item 14 "Weather Balloon," under
"Relative to Radar Sighting," should include a statement of the type of
radar set used.

FOR THE CHIEF, AIR WEATHER SERVICE

/s/ W. A. West
W. A. WEST
Lt. Colonel, USAF
