HEADQUARTERS FIFTEENTH AIR FORCE, Colorado Springs, Colorado, 20 MAY 1949.

TO: Commanding General, Air Materiel Command, Wright-Patterson Air Force
Base, Dayton, Ohio. ATTN: MCIAIX

The incident referred to in basic has been investigated by the
Intelligence Division of this Headquarters and the following information
is furnished with numbers approximating the "Guide to Investigation,"
Unidentified Aerial Objects:

1. Date of Observation: 15 April 1949
   Date of Interview: 14 May 1949

2. Exact time of Observation: 1530 Mountain

3. Place of Observation: Peterson Air Field, Colorado Springs,
   Colorado,

4. Position of Observers: On ramp in front of operations building.

5. What attracted attention to object: One observer was watching
   an aircraft entering the traffic pattern and sighted the objects. He
   then called the attention of the other two observers to the objects.

6. Number of Objects: Two in formation.

7. Apparent Size: 6 to 8 inches in diameter. Observers stated
   that objects appeared somewhat smaller at the presumed altitude of 30,000
   feet than a B-29 would appear at the same altitude,

8. Color of Objects: Color of unlighted, frosted electric light
   bulb,

9. Shape: Objects seemed perfectly round.

10. Altitude: First observed at 30° above horizon.

11. Direction from Observers: When first seen - 160°; when last
   seen - 0°. Objects passed directly overhead.

12. Distance from Observer: When first seen appeared to be about
   20 miles away.
